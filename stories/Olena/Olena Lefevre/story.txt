Born Olena Lefevre, she had grown up in the sprawling metropolis of Moscow, where her family's modest bakery had been a staple for generations. But as she grew older, Olena found herself drawn to the arts, spending every spare moment sketching and painting in her small studio apartment.

Despite her talent, Olena's parents were never supportive of her career aspirations, insisting that she follow in their footsteps and take over the family business. But Olena's heart was not in it, and she eventually rebelled against their wishes, leaving Moscow to make a name for herself as an artist.

Now, years later, Olena Lefevre sat in the café, lost in thought as she pondered her next move. She had just received a rejection letter from a prestigious gallery, but she was not deterred. Instead, she felt a surge of determination and began to sketch out ideas for a new series of paintings.
