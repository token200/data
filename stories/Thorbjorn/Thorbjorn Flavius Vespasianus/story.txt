As Ragnar lay defeated at his feet, Thorbjorn felt a sense of pride and accomplishment wash over him. He had proven himself to be a true leader, worthy of the title that would one day be bestowed upon him: Thorbjorn Flavius Vespasianus, son of Harald Ironfist. The chieftain's blessing was enough to solidify his claim, and he became the new leader of their clan.

Under Thorbjorn's leadership, the tribe prospered, and he expanded their territories through a series of strategic victories. He was a just ruler, beloved by his people, and his name became synonymous with bravery and strength. And though he faced many challenges along the way, Thorbjorn remained steadfast, ever true to himself and his family's legacy: the indomitable spirit of Thorbjorn Flavius Vespasianus.

Years went by, and Thorbjorn grew old but never lost his vigor. He passed on his knowledge and skills to a new generation, ensuring that his name would live on forever.
