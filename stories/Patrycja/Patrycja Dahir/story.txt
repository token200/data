From a young age, she was drawn to the world of art, spending hours sketching and painting the cityscapes that inspired her.

As she grew older, Patrycja began to develop her own unique style. She experimented with different mediums, from oil paints to bronze, but it wasn't until she discovered glassblowing that she found her true medium. There was something about the way the molten glass could be shaped and molded that resonated with her.

Patrycja's sculptures quickly gained attention in Poland and beyond. Her use of color and light created an otherworldly effect, as if the pieces were alive. collectors and museums clamored for her work, but Patrycja remained true to her artistic vision.

Despite her success, Patrycja remained humble and dedicated to her craft. She spent hours in her studio, perfecting each piece and pushing the boundaries of what was possible with glass. Years passed, and Patrycja's reputation continued to grow. Her work was exhibited in galleries around the world, and she became known for her innovative use of materials. But through it all, she remained true to her roots, always drawing inspiration from the city that had nurtured her artistic talents.
