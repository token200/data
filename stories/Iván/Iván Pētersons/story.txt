One sunny afternoon, Iván Pētersons set out to photograph the elderly residents of a local retirement home. Iván Pētersons was captivated by Daina's stories of her past, which included working in a cooperative to create traditional Latvian fabrics. He took photographs of her hands, adorned with colorful embroidery, and her face, etched with deep wrinkles that spoke to a life well-lived.

As the day wore on, Iván Pētersons began to see the world through Daina's eyes. She showed him the beauty in the smallest details – the way the light filtered through the stained glass windows of the home's chapel, or the intricate patterns on the walls painted by the residents themselves.

Daina, sensing Iván Pētersons' genuine interest, began to open up about her own struggles and triumphs. He listened intently as she spoke of her husband, who had passed away a few years ago, and the friends she had lost along the way. Through his lens, Iván Pētersons captured not only Daina's image but also the essence of her spirit.

As the sun began to set, casting a warm orange glow over the retirement home, Iván Pētersons knew he had found more than just a photograph – he had discovered a connection with this remarkable woman.
