Selma Maria Mägi was an Estonian artist known for her bold and expressive paintings. Born in 1910 in Tartu, Estonia, she grew up in a family of modest means but rich cultural heritage. From a young age, Selma showed a keen interest in art, often spending hours sketching and painting the beautiful landscapes of her homeland.

As a young woman, Selma was encouraged by her parents to pursue her passion for art. She enrolled in the Tartu Art School, where she honed her skills under the guidance of some of Estonia's most renowned artists. After completing her studies, Selma went on to study painting at the Kunstakademi (Art Academy) in Tallinn.

Selma's artistic style was characterized by vibrant colors and dynamic brushstrokes, often depicting the rugged beauty of Estonian landscapes. Her paintings were not only aesthetically pleasing but also carried a deep sense of emotion and storytelling. Selma's works were heavily influenced by her love for nature and her desire to preserve Estonia's natural heritage.

Throughout her career, Selma was recognized for her talent with numerous awards and exhibitions. She became one of the most celebrated artists in Estonia, known for her unique style and unwavering dedication to her craft. Despite facing challenges and setbacks along the way, Selma remained committed to her art, always striving to push the boundaries of what was possible.

One of Selma's most famous works is "Estonian Landscape," a painting that showcases her mastery of color and composition. The work features a sweeping vista of Estonian countryside, with rolling hills and towering trees stretching as far as the eye can see. The painting is bathed in a warm golden light, capturing the essence of a summer's day.

Selma Maria Mägi passed away in 1975 at the age of 65, leaving behind a legacy of incredible artwork. Her paintings continue to be celebrated and admired by art lovers around the world, a testament to her enduring talent and creativity. To this day, Selma is remembered as one of Estonia's most beloved artists, her name synonymous with beauty and artistic expression.
