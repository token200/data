Selma Kwiatkowski was a name whispered in awe by those who knew her. A free-spirited artist with a wild mane of curly hair and a paint-stained apron, she had a way of bringing people together through her art.

As the sun set over the small town of Willow Creek, Selma's studio came alive with the sound of laughter and music. Her latest exhibition was underway, featuring vibrant portraits and landscapes that seemed to pulse with life. Guests milled about, sipping wine and discussing the intricate details of each piece.

Selma moved effortlessly between tables, chatting with strangers-turned-friends as they admired her work. She was a magnetic presence, drawing people in with her warmth and generosity of spirit. Despite her fame within local art circles, she remained humble and grounded, always willing to share her knowledge and expertise with others.

As Sophie gazed deeper into the painting, Selma noticed a look of recognition on her face. "What do you see?" Selma asked, curiosity getting the better of her.

Sophie hesitated for a moment before responding, "I see myself." It was a fleeting glance, but one that spoke volumes to Selma. She smiled knowingly, recognizing the same sense of freedom and creative expression in Sophie's eyes that she saw in her own mirror each morning.
