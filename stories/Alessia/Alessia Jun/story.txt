Alessia Jun stepped off the train and onto the bustling platform, her bright smile illuminating the crowded station. She spotted him standing by the exit, his dark hair mussed and his eyes crinkled at the corners as he smiled.

It had been months since Alessia Jun had last seen her brother, Leonardo. They had drifted apart after their parents' passing, each trying to find their own way in the world. But despite the distance, Alessia's thoughts had often turned to him, and she was thrilled to be reunited with him now.

Leonardo swept her into a tight hug, holding her close as they both breathed in the familiar scent of the city. "I've missed you so much, sis," he whispered, his voice rough with emotion.

Alessia Jun laughed, feeling a lump form in her throat. "I've missed you too, Leo," she replied, her eyes shining with tears.

"What's this?" Alessia asked, curiosity piqued.

"I wrote you a story," he said, his eyes sparkling with mischief. "It's about our family, growing up together. I hope you like it."

Alessia took the paper from him, her heart racing as she began to read. The words flowed effortlessly, weaving a tapestry of memories and emotions that felt both deeply personal and utterly familiar.
