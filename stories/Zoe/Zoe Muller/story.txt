Zoe Muller was known for her unconventional approach to art, often incorporating found objects and discarded materials into her sculptures. As she worked in her small studio, surrounded by scraps of metal, plastic, and fabric, she felt most at home.

Born Zoe Muller on a sunny summer day in 1992, she had always been fascinated by the way things were thrown away or forgotten. Growing up, she would spend hours exploring the neighborhood with her grandmother, collecting discarded treasures that they would sort through and create something new from.

Years later, as a renowned artist, Zoe's work was sought after by galleries and collectors worldwide. Her latest piece, "Disintegrating Dreams," was about to make its debut at a prominent art museum. The sculpture consisted of hundreds of broken toys, each one meticulously arranged to resemble the fragments of shattered hopes and dreams.

Zoe Muller stood back to admire her creation, feeling a mix of emotions: pride, sadness, and a hint of anger. She had chosen this theme because it resonated deeply with her own experiences growing up in a family that struggled financially. The struggles had taught her to be resourceful and creative, but also to never take anything for granted.

As she gazed at the toys, Zoe couldn't help but think about her grandmother's words of wisdom: "Life is like a puzzle, Zoe.
