Growing up in a family of modest means, Kārlis had always been fascinated by the old cars that his father would fix in their backyard. His father, also named Kārlis Dąbrowski, had taught him everything he knew about mechanics and problem-solving. After completing his studies in engineering, Kārlis set out to build a career as a restorer, determined to one day open his own shop.

Years passed, and Kārlis's skills improved dramatically. He began to receive commissions from collectors and enthusiasts, who were drawn to his attention to detail and ability to breathe new life into neglected vehicles. His reputation grew, and soon he was in high demand. Cars from all over Europe would arrive at his doorstep, ready for a transformation.

As he worked on the car, he shared stories of his own experiences restoring cars with Aiva, regaling her with tales of his father, who had taught him everything he knew.

When the restoration was complete, Aiva was overjoyed, and Kārlis Dąbrowski couldn't help but feel a sense of pride. He had not only saved an old car from obscurity but also shared a piece of his own history with someone who truly appreciated it.
