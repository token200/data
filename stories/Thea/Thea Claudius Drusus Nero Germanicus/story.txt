And though he had never married or had children, his name was still revered throughout the empire. Thea wondered what it would be like to follow in his footsteps, to make a name for herself as a leader and a force for good.

One day, while helping at the baths, Thea met a young man named Marcus. He was kind and gentle, with a quick wit and a sharp mind. As they talked, Thea found herself drawn to him, sensing that he shared her values and passions.

Marcus was taken aback by Thea's intensity and purpose, but he was also impressed by her intelligence and compassion. Together, they began to make plans for the future, discussing ways in which they could use their skills and talents to benefit the empire.

Thea knew that she would always be a member of the imperial family, no matter what path she chose. But with Marcus by her side, she felt a sense of freedom and possibility that she had never known before. And as they walked out of the baths together, Thea Claudius Drusus Nero Germanicus felt a spark of excitement for the adventures to come.
