His employer, the wealthy and influential senator Lucius, had tasked him with recovering a rare scroll that was said to hold secrets of ancient Japanese magic.

Shimizu navigated through the winding alleys and market stalls, dodging merchants and pedestrians along the way. The vendor, noticing his unusual clothing and dark hair, asked about his heritage, to which Shimizu replied with pride, "I am Shimizu Valerius Aurelius Constantinus, son of the great samurai clan from Japan."

"I am here for the scroll," Shimizu replied calmly, producing a small token that Lucius had given him in exchange for its safe return.

The curator hesitated before handing over the scroll, which Shimizu carefully wrapped and tucked into his cloak. As he exited the museum, he felt a sense of satisfaction wash over him. He was Shimizu Valerius Aurelius Constantinus, after all – a man of honor and duty who had proven himself once again.

Upon his return to Lucius's estate, Shimizu presented the scroll with a flourish, earning a hearty handshake from his employer. "Well done, Shimizu," Lucius exclaimed. "You have earned my gratitude and the trust of my family. You are indeed a true representative of your noble heritage – Shimizu Valerius Aurelius Constantinus."
