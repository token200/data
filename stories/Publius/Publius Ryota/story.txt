His razor-sharp wit and commanding presence had earned him both admirers and critics, but one thing was certain – Publius Ryota was a force to be reckoned with.

As he wove through the stalls, Publius Ryota's eyes scanned the crowds, searching for potential clients or patrons who could help further his career. Publius Ryota spotted his friend Marcus, a fellow orator who had also made a name for himself in Rome's intellectual circles.

The Red Griffin's patrons listened with rapt attention, their faces illuminated only by the flickering candles and the soft glow ofPublius Ryota's words.

In this dimly lit world of politics and rhetoric, Publius Ryota was a rising star, his name on everyone's lips. And as he stood up to take his leave, Marcus smiled and clapped him on the back. "You're going to make a great senator one day, Ryota," he said with a grin.
