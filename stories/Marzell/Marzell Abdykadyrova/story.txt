Born in a small town in Central Asia, Marzell grew up surrounded by the vast steppes and majestic mountains that would later inspire her photography. Her fascination with light and shadows was nurtured by her father, a skilled lantern maker who taught her how to harness the beauty of darkness.

After completing her degree in photography from Moscow State University, Marzell began her career as a freelance photographer, traveling extensively throughout Europe and Asia to capture the essence of urban landscapes. Her photographs were met with critical acclaim, with publications such as The New York Times and National Geographic featuring her work.

Marzell's style was characterized by its moody tone and ability to convey the emotional depths of her subjects. One of Marzell's most notable projects was "The City in Motion," a multimedia installation that explored the lives of street vendors and traders in bustling cities across Asia. The project was met with international recognition, earning her a prestigious award at the Venice Biennale.
