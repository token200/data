Now, as an adult, she found herself drawn back to the place of her birth, eager to reconnect with the people and traditions that shaped her identity.

The old stone buildings, the winding rivers, and the vibrant market square all seemed to hold secrets and stories waiting to be uncovered. She stopped at a small café on Main Street, ordering a cup of coffee and striking up a conversation with the owner, an elderly woman named Babcia.

Babcia's eyes lit up as Julia introduced herself as Julia Kamiński, daughter of her old friend, Janusz. There were aunts and uncles, cousins and grandparents – all gathered together to celebrate another year of life. Throughout the evening, Julia Kamiński listened intently as stories of her ancestors were shared, their laughter and tears mingling with the scent of pierogi and bigos.
