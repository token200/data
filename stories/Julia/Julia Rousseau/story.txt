Julia Adèle Rousseau was a free-spirited artist who had always been drawn to the vibrant streets of New Orleans. With her wild curly hair and paint-stained hands, she seemed to embody the very essence of the city's bohemian vibe.

According to family legend, Julia Adèle's ancestor had been a talented artist herself, known for capturing the city's essence on canvas.

Inspired by this rich history, Julia decided to create her own artwork that would pay homage to the city she loved. She set up her easel in Jackson Square, where she spent hours painting the vibrant scenes before her eyes. Her brushstrokes were bold and expressive, capturing the energy of the city with a sense of wonder and curiosity.

Julia was happy to share her passion for the city and its people, but she also made sure to keep her artistic process private, preserving the magic of her creative world.
