The task was daunting, but François was determined to unlock the secrets of the great mind. He had already made significant progress, and his translation was set to be published within the year.

The wise leader invited François to his chambers, where he revealed that he had taken notice of the young man's remarkable work. The emperor saw potential in François and proposed an opportunity: to serve as his personal advisor and translate key texts for the imperial court.

François was both honored and intimidated by the prospect. He accepted the offer and threw himself into his new role, pouring over ancient texts and advising the emperor on matters of state. As he worked, François found that his unique perspective – a blend of scholarly rigor and imperial insight – proved invaluable to the ruler.

Under François's guidance, the empire flourished. He translated texts on agriculture, medicine, and governance, helping to improve the lives of citizens throughout the empire. The emperor came to rely on François's counsel, valuing his opinion above that of other advisors.

As the years passed, François Flavius Domitianus Augustus became one of Rome's most respected figures. His name was whispered in awe by scholars and politicians alike, a testament to his intellectual prowess and unwavering dedication to his craft. Though he remained humble and unassuming, François knew that his work had left an indelible mark on the empire, cementing his place in history as one of its most remarkable minds.

In the evenings, as François sat at his desk, quill in hand, he would smile to himself, knowing that he had found his true calling. The Oracle had unlocked a door, and now the world was his to explore and shape.
