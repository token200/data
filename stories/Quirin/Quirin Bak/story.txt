The pay was substantial, but the risks were significant. Many experts deemed it impossible to tap into that kind of power without destabilizing the planet's magnetic field.

Undeterred, Quirin Bak threw himself into the project, pouring over theories and experimenting with novel materials. His workshop became a hive of activity as he assembled a team of talented engineers and scientists to aid him in his quest.

As the months passed, Quirin Bak's obsession with Erebus only intensified. He spent countless hours in the lab, tweaking designs and testing prototypes. His colleagues began to worry that he was sacrificing too much sleep and social interaction for the sake of progress.

Quirin Bak had created a device capable of siphoning off a fraction of the Earth's core energy, without disrupting the planet's delicate balance. The implications were staggering – limitless clean power, no longer dependent on fossil fuels or renewable sources.

The news of Quirin Bak's breakthrough spread like wildfire, captivating the attention of governments and corporations worldwide. As he sat in his cluttered workshop, surrounded by scattered notes and half-assembled prototypes, Quirin Bak couldn't help but feel a sense of pride and accomplishment. He had done it – not just with Erebus, but with his own unique brand of genius.
