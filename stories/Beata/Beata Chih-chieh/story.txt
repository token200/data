As she grew older, Beata honed her skills under the tutelage of the wise and venerable wizard, Eryndor Thorne. Beata's natural leadership abilities soon caught the attention of the kingdom's ruler, King Arin III. He saw great potential in the young warrior and offered her a place in his personal guard. Beata accepted the honor, and soon found herself fighting alongside the king's most trusted soldiers against invading forces from neighboring lands.

Despite the risks, Beata thrived in her new role, earning the respect and admiration of her comrades. Her full name, Beata Chih-chieh, became synonymous with bravery and cunning. When the kingdom was threatened by a dark sorceress named Lyra, Beata was chosen to lead the royal guard against her.

The final battle was intense, with both sides suffering heavy losses. However, Beata's unyielding determination and mastery of fire magic ultimately proved decisive. With a fierce cry, she charged forward, unleashing a torrent of flames that drove Lyra back and shattered her dark crystal staff. The sorceress fled in defeat, and the kingdom was saved once more.

In the aftermath of the battle, King Arin III hailed Beata Chih-chieh as a hero, praising her selflessness and unwavering commitment to justice. As she stood before the king, her eyes shone with a quiet pride, knowing that she had truly found her calling as a protector of the realm.
