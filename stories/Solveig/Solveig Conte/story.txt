As Solveig grew older, her fascination with music became increasingly evident. She would spend hours practicing the piano, pouring her heart and soul into each piece she played. In fact, Solveig's love for Chopin was so profound that she had dedicated an entire room in her family's studio to his works.

Despite her family's legacy as artists, Solveig felt an overwhelming urge to break free from the mold and forge her own path.
