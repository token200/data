Danuta Michielin was a Polish-American jazz singer and pianist who left an indelible mark on the music scene of the 1960s. Born in 1924, she began her career as a cabaret singer in Warsaw before emigrating to the United States.

Her voice was both emotive and powerful, with a hint of Eastern European flair that captivated audiences worldwide. She performed at some of the city's most renowned clubs and venues, rubbing shoulders with legendary musicians like Billie Holiday and Frank Sinatra.

Danuta's repertoire expanded beyond traditional Polish folk music to include American jazz classics and show tunes. Her rendition of George Gershwin's "Summertime" is still widely regarded as one of her greatest performances. The song's melancholic quality resonated deeply with Danuta, whose own life was marked by hardship and perseverance.
