Toms Wei, also known as Lee Wei, was a talented young artist living in Taipei, Taiwan. Growing up in a family of modest means, he had always found solace in his art, spending hours sketching and painting in his small bedroom.

As a child, Toms would watch old movies with his grandmother, mesmerized by the brushstrokes of Chinese watercolor artists on screen. He was particularly fascinated by the way they could capture the softness of skin and the depth of emotion on their subjects' faces. This fascination sparked his own passion for painting, which only grew stronger as he entered adulthood.

Toms Wei's art was not just a hobby, but a reflection of his soul. His vibrant colors and bold strokes seemed to pulse with an inner energy, as if the paintings themselves were alive. People who saw his work couldn't help but feel a sense of wonder and awe at the sheer emotion that poured out of every brushstroke.
