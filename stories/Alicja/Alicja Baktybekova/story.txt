Alicja was a young Kazakhstani woman with a passion for photography and storytelling. She had always been drawn to the beauty of her homeland's diverse landscapes and cultures, and had honed her skills as a photographer while traveling extensively throughout Central Asia.

As she approached the seafront, Alicja spotted a group of local children playing on the beach, their laughter carrying on the wind. She smiled, remembering the countless hours she had spent as a child playing on these very same shores with her own siblings.

Alicja began to snap photos of the children at play, capturing their joy and energy as they chased each other in the waves. As she worked, she noticed an elderly woman sitting alone on a bench, watching the children with a warm smile.

Intrigued, Alicja approached the woman and introduced herself. To her surprise, the woman spoke little English but began to share stories of her own childhood, spent playing on these same beaches as a young girl.

Over the next few weeks, Alicja returned to visit the elderly woman, who came to be known as "Nana" to her. As they spent more time together, Nana shared with Alicja the history and traditions of her community, from the ancient Silk Road traders to the modern-day fishing villages that lined the coast.

Alicja was captivated by Nana's stories and began to see her homeland in a new light. She started to incorporate elements of Nana's heritage into her photography, capturing the vibrant colors and textures of the local markets, mosques, and traditional clothing.

As Alicja continued to work on her project, she began to notice the ways in which Nana's stories were intertwined with her own experiences as a Kazakhstani woman living abroad.
