Amélie's days were filled with small joys: watching the sunlight dance through the leaves of the ancient trees, listening to the gentle lapping of the river against its banks, and sipping coffee at the local café.

One morning, while strolling through the market, Amélie spotted a young girl sitting alone at a table, her eyes fixed on a small, worn-out book. Something about the girl's demeanor resonated with Amélie, so she approached her and asked if she could join. The girl, whose name was Sophie, told Amélie that this was her favorite book – a collection of short stories by Gustave Flaubert.

Amélie and Sophie spent hours talking, lost in conversations about literature, life, and everything in between. As the sun began to set, casting a warm glow over the town, Amélie realized she had found not only a kindred spirit but also someone who needed a friend.

Over the next few weeks, Amélie and Sophie grew closer, exploring the town together, sharing secrets, and laughing together. Amélie introduced Sophie to her favorite haunts: the hidden gardens, the bakery where they shared flaky croissants and coffee, and the riverbank where they sat watching the stars.

As their friendship blossomed, Amélie found herself seeing the world through Sophie's eyes – with a sense of wonder that she hadn't experienced in years. And Sophie, in turn, felt seen and understood by Amélie, who knew exactly how to listen without judging.

One day, as they sat together at the café, sipping coffee and reading Gustave Flaubert's stories aloud to each other, Amélie smiled, knowing that she had found a true friend in Sophie. "Merci pour être là," she said, her eyes shining with gratitude.
