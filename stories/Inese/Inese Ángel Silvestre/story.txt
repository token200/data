Her parents, both artists themselves, encouraged her creative side, teaching her how to paint and draw the vivid landscapes that inspired her.

As Inese grew older, her passion for art only intensified, leading her to study fine arts in college. It was there that she discovered a newfound love for photography, capturing the raw beauty of the world around her through the lens of a camera. Her black-and-white portraits were particularly striking, imbuing her subjects with a sense of timelessness and gravitas.

After graduation, Inese set out on her own, traveling to remote corners of the globe in search of new inspiration. Wherever she went, her camera was always by her side, capturing the stories and secrets of the people she met.

One of Inese's most remarkable photographs, taken during a solo expedition to the Amazon rainforest, would become one of her claim to fame. The haunting image, which showcased a majestic jaguar perched atop a tree branch, earned her widespread recognition and catapulted her to the forefront of the art world.

Inese Ángel Silvestre had truly found her calling – using her camera as a tool to tell stories that needed to be told, and to capture the beauty of the world in all its complexity.
