Finn's curiosity was piqued, and they pushed open the door to reveal a dimly lit interior filled with patrons who seemed to be from all corners of the globe. The air was thick with smoke and the clinking of glasses, and Finn felt an instant sense of belonging as they made their way to the bar.

She flashed Finn a warm smile, and they felt an inexplicable jolt of recognition.

"Welcome to The Red Griffin," she said, her voice low and husky as she slid a small note across the counter towards Finn. "I've been expecting you, Finnley 'Finn' Krumins."

As the night wore on, Finn found themselves drawn into a world of secrets and ancient magic, with this enigmatic woman at its center. And though they still kept their true nature hidden beneath the surface, they couldn't help but feel a sense of belonging in this place – among these strangers who seemed to hold so much mystery and intrigue.

For Finn Krumins, the night had only just begun, and the adventures that lay ahead were sure to be anything but ordinary.
