Her work earned her international recognition and led to her appointment as director of SEI.

Under Leni's leadership, SEI transformed into a hub for intergalactic research and innovation. She assembled a team of experts from all over the world to tackle some of humanity's most pressing questions about the cosmos. Their mission was bold: to send the first human mission to Mars within a decade.

One of Leni's most ambitious projects was the development of a revolutionary new propulsion system called "Aurora." This cutting-edge technology promised to reduce travel time between Earth and Mars from months to weeks, making interplanetary missions more feasible. The team worked tirelessly, pouring over complex equations and conducting rigorous simulations to bring Aurora to life.

Leni's dedication to her work inspired countless young minds around the world.
