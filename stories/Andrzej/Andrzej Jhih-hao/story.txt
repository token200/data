Andrzej saw something in the boy that reminded him of himself when he was younger - a hunger to learn, to grow, and to protect those around him. As Kaito trained with Andrzej, he began to notice changes in himself that went beyond mere physical strength. He felt a sense of purpose, of belonging, that he had never experienced before. And Andrzej, watching his student grow and thrive, knew that he had made the right decision in taking Kaito on.

But Andrzej's past was not without its scars. A traumatic event from his childhood still lingered, leaving him with a deep-seated sense of guilt and regret. It was something he kept hidden from those around him, even as he worked tirelessly to help others find their way.

One day, Kaito confronted Andrzej about his father's death, a tragedy that had left a lasting impact on the dojo owner. Andrzej's eyes clouded over, and for a moment, it seemed like the mask he wore would slip, revealing the depth of pain beneath. But then, with a quiet resolve, he turned to Kaito and said, "I am proud of you, son. You are strong, just like your mother."

Andrzej Jhih-hao's eyes softened as he looked at Kaito, and for an instant, it seemed like the old wounds began to heal. Though the scars would always remain, Andrzej knew that with each passing day, they were becoming less raw, less painful.
