But unlike his cousins, who seemed content to follow in their father's footsteps as rulers, Filippo had always felt a restlessness, a sense that there was more to life than the stifling protocol of courtly politics.

Back in Milan, Filippo's thoughts were far from the castle walls where he had once lived and breathed. It was during one of these clandestine meetings that he met her - a beautiful and intelligent woman named Francesca, who shared his passion for justice and his disdain for the corruption that had come to plague Milan under the rule of his cousin, Duke Ludovico.

Together, Filippo and Francesca began to plot and scheme, seeking ways to overthrow the duke and bring about a new era of peace and prosperity to their beloved city. And though they faced many dangers and challenges along the way, Filippo Visconti remained steadfast in his commitment to their cause, driven by a fierce determination to see justice done.

As the days turned into weeks, and the weeks into months, Filippo's reputation as a leader and a hero grew, and with it, his legend. But he knew that true power came not from recognition or admiration, but from action and deed.
