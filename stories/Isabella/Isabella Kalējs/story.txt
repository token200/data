As she wandered through the stalls, Isabella Kalējs's fingers trailing over the spines of stuffed birds and squirrels, she felt a sense of kinship with these often-overlooked beings.

Isabella Kalējs's heart skipped a beat as she accepted the gift, running her thumb over the intricate carvings.

With the vendor's words still echoing in her mind, Isabella Kalējs continued her exploration of Riga's hidden corners. She stumbled upon an abandoned church, its stone walls covered in ivy and moss, where she found inspiration for a new piece – a taxidermy installation that blended the sacred with the mundane.

As the sun dipped below the rooftops, casting long shadows across the city, Isabella Kalējs knew she had found her calling. She would weave tales of life and death, of beauty and decay, through the intricate threads of art and taxidermy.
