Rumors swirled about his origins – some said he was born on the streets of Taipei, while others claimed he came from a life of privilege and had turned to thievery for thrills. But one thing was certain: Sun Jhih-hao was a force to be reckoned with.

With a plan honed over months of research and preparation, he snuck into the museum through a ventilation shaft and made his way to the gallery housing the priceless Jadeite Cabbage.

As he lifted the vase off its pedestal, the security cameras captured only a fleeting glimpse of him – just long enough for him to leave behind a subtle signature on the display case. As the manhunt continued, whispers began to spread about Sun Jhih-hao's true intentions. Some said he was using his exploits to fund social causes and bring attention to marginalized communities. Others claimed he was simply addicted to the rush of adrenaline that came with each daring caper.

One thing was certain: Sun Jhih-hao had cemented his place in history as one of the greatest thieves of all time, leaving behind a legacy that would be studied for generations to come.
