As tensions between the two men escalated, it seemed as though Hadrianus's position would be threatened.

But Sun Aelius Hadrianus was not one to be underestimated. He outwitted Commodus at every turn, using his cunning and diplomacy to maintain his power and influence. In the end, he emerged victorious, solidifying his position as a dominant force in Roman politics.
