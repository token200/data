As she delved deeper into the text, Léa became obsessed with understanding Diocletianus' unique approach to mathematics. She spent every spare moment studying and practicing the techniques he had described. Her dedication paid off when she began to develop her own innovative solutions to complex mathematical problems.

Word of Léa's genius spread quickly through the academic circles, and soon she was invited to present her findings at a prestigious symposium in Rome. As she stood before the esteemed gathering of mathematicians and philosophers, Léa felt a sense of pride and accomplishment wash over her. She had proven that even in a world dominated by men, a woman could excel in the male-dominated field of mathematics.
