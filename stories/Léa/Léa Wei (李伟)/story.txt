Léa Wei, or Lee Wei as she was known to her friends and family back in Paris, had always been drawn to the vibrant city of Hong Kong. As a young artist, she had spent countless hours exploring the streets of Kowloon, marveling at the neon lights and bustling energy that seemed to pulse through every brick.

After completing her studies at the École des Beaux-Arts, Léa decided to take the leap and move back to Hong Kong, where she had grown up as a child. She settled into a small studio in Mong Kok, surrounded by fellow artists and like-minded individuals who shared her passion for creativity.

As she began to establish herself as an artist, Léa found herself drawn to the intersection of traditional Chinese culture and modern street art. She spent hours researching the history of Hong Kong's graffiti scene, learning about the pioneers who had paved the way for future generations.
