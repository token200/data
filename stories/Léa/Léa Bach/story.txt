Léa Bach was a French singer-songwriter known for her eclectic and introspective style. Born on October 12, 1986, in Nantes, France, Léa began writing songs as a teenager, inspired by the folk and indie-rock scenes of her hometown.

After releasing her debut album in 2009, Léa Bach's music gained a small but dedicated following in France. Her introspective lyrics and soothing melodies resonated with fans, who appreciated her unique voice and perspective. As she continued to write and record music, Léa developed a distinctive sound that blended elements of folk, pop, and electronic genres.

Léa's second album, released in 2012, was met with critical acclaim and commercial success. The album featured hit singles like "Hymne" and "Ondine," which showcased Léa's ability to craft memorable hooks and atmospheric soundscapes.

Over the years, Léa Bach has collaborated with various artists and producers, including Bon Iver and Radiohead's Thom Yorke. Her work has been praised for its emotional depth and sonic experimentation. Despite her growing success, Léa remains committed to creating music that is authentic and meaningful to her.
