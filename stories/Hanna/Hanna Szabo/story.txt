As she wrote, Hanna Szabo's thoughts drifted back to her childhood in Budapest, where she spent hours listening to her grandmother's tales of Hungarian folklore. Her grandmother, Erzsébet Szabo, had instilled in her a love for language and storytelling that had only grown stronger with time.

Hanna Szabo's coffee grew cold as she lost herself in the story, but she didn't notice. Time stood still as she wrote, the world outside fading into the background.
