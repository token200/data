As an artist, Mattia was never satisfied with the world around him. The city streets were too bright, too sterile, too devoid of emotion. He needed to capture the imperfections, the grittiness, the raw energy that pulsed through every street corner and alleyway.

His latest obsession was a young couple he'd seen on one of his late-night walks. They'd been laughing, holding hands, their eyes shining with happiness as they walked hand in hand along the riverbank. Mattia had tried to capture that feeling on canvas, but it seemed like an impossible task.

He dipped his brush into a vibrant shade of blue and began to block out the shapes of the couple's faces, trying to recapture the essence of their joy. But every time he added more detail, it felt like he was losing the magic. He wanted it to be raw, unbridled, like the night air itself.

As he worked, Mattia's thoughts wandered back to his childhood, growing up in a world that seemed too perfect, too controlled. His parents had been artists themselves, but they'd never let him express himself freely, pushing him towards "practical" careers instead. The pain of those years still lingered, making every brushstroke feel like a betrayal.

The hours blurred together as Mattia lost himself in the colors and textures of his canvas. Sweat dripped from his brow, mixing with the paint on his fingers, but he didn't notice. He was too far gone, chasing that elusive spark of life and emotion.
