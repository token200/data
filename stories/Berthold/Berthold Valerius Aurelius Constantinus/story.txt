As he grew older, Berthold's desire for recognition only intensified. He began to attend lavish parties and gatherings, where he would effortlessly charm his way into conversations with influential people. Berthold saw this as his chance to shine. He spent months training, honing his skills in martial arts, sword fighting, and horsemanship. His hard work paid off, and he emerged victorious from the tournament. The lord himself presented Berthold with the grant of land and the title of Lord of the Manor.

As news of his triumph spread, Berthold's fame grew exponentially. People from all over would come to see the young lord in action, to bask in his aura of confidence and power. Berthold Valerius Aurelius Constantinus had finally achieved his goal: he was a man of stature, respected by all.

However, with great power comes great responsibility, and Berthold soon found himself facing challenges he had never anticipated. He must navigate the complexities of lordship, balancing the needs of his subjects with his own ambitions. Though unsure if he was truly ready for this level of responsibility, Berthold stood tall, determined to prove himself worthy of his title.

As the years passed, Berthold continued to grow in stature and wisdom. His name became synonymous with bravery and leadership. And though the weight of his responsibilities often threatened to overwhelm him, he remained steadfast, ever true to his vision of greatness for himself and those around him.
