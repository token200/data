One day, a young apprentice named Jānis stumbled upon Berthold's workshop while searching for work. The old man took pity on the eager youth and offered him an apprenticeship. As Jānis learned the art of woodworking under Berthold Eglīte's guidance, he began to uncover the secrets behind his master's remarkable creations.

Berthold Eglīte was known for his attention to detail and his ability to infuse each piece with a sense of soul. He would spend hours selecting the finest woods, carefully sanding down the grain to reveal the hidden patterns within. Jānis watched in awe as Berthold Eglīte's hands moved deftly, shaping the wood into beautiful forms that seemed to come alive in his hands.

As the months passed, Jānis became more skilled and confident in his own craft. He began to assist Berthold Eglīte on larger projects, learning how to combine different woods and techniques to create truly breathtaking pieces. Berthold Eglīte took pride in his young apprentice's progress, but he also knew that the true secret to his success lay not in the tools or materials, but in the way he saw the world.

One evening, as Jānis was leaving the workshop, Berthold Eglīte called out to him.
