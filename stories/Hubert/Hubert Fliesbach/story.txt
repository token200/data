As they talked, Hubert Fliesbach felt a sense of connection he'd never experienced before. It was as if he'd found someone who truly understood him, someone who saw beyond his eccentricities to the heart of his being. For the first time in years, he felt like he could be himself, without apology or pretension.

Over the next few weeks, Emma returned often to visit Hubert Fliesbach. They'd stroll through the city together, discussing art and literature and philosophy. Hubert found himself growing more confident, more at ease in his own skin. For a man who had always felt like an outsider, being with Emma made him feel like he belonged.
