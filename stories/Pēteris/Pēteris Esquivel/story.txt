Pēteris Esquivel was known for his unique blend of traditional Lithuanian folk music and modern electronic beats. As a child, he spent countless hours listening to the sounds of his grandfather's accordion playing, which sparked a passion within him that would one day become Pēteris Esquivel.

He would often sneak into the local taverns to watch traditional folk dances and listen to musicians play lively tunes on their instruments. These experiences greatly influenced his music style, which blended traditional instruments like the kanklės and balys with modern electronic production techniques.

His debut album, "Lithuanian Nights," was met with critical acclaim, and he quickly gained a following among fans of both traditional folk music and electronic dance music.
