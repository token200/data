But Detlef's success came at a cost. His relationships suffered, and his parents struggled to balance their own lives with their son's demanding training schedule. Detlef's body began to pay the price for its constant exertion, suffering from chronic injuries that kept him off the ice for months at a time.

Despite the challenges, Detlef continued to push himself, driven by an insatiable desire to be the best. He trained harder than ever before, pouring every ounce of energy into his craft. And it paid off – Detlef won back-to-back Olympic gold medals in 2010 and 2014, cementing his status as one of the greatest speed skaters of all time.

As the years went by, Detlef's body began to decline. His speed slowed, and his injuries became more frequent. He realized that he could no longer compete at the highest level, and with a heavy heart, he announced his retirement from speed skating.
