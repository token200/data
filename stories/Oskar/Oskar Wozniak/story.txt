Oskar Wozniak walked into the dimly lit café, sipping on his coffee as he scanned the room for familiar faces. The entrepreneur had been in the tech industry for years, building a reputation as one of its most innovative minds. Her friends and colleagues affectionately called her Ollie, but she preferred to be addressed by her full name, Oskar Wozniak.

Her parents, both engineers themselves, encouraged her curiosity, helping her build her first computer at the tender age of 12.

After college, Oskar landed an internship at a prestigious tech firm, where she quickly made a name for herself as a brilliant designer and developer. She spent years working on high-profile projects, collaborating with top talent in the industry. Her breakthrough came when she co-founded her own startup, which eventually led to her being acquired by a major corporation.
