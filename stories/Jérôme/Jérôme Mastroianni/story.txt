Born in Paris to a family of modest means, Jérôme's early life was marked by hardship and struggle. However, from a young age, he showed a natural talent for art that far surpassed his station in life. He spent hours sketching and painting, often sneaking into the Louvre at night to study the works of the masters.

As he grew older, Jérôme's passion for art only intensified. He enrolled in the École des Beaux-Arts, where he honed his skills and developed a unique style that blended traditional techniques with bold, modern colors.

After completing his studies, Jérôme set out to make a name for himself in the art world.
