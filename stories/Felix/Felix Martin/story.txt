Felix Martin sat at his desk, staring blankly at the stack of bills and overdue notices in front of him. As a freelance writer, he had always prided himself on being self-sufficient, but lately, it seemed like the world was conspiring against him.

He let out a deep sigh and rubbed his temples, feeling the familiar tension build in his head. It had been months since he'd landed a decent gig, and the uncertainty was starting to take its toll. His rent was due soon, and he couldn't afford to miss it.

Felix smiled weakly as Rachel set the plate down on his desk and sat down beside him. They chatted for a bit about everything and nothing, but Felix couldn't shake the feeling of frustration that had been building up inside him.

"What's really going on?" Rachel asked, noticing the look in his eye. "You can tell me."

Felix sighed and leaned back in his chair, running his hands through his tangled hair. "It's just...I don't know if I'm doing this writing thing right anymore," he admitted, feeling a lump form in his throat.

Rachel put a reassuring hand on his arm. "You're talented, Felix. You'll get another gig soon. In the meantime, let me ask you something: what do you really want to be doing?"

With newfound determination, Felix stood up from his chair and began pacing back and forth across the room. "You know what? I think you're right," he said, feeling a sense of purpose wash over him. "I'm going to start writing that novel now."
