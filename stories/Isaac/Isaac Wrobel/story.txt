As a child, he would spend hours exploring the yards, dreaming of one day owning his own restoration shop.

And they weren't wrong – Isaac could spot a rare 1969 Mustang from a mile away.

The paint was chipped, the seats were torn, and the engine seemed to be held together with twine and hope. But Isaac saw something special – a 1967 model, with the iconic full-size grille and original sunroof.

He bought the Bronco on a whim, knowing that it would take countless hours of work to restore it back to its former glory. And so he set to work, pouring over manuals, scouring for parts, and meticulously reupholstering the seats by hand. Isaac Wrobel's shop became a place where time stood still – old car enthusiasts gathered around the workbenches, swapping stories and sharing advice.

As the Bronco began to take shape, so did Isaac's reputation as a master restorer. People came from all over to see his work, and soon he was fielding offers for commissions and restoration services that would make even the most seasoned car enthusiast green with envy. And through it all, Isaac Wrobel remained humble, driven by a love of old cars and a passion for bringing them back to life.
