Reece Salamanca sat alone in the dimly lit cantina, nursing a whiskey on the rocks. The neon lights of the city outside cast an eerie glow over the cramped space, but Reece was oblivious to it all. They were lost in thought, their mind wandering back to the events that had brought them to this point.

As they sat there, Reece's thoughts drifted to their full name: Reece Salamanca, a name that held weight and significance for those who knew them. Growing up, their parents had always told them that their name was a blessing, one that came with great responsibility. And now, as they navigated the treacherous world of organized crime, Reece couldn't help but wonder if it was all worth it.

Reece's eyes scanned the room, taking in the shady characters that frequented this place. The patrons were a rough bunch, each with their own secrets and motivations. Some were here to make deals, while others came seeking refuge from the law. But Reece knew better than to trust anyone too quickly. They had learned that lesson the hard way.

Just then, a figure emerged from the shadows, catching Reece's eye. It was an old acquaintance, one who had been instrumental in helping them get where they were today. The man approached Reece with a sly smile, his eyes crinkling at the corners as he took in their appearance.

"Reece Salamanca," he said, his voice dripping with familiarity. "I see you're still running from your past."

Reece raised an eyebrow, intrigued despite themselves. What did this stranger want? And how much did they know about Reece's true identity?

"I'm not sure what you're talking about," Reece replied, trying to sound nonchalant.

But the man just chuckled, his eyes glinting with amusement. "Don't play dumb, Reece Salamanca. I've seen too many of your games. You're hiding something, and I aim to find out what it is."
