Farah Dupont was a name whispered in awe by those who knew her. A free-spirited artist, she lived life on her own terms, never afraid to take risks or challenge the status quo. Her paint-splattered hair and eclectic style made her stand out in a crowd, but it was her art that truly set her soul ablaze.

Born to a French mother and an Indian father, Farah's roots ran deep with both cultures. Growing up, she was surrounded by vibrant colors and exotic scents that fueled her creativity. She spent hours watching her grandmother weave intricate patterns on her loom, the rhythmic motion teaching her patience and attention to detail. As she grew older, Farah began to channel this energy into her art.

Her canvases pulsed with life, as if the very essence of India's rich heritage had been distilled onto them. Rich hues of crimson and saffron mingled with softer shades of rose and blue, evoking feelings of ancient rituals and mystical connections. Her subjects often shifted between mythical creatures and everyday women, their faces both familiar and mysterious.
