Aivars Errani was a master thief known for his unparalleled skill and cunning. Born with an uncanny ability to pick locks and slip in and out of the shadows unnoticed, Aivars had honed his craft over years of experience on the streets of the city.

As a young man, Aivars had been drawn into the world of thievery by his older brother, who had taught him everything he knew. But as Aivars grew older, he began to forge his own path, taking on high-stakes jobs and earning a reputation as one of the best in the business.

One night, Aivars Errani received a message from an unknown client with a proposal that seemed too good to be true: a priceless artifact would be left unattended at a charity gala, and all Aivars had to do was get in, grab it, and get out. The pay was staggering, and Aivars couldn't resist the opportunity.

But just as he reached for the artifact, Aivars' eyes met those of a familiar face: his brother, now turned against him by their long-standing rivalry. The two engaged in a fierce battle, with Aivars using every trick in the book to try and gain the upper hand. In the end, it was Aivars Errani who emerged victorious, but not without a few scars.

As he made his escape, Aivars couldn't help but feel a twinge of guilt for having turned against his brother. Despite their differences, they had always been close, and this betrayal would never be easily forgotten. But Aivars pushed the thought aside, focusing instead on the payday ahead.

With the artifact safely in hand, Aivars made his way back to his hideout, a small room above a pawnshop in the city's seedy underbelly.
