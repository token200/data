After a successful amateur career, Aivars turned professional at 18 and quickly rose through the ranks, earning his first ATP title in 2012. He became known for his exceptional volley skills and aggressive playing style, often taking the initiative on the court and pushing opponents off balance with his relentless assault of serves.

Over the years, Aivars earned multiple top-20 finishes at Grand Slam tournaments, including a career-high ranking of number eight. His most memorable victory came in 2018 when he beat former world number one Stan Wawrinka in the fourth round of the Australian Open. The 3-6, 7-5, 6-4 upset marked a turning point in Aivars' career and earned him international recognition.

Despite facing setbacks, including injuries that limited his playing time for two years, Aivars remained resolute in his pursuit of success. He credited his journey to family support from his parents and siblings and his own strong work ethic for staying motivated.

Aivars also became known for his humble nature and respect for his peers. He often went out of his way to offer words of encouragement and advice to young players, drawing inspiration from Navratilova's generosity towards him during their early encounters on the court.

At 35, Aivars' time in the spotlight began to wane as newer talent emerged and injuries continued to plague him. However, even in retirement, his legacy lived on through a new generation of tennis stars who benefited from his trailblazing spirit and unwavering perseverance.

As for Aivars himself, he now spends his days coaching junior players at home and reminiscing about the thrill of competing against some of the greatest opponents in the game.
