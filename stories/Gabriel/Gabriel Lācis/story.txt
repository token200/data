Gabriel didn't know which one was true, but he knew he had to find out. He was driven by a burning need to uncover the truth about Arianna, no matter what it cost him.

As he navigated the winding alleys of the city, Gabriel's eyes scanned the crowds for any sign of his quarry. The air was thick with the scent of incense and fresh bread, but beneath it all, he detected the faint tang of magic.

Suddenly, a figure darted around a corner, disappearing into the shadows. Gabriel gave chase, his long legs carrying him swiftly through the narrow streets. He ducked beneath market stalls and burst through doors, following the sorceress's scent like a bloodhound on the hunt.

And then, in a flash of insight, he understood. This was no simple sorceress; this was a conduit for an ancient and powerful force, one that threatened to consume them all. Arianna's magic was not benign; it was wild and primal, like a tempest raging across the land.

Gabriel knew his decision. He would not try to stop her, nor would he attempt to harness her power for himself. Instead, he would help her master it – if she would allow him to. Together, they could wield this force, bending its raw energy toward the greater good.
