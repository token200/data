Florence María was a free-spirited artist who lived life on her own terms. Born Florence María to a family of modest means in a small coastal town, she had grown up surrounded by the salty sea air and vibrant colors of the ocean.

As a child, Florence María would spend hours sketching the waves and seagulls that frequented the shore, her pencil moving deftly across the paper as she tried to capture their essence. Her parents, though initially skeptical of their daughter's artistic pursuits, soon came to see the passion and dedication that drove Florence María to create.

As she grew older, Florence María's talents became increasingly evident.
