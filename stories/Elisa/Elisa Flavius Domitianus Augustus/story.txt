Her dark hair was tied back in a neat braid, and her eyes sparkled with a keen business sense. With a confident smile, Elisa haggled with a vendor over the price of a shipment of fine Egyptian linens. Her opponent, a gruff old man named Marcus, refused to budge on the price, but Elisa was not one to back down. She produced a small pouch of gold coins and placed it on the table, her eyes locked on Marcus's.

"Take it or leave it," she said, her voice firm but polite.

Marcus hesitated for a moment before reluctantly agreeing to the deal. As he packed up his goods, Elisa watched him with a mixture of satisfaction and amusement. She had always been skilled at negotiating, thanks to her years of study under her father's tutelage.

As the sun began to set, Elisa made her way back to her family's palace, where she was greeted by her mother, Augusta. The two women shared a warm hug, and then Augusta led Elisa to the throne room, where her father, Flavius, sat waiting.

"Mother, I have news," Elisa said, taking her place beside her father on the throne. "I have negotiated a deal with Marcus that will increase our profits by 20%."

Flavius beamed with pride, placing a hand on Elisa's shoulder. "Well done, my daughter," he said. "You are truly following in my footsteps."

As they celebrated their good fortune, Elisa couldn't help but feel a sense of pride and accomplishment. She was not just the daughter of a wealthy family; she was a shrewd businesswoman with a bright future ahead of her.

With her family's support, Elisa Flavius Domitianus Augustus was poised to take on the challenges of the desert trade world, and make a name for herself as one of its most successful merchants.
