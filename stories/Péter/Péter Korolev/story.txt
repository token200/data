Despite his natural aptitude for stealth and subterfuge, Péter's true calling was not espionage, but rather music. As a child, he had spent hours listening to classical records in his room, mesmerized by the melodies and harmonies. It wasn't until he stumbled upon an old upright piano in his attic that he truly found his passion.

Years later, as Péter Korolev, he found himself playing sold-out shows at some of Moscow's most esteemed concert halls. His music was a unique blend of Eastern European folk traditions and modern classical influences, drawing in audiences from far and wide.

But Péter's life wasn't without its challenges. As the son of a spy, he had always been wary of being discovered by those who would seek to exploit his family's past. He moved constantly, never staying in one place for too long, and always taking great care to maintain his cover.

One night, after a particularly grueling tour schedule, Péter Korolev found himself back at his childhood home, surrounded by the familiar comforts of his parents' old apartment. For a moment, he felt like he was 10 years old again, listening to his parents argue in hushed tones about their next mission.

As he sat at the piano, running his fingers over the keys, Péter felt a sense of peace wash over him. This was where he belonged – not in the world of espionage, but on stage, lost in the music that had always been his true calling.
