People found themselves drawn to Agathe's aura, like moths to a flame. She had an unnerving presence that both repelled and attracted them at the same time. Her movements were fluid, almost ethereal, as if she was gliding across the floor rather than walking.

The shopkeeper, a hunched old man with sunken eyes, looked up from behind the counter and regarded Agathe with an unnerving intensity. "Ah, you've found the melody of lost souls," he said, his voice barely above a whisper.

Agathe's smile grew wider, her green eyes glinting in the dim light. For an instant, it seemed as if she understood some hidden truth that lay beyond the confines of this strange and dusty shop.

Without another word, Agathe opened her jacket and revealed a small tattoo on her left shoulder – a symbol etched into her skin like a promise or a warning. The ballerina music box slid to the floor with a soft thud, as if it had been set free by an unseen force.
