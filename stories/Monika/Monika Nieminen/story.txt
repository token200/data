She had always been driven, always pushed herself to be better, even when others doubted her abilities. Her parents, Finnish immigrants who had worked tirelessly to give her every opportunity, had instilled in her the importance of education and perseverance.

As she began to read through the letters, Monika felt a familiar sting of disappointment and frustration. How could they not see it? Didn't they know that she was meant for more than this?

Just then, there was a knock at the door. It was her best friend, Anna, who had stopped by to check on her.

"Hey, girl, what's going on?" Anna asked, noticing the stack of letters. "You look like you've lost your last marble."

Anna listened attentively, her expression sympathetic. When Monika finished speaking, Anna sat back and took a deep breath.

"You're not defined by these letters," she said gently. "You're so much more than just one university's acceptance or rejection."

Monika looked at her friend, feeling a spark of doubt ignite within her. Was Anna right? Had all this hard work been for nothing?
