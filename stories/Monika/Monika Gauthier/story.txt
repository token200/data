Monika Gauthier stepped off the train and onto the worn platform, the chill of the evening air snapping her out of her reverie. As she made her way through the quiet station, the soft glow of the streetlights casting long shadows behind her, she felt a sense of restlessness wash over her.

The memories of her past still lingered, however – the laughter with friends, the quiet nights spent pouring over books in the library, and the whispered secrets shared with her brother, Pierre.

As she walked through the village, Monika Gauthier couldn't help but feel a sense of disconnection from the world around her. The faces passed by on the street were strangers, their eyes fleeting as they went about their daily routines. She felt like an outsider, a ghost haunting the edges of this quiet community.

Monika Gauthier smiled, feeling a sense of belonging for the first time in weeks. Maybe, just maybe, this small town would become her new home – one where she could be herself, without apology.
