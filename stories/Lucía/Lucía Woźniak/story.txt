Growing up in a small town surrounded by crumbling factories and worn-down buildings, Lucía developed a unique perspective on the world. She saw beauty in decay and decay as beauty. Her studio was filled with sketches of the old buildings she had once called home, their peeling paint and rusty gates capturing her imagination like nothing else.

As an artist, Lucía's work was met with both praise and skepticism. Some saw her unique style as a reflection of her surroundings, while others dismissed it as mere nostalgia. But for those who took the time to look closer, Lucía's paintings revealed a world full of life and story.
