Ilze Pei-ling was a 25-year-old Taiwanese-American poet who had always been drawn to the mystical and surreal. Growing up in Taipei, she would spend hours listening to her grandmother's tales of ancient deities and forgotten mythologies, which sparked a sense of wonder and curiosity within her.

As a child, Ilze would often sneak into her grandfather's attic, where he stored old trunks and dusty tomes filled with stories from the past. It was there that she discovered her love for poetry, particularly the works of Taiwanese poets like Guo Guangyu and Yang Yuhua.

Ilze's own writing style was a unique blend of modernism and traditional forms, influenced by the rich cultural heritage of Taiwan. Her poems often explored themes of identity, family, and the struggles of growing up in a rapidly changing world.

Despite her talent, Ilze struggled to find success as a poet. She worked part-time jobs to make ends meet while writing at night, often feeling like an outsider in both worlds.
