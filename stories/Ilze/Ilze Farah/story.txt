Ilze Farah was a renowned photographer known for her poignant and thought-provoking images. Her camera had been an extension of herself since she was a child, capturing the beauty in the world's imperfections. Born in a small village on the outskirts of Mumbai, India, Ilze's passion for photography began when her mother, a skilled weaver, would show her intricate patterns and textures.

Her images often highlighted social injustices, such as poverty, inequality, and environmental degradation. Ilze's work caught the attention of prominent galleries and magazines, leading to international exhibitions and publications. Her images told a powerful story of resilience and survival, highlighting the courage and determination of these remarkable individuals.
