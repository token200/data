He smiled as he watched Sofia's determination falter in the face of the evidence against her source. She was so close to uncovering the truth that she was now on the verge of collapse. Alfred leaned back in his chair, steepling his fingers together as he waited for the perfect moment to strike.

"Tell me, Sofia," he said, his voice dripping with condescension, "what makes you think this source is trustworthy?"

Sofia hesitated, and Alfred's eyes gleamed with excitement. She was on the edge of a precipice, and he was ready to push her over.

"I...I don't know if I can trust him," Sofia stammered, her voice trembling.

Alfred chuckled, a low rumble that seemed to shake the foundations of his office. "Ah, but you must trust someone, my dear," he said, his words dripping with sarcasm. "And in this case, it's either your source or me."

As Sofia's eyes darted around the room, Alfred watched her mind racing with doubts and fears. He had manipulated her into questioning her own judgment, and now she was on the brink of collapse.

The game was far from over, but Alfred knew he had the upper hand. With a wave of his hand, he summoned his most trusted assistant, who arrived with a folder containing documents that seemed to prove the whistleblower's guilt.

Sofia's eyes widened in horror as she read through the evidence, her conviction crumbling like sand between her fingers. Alfred smiled, watching as she retreated from the story, her reputation in tatters.

And as for the whistleblower? Alfred had taken care of him, quietly eliminating any potential threats to his empire. The game was always about power, and he had played it perfectly.

As he sat back in his chair, Alfred felt a sense of satisfaction wash over him. He was the master of his domain, and no one could take that away from him. Not even Sofia, or her source, or anyone else who dared to cross his path.
