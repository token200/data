The bar was quiet, save for the occasional clink of glasses or hushed conversation between patrons. It was the kind of place where people came to escape, or to find themselves in the midst of chaos.

As Karlis sipped their whiskey, they began to lose themselves in thought once more. The memories came flooding back: childhood laughter with cousins, lazy summer days spent exploring woods behind their family's old farmhouse. And then there were the dreams – vivid, Technicolor fantasies that defied reason and pushed the boundaries of what was deemed acceptable.

Max watched Karlis Arias from across the room, a knowing glint in his eye. He'd seen plenty of lost souls wander into this bar over the years, searching for something to fill the void within.
