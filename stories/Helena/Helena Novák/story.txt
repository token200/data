Helena Novák stepped off the train and onto the worn platform, her eyes scanning the sleepy town with a mix of curiosity and trepidation. She had left behind the chaos of her life in Prague, seeking refuge in this small, rural town where no one knew her name – Helena Novák, the former lead singer of the Czech rock band, Velvet Riot.

As she made her way through the quiet streets, the weight of her past began to settle in. The memories of sold-out shows and screaming fans still lingered, but they were now tinged with regret. She had walked away from it all, leaving behind a legacy and a career that had defined her for so long.

Helena Novák took a deep breath, feeling the cool night air fill her lungs as she walked towards the small café where she had booked a room for the night. Inside, the warm light and gentle hum of conversation enveloped her, providing a sense of comfort she hadn't experienced in months.

As the night wore on and the town grew quiet, Helena Novák realized that perhaps this small town in the countryside wasn't the refuge she had hoped for. But it was a chance to start anew, to find a sense of peace and belonging that had eluded her for so long.
