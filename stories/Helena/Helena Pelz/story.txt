Helena Sofia Maria Pelz was a free-spirited artist known for her vibrant paintings of cityscapes and landscapes. Her style was a unique blend of expressionism and realism, reflecting her fascination with the world around her. Born in Berlin, Germany, Helena grew up surrounded by art and culture, which later influenced her own creative pursuits.

She spent countless hours studying the works of Monet, Van Gogh, and Picasso, learning to appreciate the nuances of color and light that defined each artist's unique style. Her experiences abroad not only shaped her artistic vision but also instilled in her a sense of wanderlust and adventure.

Upon her return to Berlin, Helena set up her own studio and began showcasing her work at local galleries and exhibitions. Word of her talent spread quickly, and soon she was receiving commissions from collectors and businesses looking to commission unique pieces.
