Müller smiled, feeling a sense of relief wash over her. She had been dreading this trip for weeks, ever since she'd received the news that her mother's art gallery was in trouble. As the daughter of the gallery's owner, Christiane Müller felt responsible for helping to save it.

Sophie led Müller through the winding streets of Montmartre, pointing out quaint cafes and charming boutiques along the way. They finally arrived at the gallery, a beautiful old building with high ceilings and large windows that overlooked the city.

 Inside, Müller's mother was pacing back and forth, looking worried. "Christiane, thank goodness you're here," she said, embracing her daughter tightly. "I don't know what I would do without you."

Müller hugged her back, feeling a sense of purpose wash over her. Together, the three women - Sophie, Müller's mother, and Christiane Müller herself - set to work brainstorming ways to save the gallery. As the days passed, Müller found herself becoming more and more invested in the gallery's future. She spent long hours helping her mother with the business side of things, and even managed to squeeze in some time for her own art projects.

In the end, it was Christiane Müller's determination and creativity that helped save the gallery. With Sophie's support and her mother's guidance, she was able to rally the community and secure a spot on the prestigious Paris Art Fair.
