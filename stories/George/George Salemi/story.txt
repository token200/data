George Salemi was a man with a passion for history and music. As a renowned musicologist, he had spent his career studying the evolution of jazz from its early days in New Orleans to its current forms in contemporary cities.

This early exposure sparked George's fascination with jazz history, and he went on to earn his Ph.D. in musicology from Harvard University.

After completing his education, George Salemi spent years researching and writing about the impact of migration on jazz development. His book, "The Migration of Jazz: A Study of Its Evolution," became a seminal work in the field, widely praised by critics and scholars alike.
