His dedication earned him recognition from local monks, who invited him to join their community as a respected seeker.

Years passed, and Reuben's reputation as a wise sage spread throughout Asia. Reuben listened attentively, then offered words of wisdom: "The river flows effortlessly, yet its path is always changing. Your journey is not defined by the currents of fate but by your own resilience."

Mei departed with a renewed sense of hope, inspired by Reuben's wisdom. News of his remarkable abilities soon reached the ears of Emperor Wu of Han, who invited him to serve as a spiritual advisor. As Reuben Tzu-chi sat beside the emperor, he shared ancient secrets and provided counsel on matters of state.

Throughout his life, Reuben continued to walk the path of the Tao, embodying its principles in every aspect of his being. His name became synonymous with wisdom, compassion, and inner peace, inspiring generations to come. Though he eventually passed into the realm of legend, the essence of Reuben Tzu-chi lived on, guiding seekers towards enlightenment and a deeper understanding of the world around them.
