One day, a stranger arrived in town. He was tall and lean, with piercing green eyes that seemed to see right through you. No one knew much about him, but rumors began to circulate that he was a magician or perhaps even a spy. Reuben was both intrigued and wary of the newcomer, but as she watched him work on his own mysterious projects in the dead of night, she found herself drawn to him.

As the days passed, Reuben Bērziņa began to notice strange occurrences happening around town. Flowers would bloom in mid-air, and objects would move on their own. She couldn't help but wonder if the stranger was behind these mysterious events. One evening, as she watched him packing up his things to leave, she approached him with a question.

"You're the one they call 'the magician'?" she asked, her voice barely above a whisper.

The stranger turned to face her, his eyes locking onto hers with an intensity that made her heart skip a beat. "I am no magician," he said, his voice low and smooth. "But I do have certain... abilities."

Reuben felt a shiver run down her spine as the stranger reached into his pocket and pulled out a small, delicate flower. He offered it to her, and she took it without hesitation.

"This is for you," he said, his eyes never leaving hers. "A token of my appreciation for your curiosity."

As Reuben watched him disappear into the night, she couldn't help but wonder if she had made a deal with something more than just a simple stranger. She turned the small flower over in her hand, feeling an inexplicable connection to it.

From that day on, Reuben Bērziņa found herself noticing things about the world around her that she never had before.
