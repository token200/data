Despite its unorthodox nature, "Echoes in the Abyss" had already generated significant buzz within the music community. Some critics praised Ulan Zariņš's boldness and innovation, while others dismissed his work as pretentious or even bizarre.

Ulan Zariņš himself remained unfazed by the criticism. He was driven by a desire to push the boundaries of what was possible in music, and to explore the uncharted territories of the human experience. One evening, as Ulan Zariņš sat at his piano, working on a new piece, he felt a sense of clarity wash over him. The music swirled around him like a vortex, drawing him deeper into its depths.
