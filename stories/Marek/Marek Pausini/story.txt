As a child, Marek would often sneak into his father's art studio to watch the older artists at work. He was particularly drawn to the bold colors and expressive brushstrokes of the Polish avant-garde movement. As he grew older, Marek began to experiment with his own art, teaching himself techniques and styles that he had observed in the studio.

Despite his passion for painting, Marek struggled to find his place in the world. Feeling discouraged, Marek turned to his father's old easel in the backyard and began to paint once more.
