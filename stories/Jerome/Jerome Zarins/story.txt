One rainy evening, Jerome Zarins received a visit from the local police department, seeking his expertise in solving a string of mysterious burglaries. The owner of the targeted businesses - a small bakery and a vintage bookstore - was beside herself with worry, convinced that her livelihood was at stake.

Jerome Zarins listened intently, his eyes narrowing as he processed the details. He asked pointed questions, his tone measured, yet probing enough to make even the most evasive witnesses squirm. As he packed up his things to leave, one of the officers approached him with a curious expression. "Jerome Zarins, how do you do it?" they asked, their tone laced with admiration.

Jerome Zarins smiled wryly, knowing that only a handful of people understood the intricacies of his mind. He leaned in, his voice barely above a whisper. "It's simple, really," he said. "I just listen to what you're not saying."
