KP's hard work paid off when he was drafted by the Washington Wizards with the 5th overall pick in the 2003 NBA draft. He quickly became a fan favorite in D.C., known for his explosive scoring ability and clutch performances.

Despite struggling to adjust to the faster pace of the NBA, KP persevered and continued to develop as a player. He eventually found success with the Dallas Mavericks, where he formed a dynamic duo with Dirk Nowitzki. Together, they led the team to the Western Conference Finals in 2006.

But KP's personal life was marred by controversy. In 2007, he was arrested for driving under the influence (DUI) after crashing his car on the side of the road. He pleaded guilty and served a year-long suspension from the league.

Despite this setback, KP remained committed to his craft. He continued to work tirelessly in the gym, refining his skills and adapting to new roles on the court. In 2010, he signed with the Phoenix Suns as a free agent, where he formed a potent trio with Steve Nash and Amar'e Stoudemire.

KP's most memorable moment came during the 2011 playoffs, when he hit a game-winning three-pointer against the San Antonio Spurs in Game 5. The buzzer-beater capped off an incredible comeback for the Suns, sending them to the Western Conference Finals.

Throughout his career, KP was known for his rugged individualism and no-nonsense approach to the game. But despite his tough exterior, he had a soft spot for his family and friends back in Latvia.
