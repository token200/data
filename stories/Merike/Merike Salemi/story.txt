Merike Salemi stood on the windswept shores of Tallinn, Estonia, her long black hair whipping about her face as she gazed out at the Baltic Sea. A renowned photographer and artist, Merike had spent years capturing the beauty and essence of her native country, but today she felt restless.

As she breathed in the salty air, Merike's mind wandered to the exhibition that was soon to open in Berlin. She had poured her heart and soul into the work, a collection of photographs and paintings inspired by the mythical creatures of Estonian folklore. "The Fae of Estonia" was the title of the show, and Merike couldn't wait for the world to see it.

As she walked through the deserted streets of Tallinn's Old Town, Merike felt a sense of nostalgia wash over her. She had grown up in this very city, listening to the stories of her grandmother about the mythical creatures that roamed the forests and bogs. The tales of the kiusa, the hulgi, and the nukitsa had sparked her imagination as a child, and now, as an adult, Merike sought to capture their essence on canvas.

As she turned a corner, Merike spotted a group of street performers gathered around a makeshift stage.
