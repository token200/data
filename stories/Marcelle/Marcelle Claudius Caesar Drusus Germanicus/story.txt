Upon arriving, he heard whispers of an impending invasion by the Germanic tribes, long-time enemies of Rome.

His thoughts drifted to his father, who had once entrusted him with the command of a legio, only to withdraw it mere weeks later when Tiberius deemed Marcelle not yet ready for the responsibility. But Marcelle knew that was simply politics; he had already seen action on several campaigns in Germania, and his experience would be invaluable in this coming war.

Without warning, Gaius grasped Marcelle's arm, pulling him close to whisper a single word: "Tonight." A spark of understanding flared between them – the night ahead promised change, perhaps even rebellion. But for Marcelle Claudius Caesar Drusus Germanicus, the uncertainty was intoxicating; he felt alive.

With Gaius's parting nod, Marcelle vanished into the crowd, disappearing into a world of anticipation and possibility.
