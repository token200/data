Despite his success, Ricardo Kask struggled with self-doubt and imposter syndrome. He felt like an imposter among his peers, who seemed to effortlessly create pieces that spoke to something deep within themselves. His own work, however, was more about experimentation than emotional connection.

The vendor claimed they had been salvaged from an old building that had been demolished years ago. Intrigued by the story behind each handle, Ricardo Kask purchased several and began incorporating them into his art.

Ricardo Kask's studio began to fill with an assortment of door handles, each one telling its own tale.
