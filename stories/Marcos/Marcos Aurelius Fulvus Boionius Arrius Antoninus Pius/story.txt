And his son, Commodus, though still young and unpredictable, showed signs of following in Pius's footsteps.

As the day wore on, Pius attended to matters of state, receiving ambassadors from foreign lands and making important decrees that would shape the course of Roman history. Despite the weight of responsibility, he remained humble and grounded, always mindful of his duties as a leader.

He pondered the mysteries of life and death, reflecting on the wisdom of his favorite philosopher, Epictetus.
