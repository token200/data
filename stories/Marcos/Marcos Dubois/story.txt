Marcos Dubois was a man with a passion for art and adventure. As a young boy, he spent hours sketching the vibrant cityscapes of Paris, where his family had settled after fleeing war-torn Haiti. Growing up, he dreamed of becoming an artist, but his parents, who struggled to make ends meet, encouraged him to pursue a more stable career.

Undeterred, Marcos enrolled in art school, where he honed his skills and developed a unique style that blended traditional Haitian techniques with modernist flair. His teachers were impressed by his talent, but it was his bold personality that truly set him apart. Whether he was painting, sculpting, or simply talking to anyone who would listen, Marcos had an infectious energy that drew people in.

After art school, Marcos struggled to make a name for himself. He worked odd jobs and painted murals in abandoned buildings, just to keep creative juices flowing. But when the 2010 Haiti earthquake hit, everything changed. The devastating disaster destroyed his family's home and left thousands without shelter or access to basic necessities.

Marcos decided to channel his grief into something positive.
