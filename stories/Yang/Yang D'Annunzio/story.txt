As the sun dipped below the rooftops, Yang found himself captivated by Lena's stories of the American South and her experiences as a woman in a male-dominated field. He shared his own struggles with traditional Italian society and the stifling expectations placed upon him as a young artist.

Their conversation flowed effortlessly, like a slow dance through the streets of Montmartre. The night air was filled with the scent of cigarette smoke and fresh paint, and Yang felt his heart flutter in Lena's presence.
