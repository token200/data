Intrigued, Sebastian Hussein lit a cigarette and began walking down the street, lost in thought. He had always had a knack for sniffing out clues, and he couldn't resist the temptation to dig deeper into this new case.

She looked up at him with tears in her eyes and whispered, "Please, Mr. Hussein, I need your help. My sister has gone missing, and the police aren't taking me seriously."

Sebastian Hussein's expression softened as he took in her distress. He nodded sympathetically and handed her a tissue from his pocket. "Tell me more about your sister," he said, pulling out his notebook.

As they spoke, the rain continued to pour down around them, but inside, Sebastian Hussein's thoughts were already racing ahead to the next step in this investigation. He made a mental note to pay a visit to the missing girl's last known address and get some leads on her whereabouts.
