Born in Poland, Mariusz had grown up in a tumultuous household, where his parents' constant arguments and infidelity had created a toxic environment. As a result, Mariusz learned early on how to shield himself from the world, becoming introverted and guarded.

Despite this, Mariusz's intellect and curiosity drove him to pursue a career in science, earning him a PhD in physics from a prestigious university. His groundbreaking research on quantum mechanics earned him recognition within his field, but it also made him an outcast among his peers, who saw him as aloof and detached.

Despite the heartbreak, Mariusz never forgot Sophie, and their reunion sparked a mix of emotions within him.

As they caught up on lost time, Mariusz found himself drawn to Sophie once again. He was struck by how little she had changed - her bright smile, piercing green eyes, and infectious laugh still held the power to captivate him. They spent hours talking, reminiscing about old times and sharing new stories, their connection growing stronger with each passing minute.

As the night drew to a close, Mariusz walked Sophie home, feeling an overwhelming sense of longing he hadn't experienced in years. He realized that his emotional armor had finally begun to crack, revealing a deep vulnerability beneath. For Mariusz Rousseau, it was a liberating feeling, one that allowed him to confront his past and forge new connections with those around him.
