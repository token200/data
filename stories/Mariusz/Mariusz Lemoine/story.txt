Mariusz Lemoine's love of music was equally strong, and he often found himself lost in the rhythm and melody of a well-played instrument. He had learned to play the piano as a child, and later had taken up the guitar, finding solace in its gentle, soothing sounds.

As an artist, Mariusz Lemoine had always been drawn to the works of the Old Masters - Rembrandt, Vermeer, and Monet. Their use of light and shadow, their ability to capture the subtleties of human emotion, was something he aspired to emulate in his own work.
