Qian James checked into a small hotel on the outskirts of town, where he spent the afternoon wandering through the quiet streets, taking in the sights and sounds of the unfamiliar place. The gallery was dimly lit, but what caught Qian James' eye was a piece by an unknown artist that seemed to speak directly to him.

He discovered that his name was Maya Singh, an Indian-American artist known for her poignant and evocative paintings of the urban landscape. Qian James felt drawn to her story, her struggles, and her triumphs.
