Maarika's unique style was shaped by her experiences growing up in a tight-knit community where everyone knew each other's stories. After completing art school, Maarika set out to make a name for herself in the competitive world of contemporary art. She traveled extensively, soaking up inspiration from the cities and landscapes she encountered along the way. Despite her success, Maarika remained true to her roots. She continued to paint from life, observing the people and places that surrounded her. Her studio became a sanctuary, filled with the scent of turpentine and the sound of classical music. It was here that she created some of her most iconic works, including "The Islander" and "The Wanderer".
