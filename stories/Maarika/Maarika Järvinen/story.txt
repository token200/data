But Maarika never spoke about her life before Kuusamo. She preferred to keep to herself, lost in thought as she gazed out at the frozen lake.

As they walked together, Maarika began to speak of her passions: poetry and nature, art and silence. She was a weaver of words, creating tapestries that told stories of the world's hidden rhythms. Her voice was like honey on the tongue, sweet and soothing.

Lena listened, entranced, as Maarika spoke of her love for Kuusamo's vast expanse. She felt as though she'd stumbled upon a treasure trove of secrets, hidden away in this quiet corner of the world. As the storm raged outside, Maarika Järvinen opened up to Lena like a gate to a secret garden.
