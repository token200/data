Her full name, Li Kask, meant "Lightning Strike" in the local dialect, a moniker that suited her lightning-fast reflexes and precision.

As she arrived at the palace, Li could feel the weight of expectation upon her shoulders. The nobles, dressed in their finest silks and jewels, gazed at her with a mixture of curiosity and disdain. Little did they know that Li Kask was about to prove herself as more than just a pretty face.

With a subtle smile, Li began to warm up, stretching her lithe limbs and testing the limits of her muscles. The nobles watched in rapt attention as she executed a series of intricate movements, each one showcasing her mastery of the ancient art of Kung Fu. Her footwork was swift, her blows precise, and her defense unyielding.

As the demonstration progressed, Li's confidence grew, and so did the murmurs of admiration from the nobles. They had underestimated this fiery warrior, but now they saw her in a new light. The Emperor himself rose to his feet, applauding her display with a warm smile.

"Well done, Li Kask," he said, extending an invitation for her to join the palace guards as their personal instructor. "Your skills are a testament to your remarkable prowess and dedication. I have no doubt that you will serve me well in times of need."

With that, Li Kask knew that she had truly earned her place among the elite. Her full name, Li Kask, echoed through the halls of the palace, and from that day on, she was known as a hero and a symbol of Chinese bravery.
