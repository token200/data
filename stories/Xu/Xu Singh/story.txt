Xu Singh was a young and ambitious artist living in the bustling streets of Old Delhi. Born as Rajiv Kumar Saxena, he had always been fascinated by the intricate patterns and colors of traditional Indian art. As a child, he would spend hours watching his grandmother, Dadiji, paint vibrant murals on their family's ancient walls.

As Rajiv grew older, his passion for art only intensified. He began to explore different mediums, from watercolor to acrylic, and even dabbled in calligraphy. His artistic talents soon caught the attention of local galleries, who offered him solo exhibitions and commissions.

Despite his growing success, Rajiv remained humble and true to his roots. He continued to draw inspiration from his childhood memories and Dadiji's teachings. His art was a reflection of the rich cultural heritage he grew up with, with intricate patterns and bold colors that seemed to pulse with life.
