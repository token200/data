But despite the constant flux, she had grown into a strong-willed and intelligent young woman.

Agrippina, in particular, was becoming increasingly erratic and paranoid, as if she sensed her own mortality.

Signe had always felt trapped by her family's dynamics, caught between the ruthless ambition of her mother and the benevolent, yet ultimately weak-willed, nature of her father. She longed to break free from this web of intrigue and forge her own path in life.

Just then, a soft voice interrupted her reverie. "Signe Claudius Nero?" Her childhood nurse, Aurelia, approached her, concern etched on her face.

As Signe gazed up at the star-filled sky, a sense of hope settled within her chest. Perhaps Aurelia was right – perhaps there were those out there who would see beyond her royal title and understand the girl she truly was.
