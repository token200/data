But Julien's success was short-lived. A tragic accident left him injured and unable to perform for months. As he lay in bed, struggling to come to terms with his new reality, he received an unexpected visit from his estranged father, a former communist soldier who had abandoned him years ago.

His father told Julien that he had been searching for him everywhere, ashamed of the pain he had caused and wanting to make amends. Julien was skeptical at first, but as he listened to his father's words, he began to see the man in a new light. For the first time in his life, he felt a sense of closure and forgiveness.

With his newfound understanding, Julien slowly began to rebuild his life. He started writing music again, this time with a sense of purpose and direction. His band, now renamed after him, released an album that was met with critical acclaim and commercial success.

As Julien Bērziņš took the stage to accept his award, he felt a sense of pride and accomplishment wash over him. He thought back to the long and winding road that had brought him here, and smiled, knowing that he had finally found his true voice.
