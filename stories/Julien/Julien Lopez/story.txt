His father, a retired journalist, would take him on long walks and teach him about the art of storytelling through images. As he grew older, Julien's interest in photography only deepened, and he began to experiment with different techniques and styles.

After studying photography in college, Julien landed an internship at a prominent magazine in New York City. It was there that he honed his skills and built a portfolio of work that would eventually land him a job as a freelance photographer.

Years later, Julien found himself on assignment in the desert, capturing the majestic beauty of Monument Valley. As he reviewed his shots, Julien couldn't help but feel a sense of pride and satisfaction. Julien's passion for photography had taken him on countless adventures, from the streets of Manhattan to the vast expanses of the American West.
