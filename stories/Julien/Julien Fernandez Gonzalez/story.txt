Julien Fernandez Gonzalez was a man with a passion for art and music. Growing up in the bustling streets of Barcelona, he spent most of his days exploring the city's hidden corners, taking in the vibrant colors and sounds that seemed to seep from every brick and stone.

As a child, Julien was fascinated by the works of Spanish surrealist artists like Dalí and Miró, whose bold and dreamlike creations sparked something within him. He spent hours pouring over their paintings, trying to decipher the hidden meanings and symbolism that lay beneath the surface.

But it wasn't just art that captivated Julien's imagination – music also played a significant role in his life. His love affair with guitar began when he stumbled upon an old Spanish lute in his grandfather's attic. The intricate carvings and delicate curves of the instrument mesmerized him, and soon he was spending hours practicing the haunting melodies of traditional Catalan folk songs.

As Julien grew older, his passion for art and music only intensified. He went on to study fine arts at the Barcelona College of Art, where he honed his skills in drawing, painting, and sculpture. It was during this time that he discovered a new appreciation for the works of modern Spanish artists like Miró and Picasso.

After completing his studies, Julien set out on a journey to explore the world's most vibrant art scenes. He spent several years traveling through Europe, immersing himself in the city's galleries, museums, and street art. The sign above the door read "La Casa de la Música," and Julien felt an inexplicable pull to enter. Inside, he found a treasure trove of vintage guitars, art supplies, and eclectic trinkets that seemed to speak directly to his soul.

It was as if fate had brought him full circle – from his grandfather's attic to this tiny shop in Madrid, where the walls were adorned with Julien Fernandez Gonzalez's own vibrant artwork.
