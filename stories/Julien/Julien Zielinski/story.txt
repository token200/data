Julien Zielinski was known for his unorthodox approach to art, blending traditional techniques with modern technology. His studio in Brooklyn's Bushwick neighborhood was a reflection of this fusion, with half-finished sculptures and sketches covering every available surface.

As a child, Julien had always been fascinated by the way light interacted with form. Growing up in Poland, he spent countless hours watching the way sunlight danced through his family's stained glass windows. This early interest in optics would later influence his use of LED lights and fiber optics in his installations.

Despite the attention from art critics and collectors, Julien remained committed to pushing boundaries. His most recent piece, "Fractured Light," was a sprawling installation that utilized specialized software to create an immersive environment. Viewers were surrounded by a maze of mirrors and glass, with light rays bouncing off every surface.

When asked about his creative process, Julien would often speak in vague terms, preferring to let the work itself reveal its intent. However, those who had witnessed him at work knew that he was meticulous in his research, pouring over texts on optics and physics to inform his art.

Julien hesitated for a moment before agreeing, knowing that this would be a chance to share his unique perspective with a wider audience.
