Julien Wei, or Lee Wei as he was known to his family and friends back in Taipei, had always been fascinated by the world of art. Growing up, he spent most of his free time sketching and painting, lost in a world of colors and textures. His parents, though not initially supportive of his creative pursuits, eventually came to see the passion and dedication that drove Julien Lee Wei forward.

After completing his studies, Julien moved to Paris to pursue a career in fine art. He was determined to make a name for himself in the competitive world of contemporary art, and he spent every waking moment honing his craft, experimenting with new techniques and mediums. His apartment became a gallery of sorts, with paintings and sketches covering every available wall.

Despite the challenges he faced as an outsider in the city, Julien remained focused on his goals. He spent long hours at the Louvre, studying the works of the Old Masters and learning from their brushstrokes.
