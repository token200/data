Istvan's eyes narrowed as he considered the request. He had always been driven by a desire to balance the needs of the Roman state with those of its citizens. As Istvan Aelius Hadrianus, he knew that this delicate balancing act was crucial to his success.

He walked through the city, meeting with merchants and traders, listening to their concerns and negotiating on their behalf. His reputation as a fair and just ruler had spread far and wide, earning him the admiration of even his most skeptical subjects.

As the sun began to set, Istvan returned to his palace, where he was greeted by his wife, Vibia Sabina. "My dear," she said, smiling, "I see you have been busy again today." Istvan smiled back, feeling a sense of contentment wash over him. Being Emperor was not just a title, but a duty, and as Istvan Aelius Hadrianus, he took that duty very seriously.

Over dinner, Istvan discussed the day's events with Vibia, sharing stories of his meetings and negotiations. As they ate, he felt grateful for this life, for this city, and for the people who had entrusted him with their fate.
