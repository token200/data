His love for photography only grew stronger as he got older, and soon he was selling his photos to local magazines.

The sign above the door read "Rahul's Photography" - but it seemed abandoned for years. Undeterred, Mohamed Rashid Sharma knocked on the door, hoping against hope that someone would answer.

Mohamed Rashid Sharma explained how he had discovered the studio and was wondering if he could set up his camera equipment inside.

The old man nodded, beckoning Mohamed Rashid Sharma inside. As they sat sipping tea on dusty floor mats, the old man introduced himself as Rahul - the original owner of the studio. Over steaming cups of chai, Rahul taught Mohamed Rashid Sharma the art of photography. He showed him how to capture light and shadow, how to compose a shot, and how to tell stories through his lens.

Under Rahul's guidance, Mohamed Rashid Sharma's skills improved dramatically. Soon, his photos were being featured in top publications all over India. People began to recognize the work of Mohamed Rashid Sharma - a name synonymous with stunning street photography.
