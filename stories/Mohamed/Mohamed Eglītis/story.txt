Others claimed he was a master of manipulation, weaving words like a skilled weaver, drawing in listeners with promises of solace and redemption.

Entranced by his words, she felt her heart skip a beat as he spoke of the trials and tribulations of life. She introduced herself after the performance, and Mohamed smiled kindly, his eyes crinkling at the corners.

"You are one who has listened with an open heart," he said, as if reading Nino's very soul. "I will write for you alone, dear Nino."

Thus began a unlikely partnership between poet and listener. Over the coming weeks, Mohamed Eglītis penned verses tailored specifically to Nino's unique experiences and emotions. Together, they strolled through Tbilisi's historic streets, exploring the city's hidden corners as he read his work aloud.

As word of their bond spread, others began to seek out Mohamed, hoping to unlock the secrets of his craft. But he remained elusive, content to weave his magic for those who were willing to listen – and trust in the depths of his mysterious, masterful words.
