As news of her discovery spread, international attention turned to Tsai Keiko. Media outlets clamored for interviews, while governments begged her to share her findings. But Keiko remained resolute, unwilling to compromise her research or reveal too much about her extraordinary work. Her quiet confidence and unshakeable conviction had earned her a reputation as one of the most brilliant minds in science.

In the midst of all this attention, Tsai Keiko found herself lost in thought, staring at the stars through the skylight of her Tokyo apartment. As she stood there, surrounded by the trappings of her success, she felt an unsettling sense of solitude, a reminder that some secrets were meant to remain hidden forever.
