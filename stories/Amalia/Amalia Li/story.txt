As she navigated through the throngs of people, Amalia Li's thoughts turned to her childhood in Beijing, where her love affair with art had begun. Her parents, both successful businesspeople, had encouraged her creative pursuits from a young age, and she had spent hours sketching and painting as a child. But it was only after moving to Tokyo that she discovered her true passion – using technology and digital media to create immersive, interactive installations.

As she watched the crowd gather around one of her pieces, a sense of satisfaction washed over her.
