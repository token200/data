Born in Budapest, Hungary, she had grown up surrounded by the vibrant colors and textures of traditional folk art. As a child, Eszter would spend hours watching her grandmother weave baskets and scarves, mesmerized by the way the threads seemed to come alive in her hands.

Years later, as Eszter Lace settled into her own studio, she was determined to carry on the family tradition. She spent countless hours perfecting her craft, experimenting with different materials and techniques until she had developed a unique style that blended traditional Hungarian motifs with modern flair.

One day, a local fashion designer stumbled upon Eszter's work while browsing an art fair.
