Eszter Jankowska sat at her desk, staring blankly at the stack of rejection letters in front of her. As a struggling writer, she had grown accustomed to the sting of disappointment, but it never got any easier.

Born and raised in Budapest, Hungary, Eszter had always been drawn to the written word. She spent hours as a child poring over books, dreaming of one day becoming a published author. But life had other plans.

After completing her degree in journalism, Eszter landed a job at a local newspaper, where she worked tirelessly to make ends meet. However, her true passion lay with fiction, not non-fiction. When the newspaper downsized, Eszter found herself without a job or a steady income.

Determined to pursue her dreams, Eszter enrolled in an online writing course, determined to hone her craft and get published. She spent countless hours honing her skills, writing short stories and novellas that she shared with friends and family.

Despite her efforts, the rejections piled up. Editors would politely decline her submissions, citing that they didn't quite fit their market or style. Eszter's self-doubt began to creep in, making her wonder if she was cut out for this writing business after all.

One day, as she was scrolling through social media, Eszter came across a post from an old friend who had recently moved to New York City. Her friend mentioned that she had started working with a small publishing house that specialized in emerging writers. Intrigued, Eszter sent her friend a message, asking if they would be interested in reviewing her work.

To her surprise, the publishing house replied within hours, inviting her for a meeting. Eszter spent weeks preparing, pouring her heart and soul into her manuscript. When she arrived at the meeting, she was met with warm smiles and encouragement from the publisher and editor-in-chief.

The next few months were a whirlwind of edits, revisions, and proofreading. Eszter poured every ounce of energy into her work, determined to get it just right. Finally, on a crisp autumn morning, her manuscript arrived back at her desk, bearing the coveted stamp of approval.
