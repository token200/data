This ancient practice allowed him to harness the power of the elements, bending the winds and summoning the rains with mere thought.

Aslanbek's arrival on the mainland had been met with both curiosity and suspicion. A stranger in a strange land, he seemed out of place among the concrete skyscrapers and bustling streets of New Eden. Yet, despite the initial unease, people began to notice that Aslanbek possessed an otherworldly aura about him.

He wandered the city streets, his eyes scanning the crowds as if searching for something or someone. His presence was accompanied by an unmistakable scent, a subtle blend of sandalwood and citrus that seemed to draw people in like moths to a flame. Strangers would approach him, feeling an inexplicable sense of trust, as if Aslanbek possessed some long-forgotten knowledge that only he could share.

One such encounter took place on a rainy evening at the city's oldest bookstore. The owner, a wise and aged man named Zephyr, had taken a particular interest in Aslanbek's enigmatic presence.
