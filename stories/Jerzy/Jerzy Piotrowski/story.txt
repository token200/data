The sun was setting, casting a warm orange glow over the historic buildings and narrow alleys. As a young historian, Jerzy spent most of his days poring over dusty tomes in the city's many museums and libraries.

Jerzy's eyes scanned the crowded street market stalls, where vendors sold everything from traditional Polish clothing to handmade wooden toys. He had been searching for a specific book for weeks, one that held the key to understanding his family's history. His grandmother used to tell him stories of their ancestors, who had lived in Poland during World War II.

As he wandered through the market, Jerzy noticed an old man hawking books from a worn leather satchel. The vendor, noticing Jerzy's interest, beckoned him over with a gnarled finger. "Ah, you're looking for something specific?" he asked in a raspy voice.
