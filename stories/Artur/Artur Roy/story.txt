His parents, both artists themselves, encouraged his creativity and provided him with endless supplies of paints, brushes, and canvases. As he grew older, Artur Roy's love for art only deepened, and he began to explore different mediums, from oil paints to watercolors.

Music became a new outlet for Artur Roy when he discovered the guitar. He spent hours practicing and learning chord progressions, eventually forming his own band with friends. The sound of their music was a unique blend of folk and rock, reflecting Artur Roy's eclectic style.

Despite his passion for art and music, Artur Roy struggled to make ends meet. As an artist, he sold paintings occasionally, but it wasn't enough to sustain him financially. He took on part-time jobs to supplement his income, working as a waiter or a bartender to earn enough to keep himself and his friends afloat.

One day, a local art gallery owner stumbled upon Artur Roy's work at an open mic night. She was impressed by his talent and offered him a solo exhibition.
