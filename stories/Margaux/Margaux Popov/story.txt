Margaux Popov, a renowned photographer known for her captivating portraits, sat in front of her camera, gazing through the lens. Her parents, both artists themselves, encouraged her curiosity, gifting her cameras and encouraging her to experiment with light and shadow.

She had shot for top publications, exhibited her work in galleries worldwide, and mentored aspiring photographers along the way. As she captured the gritty beauty of the cityscape, Margaux Popov's thoughts turned to her studio back home – the one where she had first developed her skills.
