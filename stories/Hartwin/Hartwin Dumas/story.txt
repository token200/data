His mother had passed away recently, leaving behind only a cryptic letter and a promise to uncover the truth about their family's history. Hartwin's father was long gone, lost at sea when he was just a boy.

Hartwin's curiosity was piqued. He finished his meal and approached Étienne, who beckoned him over with a gnarled hand. As they sat down together, Étienne began to spin a tale of Hartwin Dumas' ancestors, of wars fought and won, of love and loss.
