Years later, Fadumo Ozols had established herself as a respected photographer, traveling the world to capture poignant moments and stunning landscapes for publications around the globe. Her distinctive style, which blended traditional African influences with modernist sensibilities, had captivated audiences and earned her numerous awards and accolades.

Despite her success, Fadumo Ozols remained committed to using her platform to highlight social justice issues, particularly those affecting women and marginalized communities in Africa. One such assignment took Fadumo Ozols to a small village in South Sudan, where she spent several weeks documenting the lives of women who had fled their homes due to civil unrest. Her images of these resilient women – working tirelessly to rebuild their communities, caring for their children, and asserting their rights in the face of overwhelming odds – were both heart-wrenching and uplifting.

Fadumo Ozols' photography was more than just a technical exercise; it was a powerful tool for storytelling, one that allowed her subjects to share their own voices and perspectives with the world.
