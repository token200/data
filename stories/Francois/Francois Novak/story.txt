"Francois Novak," she said, her voice barely above a whisper.

He smiled, feeling a sense of belonging wash over him. "Lena."

As they walked through the streets, Francois Novak was struck by how little things had changed. The same cafes served the same coffee, the same shops sold the same wares. It was as if time had stood still while he was away.

"So, what brings you back?" she asked, her tone laced with curiosity.

Francois Novak took a bite of his breakfast, savoring the taste before responding. "I needed to get away," he said finally. "The city was suffocating me."

Lena nodded, understanding etched on her face. Francois Novak knew that she would always be here for him, no matter what. He had come back to Ravenswood seeking a sense of peace, and in Lena's eyes, he found it.
