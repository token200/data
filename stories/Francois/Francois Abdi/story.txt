Francois Abdi trudged through the muddy streets of Kigali, his worn shoes making squelching sounds with each step. Francois Abdi had always been driven by a desire to tell stories that needed to be told, and this story was no exception. He had spent hours poring over documents and speaking with sources, and he was convinced that he had finally found the evidence he needed to take down the corrupt officials.

Francois Abdi stopped in front of a rundown building, where one of his sources had agreed to meet him. He knocked on the door, and after a moment, it creaked open to reveal a woman with a look of fear in her eyes.

"I'm Francois Abdi," he said, flashing his journalist's ID. "I need your help with my story."

Francois Abdi listened intently, his notebook and pen at the ready. When she finished, he nodded thoughtfully. "I have what I need," he said, tucking his notebook into his bag.
