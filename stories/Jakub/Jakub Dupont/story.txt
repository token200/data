Jakub Dupont's laboratory was a marvel of modern science, filled with rows of humming machinery and shelves of exotic chemicals. He spent most of his days hunched over a workbench, tinkering with tiny devices and scribbling notes in a worn leather journal. Despite his intense focus, there was an air of quiet melancholy about him, as if he were haunted by secrets only he could see.

As she approached the workbench, Jakub Dupont looked up from his notes, his eyes narrowing behind thick-rimmed glasses.

He led her on a tour of the laboratory, pointing out various contraptions and explaining their purpose in hushed tones.
