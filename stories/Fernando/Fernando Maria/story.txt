As he grew older, Fernando Maria became increasingly fascinated with art. He spent countless hours in the city's museums and galleries, soaking up inspiration from the works of masterpieces that spanned centuries. His own talent was evident, even in those early days – a unique blend of modernity and traditional techniques that set him apart.

The applause that greeted his arrival seemed to wash over him like a wave of validation. People spoke with reverence about his pieces, mentioning them in hushed tones as if they held secrets only known to a select few.

"You're Fernando Maria," she said, extending a hand. "I've heard so much about your work."

Fernando Maria took her hand, feeling an unexpected sense of connection. "The pleasure is mine," he replied, his voice low and smooth. "Tell me – what do you see in my art?"
