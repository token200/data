As a child, she would spend hours watching her mother paint masterpieces on silk canvases, while her father played the guqin, an ancient Chinese zither.

As she grew older, Amandine's love for music only deepened. She began to study the piano and violin, eventually earning a scholarship to attend the prestigious Shanghai Conservatory of Music. It was there that she discovered her true calling: fusion music, blending traditional Chinese instruments with modern Western styles.

After completing her studies, Amandine traveled extensively throughout Asia and Europe, soaking up diverse musical influences and collaborating with fellow musicians from around the world. Her unique sound, which she described as "Eastern winds meeting Western waves," quickly gained international recognition.
