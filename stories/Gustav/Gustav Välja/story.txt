As he walked through the snowy forest, Gustav Välja's eyes scanned the horizon for any sign of trouble. The reindeer were restless, and he knew that they could sense his unease. Gustav Välja's expertise was not just limited to herding; he was also a skilled craftsman. He had spent countless hours learning the art from his grandmother, who had learned it from her mother before her.

As he worked, Gustav Välja's mind wandered back to his childhood. Growing up in the village, he had watched his parents struggle to make ends meet, their income tied to the whims of the tourists who came to visit Lapland. But Gustav Välja had always known that there was more to life than just survival – he had a vision for a future where the Sami people could thrive on their own terms.
