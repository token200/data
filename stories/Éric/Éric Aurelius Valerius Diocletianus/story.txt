As he grew older, Éric's insatiable curiosity and thirst for knowledge took him on countless adventures through the ancient world. He studied the works of the great philosophers, from Aristotle to Epicurus, and devoured the stories of the gods like a starving man at an all-you-can-eat feast.

But despite his many accomplishments, Éric remained humble and true to himself. He never forgot where he came from, and always kept a foot on the ground, no matter how far he soared. And indeed, Éric Aurelius Valerius Diocletianus was soon summoned to the imperial court, where he would go on to prove himself as one of the greatest minds of the ancient world. As Éric looked out at the glittering lights of Rome from his new perch, he couldn't help but smile to himself. He had come a long way from those humble dunes in Palmyra, and yet, he remained true to his roots.
