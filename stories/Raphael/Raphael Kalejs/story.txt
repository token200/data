Raphael Kalejs was a Latvian artist known for his bold and provocative works that often explored themes of identity, culture, and social hierarchy. Born in 1946 in Riga, Latvia, to a family of Jewish descent, Kalejs's experiences with persecution and exile would later influence his art.

As Raphael Kalejs delved deeper into his art, he began to explore themes of racial identity and social justice. His work often featured dark-skinned figures and critiques of colonialism, highlighting the experiences of marginalized communities. This was evident in his iconic piece "The White Slave Trade," which depicted a group of white figures standing over a figure with African features.
