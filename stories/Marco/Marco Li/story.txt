Marco Li was a man with a passion for photography and storytelling. As a documentary filmmaker, he had spent years traveling to different parts of the world, capturing the lives of people from diverse backgrounds and cultures.

Growing up, he would spend hours listening to their tales of struggle and resilience, which later inspired him to become a documentary filmmaker.

After completing his studies at film school, Marco Li began working on a series of documentaries that explored the lives of marginalized communities around the world. His first film, "The Forgotten", told the story of a group of elderly Tibetan refugees who had fled their homeland in search of safety and security. The film premiered at several international festivals, earning widespread critical acclaim.

Marco Li's next project was perhaps his most ambitious to date: a five-part series that examined the impact of climate change on communities around the world. Titled "The Last Generation", the documentary followed the lives of young people from different countries as they struggled to come to terms with their own futures in a rapidly changing world.

Throughout the series, Marco Li's camera captured the beauty and hardship of life in the face of environmental disaster. From the melting glaciers of Antarctica to the ravaged landscapes of the American West, the film took viewers on a journey to the front lines of one of humanity's greatest challenges.

As he worked on "The Last Generation", Marco Li couldn't help but think back to his own childhood in Beijing, where the city was slowly undergoing a transformation that would change its face forever.
