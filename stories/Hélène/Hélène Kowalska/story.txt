Bertrand's obsession with his craft had often left Hélène feeling like an afterthought, and she'd struggled to keep up with his mercurial moods. But despite it all, she couldn't deny the passion that still burned between them - a flame that seemed to flicker brighter whenever they were together.

As she sat there, lost in thought, Hélène's mind began to wander back to their first meeting. She'd been a young woman, full of fire and idealism, when Bertrand had swept into her life like a tornado. He'd been charming and charismatic, with an artist's eyes that seemed to see right through her. And she'd been captivated - by his passion, his conviction, and the way he made her feel like she was the only person in the world.

Hélène Kowalska took a deep breath, trying to shake off the melancholy that had settled over her. But as she gazed out into the darkness, she couldn't help but wonder if she'd ever find a love like theirs again.
