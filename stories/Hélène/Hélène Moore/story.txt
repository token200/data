Hélène Marie Thompson, better known as Hélène Moore to her friends and acquaintances, was a woman with a passion for art and a talent for self-reinvention. Born in Paris to an American father and French mother, she had always been drawn to the city's vibrant cultural scene.

After completing her studies, Hélène decided to leave France and start a new life in New York City. She settled into a small studio apartment in Brooklyn and quickly became immersed in the local art scene. Despite her success, Hélène remained true to her artistic vision, refusing to compromise on her unique style. As the years passed, Hélène's reputation grew, and she found herself in high demand as a painter and art consultant.
