The boy was no more than ten winters old, with eyes wide with wonder as he watched the warrior dispatch a group of bandits with ease. Kaito was awestruck by Lucie Hideki's skill and precision, and she noticed his fascination.

As Kaito trailed behind her, Lucie Hideki couldn't help but notice the boy's eagerness to learn. She took him under her wing, teaching him the ways of combat and survival that had been passed down through generations of her tribe. With each passing day, Kaito grew stronger and more confident, his bond with Lucie Hideki deepening.

Together, they faced countless challenges, from ferocious beasts to rival clans, but Lucie Hideki remained steadfast, protecting Kaito as if he were her own flesh and blood. The boy learned valuable lessons about loyalty, discipline, and the importance of standing up for what was right.

As Kaito grew into a young man, Lucie Hideki began to see him not just as an apprentice, but as a potential successor to her legacy. She realized that it was time to pass on the mantle of leadership, trusting Kaito to carry forward the traditions and values of their tribe. With a sense of pride and accomplishment, Lucie Hideki watched as Kaito took his place by her side, ready to face whatever challenges lay ahead.

Though Lucie Hideki's own days as a warrior were drawing to a close, she knew that her legacy was in good hands with Kaito.
