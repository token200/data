But Saltanat had other plans. Despite her parents' wishes, she had chosen to pursue a career in music, much to their dismay. She had grown up listening to traditional Kazakh folk songs and had always dreamed of performing on stage. With the support of her grandmother, who had taught her how to play the tanpura, Saltanat began taking singing lessons.

As she honed her craft, Saltanat's talent became apparent to everyone in town. She was a natural-born singer with a voice that could melt hearts. People would often gather around her whenever she performed at local events, mesmerized by her beauty and talent.

Despite her success, Saltanat remained humble and grounded. She continued to live in Tastubey, surrounded by the people who had supported her from day one. Her family may have not understood her choice of career path, but they couldn't deny her dedication and passion for music.

One day, a group of musicians from Almaty came to perform at the local festival. Among them was a renowned composer, who was impressed by Saltanat's talent. He approached her after her performance and offered her a recording contract with his label. Overjoyed, Saltanat accepted the offer and soon found herself in the studio, laying down tracks for what would become her debut album.

As the months passed, Saltanat's fame spread across Kazakhstan, and eventually beyond its borders. She became known as one of the most promising young singers of her generation, and people everywhere admired her unique blend of traditional and modern music. And though she had started out in small-town Tastubey, Saltanat Kulbayeva was now a star, shining bright for all to see.

Years later, when asked about her journey, Saltanat would smile and say that it was her grandmother's wisdom that had guided her along the way: "Your voice is a gift from God," she would remind her.
