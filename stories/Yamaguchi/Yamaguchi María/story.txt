Yamaguchi María was a young and talented painter known for her vibrant works of art that captured the essence of Japanese culture. Born in Tokyo, María grew up surrounded by the city's rich history and stunning landscapes, which would later inspire her unique style.

As a child, María spent hours exploring the streets of Shibuya with her grandmother, learning about traditional Japanese crafts and collecting materials for future projects. Her grandmother, Obaachan, was a skilled weaver who taught María the intricacies of hanabi-iko, a traditional Japanese dyeing technique.

Years later, María would go on to study fine art at Tokyo University, where she honed her skills under the guidance of renowned artists. She was particularly drawn to the works of Monet and Renoir, whose Impressionist style influenced her own use of color and light.
