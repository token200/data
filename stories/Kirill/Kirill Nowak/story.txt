Nowak's latest lead had taken him to the offices of the city's most powerful businessman, Vladimir Petrov. Rumors swirled that Petrov was embezzling funds meant for the city's infrastructure projects, leaving residents with crumbling roads and outdated public services.

With his camera slung over his shoulder and a notebook in hand, Nowak made his way to Petrov's high-rise office building. He had an appointment with the man himself, one that he hoped would finally uncover the truth behind the rumors.

As he entered the sleek, modern lobby, Nowak was greeted by a stern-faced receptionist who eyed him warily. "Mr. Nowak? You're expected," she said curtly, gesturing for him to follow her.

Nowak was ushered into Petrov's office, where the businessman himself sat behind a massive desk, his expression as cold as the marble walls that surrounded them. "Ah, Kirill Nowak," he drawled, extending a manicured hand. "I've heard so much about you. Please, come in."
