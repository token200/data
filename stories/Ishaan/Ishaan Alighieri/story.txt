Ishaan Alighieri stepped off the train and onto the worn platform, his eyes scanning the dusty station for any sign of life. He had been traveling through India for weeks, searching for a connection to his family's past. As he made his way through the crowded streets, the sounds and smells of Mumbai enveloped him - a cacophony of horns and laughter that was both overwhelming and exhilarating.

Ishaan Alighieri had always felt like an outsider in his own country, as if there was something missing from his heritage. His parents, who had immigrated to England when he was young, had never spoken much about their family's history, leaving Ishaan with a void that he longed to fill.

As he navigated the narrow alleys of Old Mumbai, Ishaan stumbled upon a small, mysterious shop tucked away in a corner. The sign above the door read "Antique Collectors' Paradise" and Ishaan felt an inexplicable pull towards it. Ishaan nodded hesitantly, unsure how much to reveal about his true intentions. The old man smiled knowingly and began to rummage through shelves lined with dusty trinkets and artifacts.

"Where did this come from?" Ishaan asked, his eyes locked on the old man's.
