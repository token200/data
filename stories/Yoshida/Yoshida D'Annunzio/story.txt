Yoshida D'Annunzio's parents had taught her the intricacies of traditional Japanese cooking, from hand-pulling udon noodles to expertly roasting yakitori chicken. As she watched the sun set behind the towering skyscrapers, she knew that one day she would pass on her knowledge and skills to a new generation of chefs.
