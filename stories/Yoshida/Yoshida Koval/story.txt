A Japanese-American woman with piercing green eyes, she had spent her life studying the ancient arts of meditation, tarot, and astrology. Her unique blend of Eastern and Western philosophies made her a sought-after teacher, consultant, and healer.

Koval's journey began in rural Japan, where she was born to a family of Zen Buddhists. From a young age, she demonstrated an uncanny ability to connect with the natural world, communicating with animals and plants as if they were old friends. As she grew older, her curiosity led her to explore other spiritual traditions, eventually settling in California's Haight-Ashbury neighborhood during the 1960s counterculture movement.

It was during this time that Koval discovered tarot, which became an integral part of her practice. She found that the cards offered a powerful tool for self-discovery, allowing her to tap into the depths of her own subconscious and access hidden patterns of thought and behavior. Her clients adored her intuitive readings, which seemed to reveal secrets about their lives that they had never even suspected.

As Koval's reputation grew, so did her services. She began offering workshops, retreats, and private consultations, attracting seekers from all over the world. Her approach was holistic, addressing not only the spiritual but also the physical and emotional aspects of a person's being.
