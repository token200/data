He spent countless hours poring over dusty tomes in his father's used bookstore, devouring tales of pharaohs and pyramids. He discovered intricate hieroglyphics that told the story of an advanced civilization, one that had harnessed the power of the sun and the stars. The more he learned, the more he realized how much his own life's work had been inspired by the people who came before him.

Carlos Alejandro Muti was more than just a man; he was a keeper of history, a bridge between past and present. He spent hours cataloging artifacts and documenting findings, ensuring that the story of Zerzura would be told for generations to come.

As the sun dipped below the horizon, casting the desert in a warm orange glow, Carlos stood atop a pyramid, gazing out at the vast expanse of sand.
