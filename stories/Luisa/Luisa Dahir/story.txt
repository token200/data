Luisa Dahir stepped off the train and onto the platform, her eyes scanning the bustling station. She had been away for months, working in Nairobi to save up for her dream of becoming a journalist. As she made her way through the crowds, she spotted her sister, Sofia, waiting for her by the exit.

"Sis!" Sofia exclaimed, throwing her arms around Luisa's neck. "I've missed you so much!"

Luisa hugged her back tightly, feeling a mix of emotions: happiness to see her family, but also a sense of unease about what lay ahead. She had always felt like an outsider in their small town, with its tight-knit community and limited opportunities for women.

Back home, Luisa's parents were struggling to make ends meet, and she knew that if she was going to help them, she needed a solid education and a decent job. But her parents didn't understand why she wanted to leave, why she needed to chase a dream that seemed so impossible.

As they walked through the streets, Sofia asked Luisa about her plans now that she was back in town. Luisa hesitated, unsure of how much to reveal. She had always been passionate about storytelling, but it wasn't something you could just talk about over dinner or at family gatherings.

"I'm thinking of starting a blog," she said finally, her voice barely above a whisper. "To write about the stories that need to be told."

Sofia's eyes widened in surprise, but then a look of determination crossed her face. "You have to do it," she said. "We believe in you, Luisa. We know you can make a difference."

Luisa smiled, feeling a surge of gratitude towards her sister. With Sofia by her side, she knew that she could conquer anything.

As they made their way back home, Luisa realized that maybe this was the start of something bigger than just herself. Maybe it was time to use her voice to speak out against injustice and fight for change.
