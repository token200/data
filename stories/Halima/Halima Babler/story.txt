Word spread quickly about Halima Babler's newfound maternal instincts, but few suspected that beneath her tough exterior lay a heart of gold.

With tears streaming down her face, she begged Halima to help her find a new place to stay.

Halima hesitated at first, still wary of her mother's motives. But something about the genuine desperation in her eyes melted her defenses. She agreed to let her mother move in with her, on one condition: that she promise to keep up with household chores and refrain from using the words "bless you" when Halima coughed.

As the days went by, Halima Babler found herself adjusting to life with a new roommate. Her mother proved to be a hard worker, but Halima still had to intervene whenever she started telling terrible jokes. Despite the occasional eye-roll, the two began to bond over late-night conversations about everything from literature to existential crises.

Through her relationships with Luna and her mother, Halima Babler slowly revealed a more vulnerable side to herself.
