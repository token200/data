Liu Szymańska stepped out of her small apartment in Beijing, taking a deep breath of the crisp morning air. As a renowned photographer, she was no stranger to the city's energy and vibrancy. But today, she felt restless.

Born to Polish parents in China, Liu had grown up navigating two worlds. Her Chinese heritage influenced her art, which often explored themes of identity and belonging. She had built a name for herself as one of Beijing's most innovative photographers, capturing moments both beautiful and haunting on film.

As she made her way through the city streets, Liu noticed something peculiar – people were staring at their phones, oblivious to their surroundings. They would walk down a street lined with vibrant street art, only to glance at their screens without giving it a second thought.

She quickened her pace, weaving past pedestrians who barely looked up from their devices. It was as if the world had become smaller and more insular, where people were more focused on individual experiences than collective ones.
