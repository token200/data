Lev Lewis stepped out of his small apartment and into the crisp morning air. He took a deep breath, feeling the familiar sting of exhaustion from another late night spent working on his latest project. As a freelance graphic designer, Lev's schedule was always unpredictable, but he wouldn't have it any other way.

He walked to the local coffee shop, where he knew the owner would be waiting with a warm cup and a sympathetic ear. "The usual, Mr. Lewis?" she asked, as he settled into his favorite booth by the window.

Lev nodded, taking a sip of the rich, bold coffee that was just the way he liked it. As he sipped, he pulled out his sketchbook and began to doodle. Ideas were always flowing at 6 AM, and Lev was happy to capture them before they slipped away.

Just as he was getting into a groove, the door swung open and a familiar face walked in. It was Rachel, his best friend and confidant since childhood. They exchanged warm smiles, and Lev closed his sketchbook, feeling grateful for the interruption.

Rachel joined him at the table, sipping on her own coffee. "How's it going?" she asked, as Lev filled her in on his latest design project.

Rachel was a writer, and she had a way of listening that made Lev feel like he could share anything with her. She was always full of insightful questions and clever observations, and Lev valued her opinion above all others.

As they chatted, an idea began to form in Lev's mind. He pulled out his sketchbook again, this time filled with rough outlines and fragmented ideas. Rachel watched with interest as he worked through the design, her eyes lighting up with excitement.

"You're thinking of launching your own business?" she asked, as Lev showed her the latest iteration.

Lev grinned, feeling a rush of excitement at the prospect. "I'm not sure yet," he said, "but I think it could be worth a shot."
