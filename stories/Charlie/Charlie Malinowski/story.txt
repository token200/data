His studio in Brooklyn's Bushwick neighborhood was a testament to this eccentricity, with canvases splattered across the walls and half-finished sculptures scattered about.

His latest exhibition, "Disintegrating Boundaries," showcased his signature blend of abstract expressionism and surrealist elements. Critics praised the bold colors and textures that seemed to dance across each piece, as if alive.

Charlie's inspiration often came from his own life experiences. Growing up in a Polish-American family, he was exposed to traditional folk art and music, which later influenced his use of vibrant colors and patterns.
