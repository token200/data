A young woman, barely out of her teens, had been found dead in an abandoned warehouse on the outskirts of town. The police were stumped, and it was up to Sylvie Gomez Perez to unravel the tangled threads of the case.

As she surveyed the scene, Sylvie's trained eye picked up on tiny details that others might have missed: a faint scent of lavender, a thread of torn fabric caught in the rusty gate. Sylvie spent hours pouring over the evidence, her mind working overtime as she pieced together the fragmented clues. It wasn't until late that night, after hours of fruitless research, that the breakthrough came: a cryptic message scrawled on the wall in red ink, a single phrase that seemed to leap off the page.

"Sombra y luz" - Shadow and light - it was an anagram, and one that Sylvie Gomez Perez knew all too well. Suddenly, the entire case clicked into place. She tracked down a series of hidden leads, each one leading her closer to the killer's true identity.
