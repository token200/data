As a young woman, Mia Pētersone had been fascinated by the way things could be transformed from one state to another. Her work was often eerie and unsettling, but it was also strangely beautiful.

One day, a local gallery owner named Anna stumbled upon Mia Pētersone's work while out on a walk. She was immediately struck by the artist's unique talent and sense of vision. Over the next few weeks, Anna met with Mia multiple times to discuss exhibiting her art.

Over their conversations, Anna discovered that Mia Pētersone had grown up in Riga, Latvia. Her parents had been artists themselves, and they had encouraged her creative pursuits from a very young age. But as she grew older, Mia became increasingly restless, feeling confined by the narrow streets and rigid traditions of her homeland.

Eventually, Mia made the bold decision to leave Latvia behind and pursue her artistic dreams in the West. Anna was thrilled when Mia agreed to exhibit her work at their gallery, and together they worked tirelessly to prepare for the show.
