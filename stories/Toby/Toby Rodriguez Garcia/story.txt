As a child, Toby would spend hours watching his mother dance salsa in the living room, her hips swaying to the beat as she spun around the room. He was captivated by her energy and passion, and he knew from a young age that he wanted to capture that same spirit on canvas.

Toby's parents encouraged his creativity, enrolling him in art classes at a local studio where he met his mentor, Maria, a renowned painter who taught him the techniques of traditional Latin American folk art. Under Maria's guidance, Toby developed his skills and learned to blend vibrant colors with bold textures, creating pieces that were both stunning and meaningful.

As he entered adulthood, Toby began to experiment with new mediums, incorporating found objects and recycled materials into his work. He became fascinated with the concept of "sustento," or self-sufficiency, and began to create art from discarded waste, giving it new life and purpose.

Toby's unique style quickly gained attention in local galleries, where his vibrant murals and sculptures were met with critical acclaim. People marveled at the way he could transform trash into treasure, infusing each piece with a sense of hope and renewal.
