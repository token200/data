As he wandered through the galleries, Gerard Leroy's eyes would often linger on the Impressionist paintings, mesmerized by the way the light danced across the canvas.

Gerard Leroy's eyes grew wide as he took in the treasures before him. He spotted a half-finished portrait of a woman, her face softened by the gentle curves of a brush stroke.
