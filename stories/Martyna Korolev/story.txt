Martyna's breakthrough came when she was selected to perform in the lead role of Giselle at the Mariinsky Theatre in St. Petersburg. Her debut performance was met with thunderous applause and critical acclaim, earning her a coveted spot as one of Russia's leading ballerinas.

Despite her success, Martyna remained humble and true to her artistic vision. She continued to push herself creatively, exploring new choreographers and collaborating with innovative artists. In 2010, she danced alongside renowned violinist Itzhak Perlman in a production of Rachmaninoff's ballet score to the Second Symphony.

Martyna Korolev is now a celebrated artist in retirement, dedicating herself to teaching, mentoring young dancers, and sharing her passion for ballet with future generations.
