Growing up in Sweden, he had spent countless hours exploring the countryside, collecting leaves and pinecones for his mother's prized garden. These simple pleasures had sparked a sense of wonder that had stayed with him throughout his life.

Lars Stenstrom's artwork was a reflection of this same curiosity. His sculptures, made from discarded materials like wire and fabric, were both whimsical and thought-provoking. They seemed to hold secrets, inviting viewers to peer into their intricate details.

One day, as Lars was assembling a new piece, he received an unexpected visit from his estranged sister, Lena. She had been living abroad for years, but her return to Sweden had brought her back to the city where Lars lived and worked.

But as they sat down to talk, something shifted inside of him. He listened intently as Lena shared stories of her adventures abroad, his initial reserve giving way to a sense of wonder.
