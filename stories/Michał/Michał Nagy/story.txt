Michał Nagy stepped off the train and onto the worn platform, the cool evening air enveloping him like a familiar friend. He had been away for months, chasing opportunities in the city that never slept, but now he was back in his hometown of Kraków, ready to settle into his family's old bakery.

The familiar smell of freshly baked bread and the sound of laughter from the bakery's windows had always been a comfort to him. Michał Nagy smiled, feeling a pang of guilt for being away so long. He had been working towards this moment for years, studying and saving to take over the family business. His father had passed away suddenly, leaving him in charge of the bakery, and he was determined to make it thrive.

This was what he was meant to do – run his family's business with love and care, just as his parents had done before him.
