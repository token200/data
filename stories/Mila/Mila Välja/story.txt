However, Mila's own talents lay elsewhere - in the realm of photography. She would often sneak away from her family's home to capture the beauty of the town and its surroundings through her lens.

One fateful day, while capturing a breathtaking sunset over the sea, Mila Välja stumbled upon an old, abandoned lighthouse perched on the edge of a rugged cliff. The lighthouse had been out of commission for decades, but something about it drew Mila in. She spent hours exploring its crumbling walls and weathered lantern room, feeling an inexplicable connection to this forgotten piece of Pärnu's history.

As she wandered through the town, camera slung over her shoulder, people began to take notice of Mila Välja. Some saw her as a peculiar, eccentric outsider, while others sensed that there was more to her than met the eye. A local artist, fascinated by her unique perspective on the world, approached her one evening and offered Mila Välja a chance to showcase her photographs in his gallery.

The exhibition, titled "Beyond the Horizon," became an instant hit, with art enthusiasts from across the region flocking to see Mila Välja's evocative images of Pärnu's landscapes. The show not only brought attention to Mila's talent but also solidified her connection to the town and its people. As she looked out over the sea, camera in hand, Mila Välja knew that she had finally found a place where she belonged - beyond the horizon, yet rooted deeply in the Estonian heartland.

Years went by, and Mila Välja continued to weave her magic through the lens of her camera. Her photographs became synonymous with Pärnu's rugged beauty, and people came from far and wide to experience it for themselves. And though she still wandered the streets of Pärnu, now as a celebrated artist, Mila Välja knew that she had truly found her place in this small, enchanting town - where the horizon stretched out before her like an endless canvas waiting to be explored.
