As she grew older, Céline's talent became harder to ignore. She began sneaking out at night to create murals on abandoned buildings, much to the chagrin of local business owners. But when the city finally recognized her work and offered her a spot in an official street art festival, Céline knew she had made it.

That summer, Céline Piątek's mural, "La Vie En Rose," became a sensation, attracting tourists and locals alike with its bold, swirling patterns and playful references to French-Canadian culture. Céline Piątek's rise to fame had been meteoric, but she remained humble and grounded.
