Céline Rodríguez García had always been fascinated by the world of art. Growing up in a small town in Spain, she spent most of her free time sketching and painting, inspired by the works of Spanish Masters like Velázquez and El Greco.

As she grew older, Céline's passion for art only intensified. She enrolled in a prestigious art school in Madrid, where she honed her skills under the guidance of some of the best artists in the country. Her hard work paid off when she was awarded a coveted scholarship to study abroad in Paris.

Céline Rodríguez García's big break came when a prominent art critic discovered her work in a small exhibition in the Eixample district of Barcelona. The critic wrote a glowing review, praising Céline's bold use of color and her ability to capture the essence of modern Spanish life.
