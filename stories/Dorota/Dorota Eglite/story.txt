In 1922, she returned home to her family's farm, where she took over as manager after her father's retirement. It was a decision that surprised many people - including herself.

Dorota Eglite proved to be an innovative farmer, implementing new techniques and crop rotation methods to increase yields and reduce waste. She also established a small school on the farm, teaching local children about sustainable farming practices and the importance of conservation.

In 1945, Dorota Eglite was awarded the title of "Best Farmer in the County" for her outstanding contributions to agriculture.
