Gergely Müller, a renowned Hungarian jazz pianist and composer, sat hunched over his keyboard, lost in thought as he stared out the window of his Parisian apartment. The City of Light was known for its artistic flair, but Gergely felt most at home when surrounded by the sounds of improvisation and creativity.

As the evening wore on, Gergely began to feel inspired by Zoli's stories about his family and their struggles during the communist era in Hungary.
