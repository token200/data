Juliette Kalēja stepped off the crowded bus and onto the worn sidewalk of downtown Riga. Juliette Kalēja had always been drawn to the artistic and bohemian crowd that inhabited this corner of Latvia, but lately, she'd found herself struggling to find her place within it.

As she turned onto Valtera iela, the sounds of traditional Latvian folk music drifted from a nearby café, drawing Juliette Kalēja in like a magnet. She pushed open the door and was immediately enveloped by the warm scent of coffee and the soft glow of twinkling lanterns. The musician playing the balalaika looked up and caught her eye, his dark hair tousled and his eyes crinkled at the corners as he smiled.

Juliette Kalēja took a seat at the bar and ordered a cup of black coffee, her eyes never leaving the musician's face. As she waited for her drink to arrive, she felt an inexplicable sense of calm wash over her, as if all her worries had been left at the door. When the musician finally finished his set, he made his way over to her, introducing himself as Edvins.

As they talked, Juliette Kalēja felt herself being drawn back into the world she'd been struggling to reconnect with – the world of art and music and community. As the night drew to a close, Edvins asked Juliette Kalēja if she'd like to join him on stage the following evening, where he would be performing at a benefit concert for a local charity. Without hesitation, Juliette Kalēja agreed, feeling a sense of purpose and belonging that had been missing from her life for far too long.
