He spent countless hours pondering the mysteries of life, morality, and human nature. As he delved deeper into his studies, he became increasingly fascinated by the teachings of Epictetus, a former slave turned philosopher who had captured Bernard's imagination.

Bernard's natural leadership qualities soon caught the attention of his fellow Romans, who began to look up to him as a beacon of hope in times of turmoil. When the empire was threatened by wars and corruption, Bernard saw an opportunity to make a difference. The young man asked how Bernard had achieved such success in life, despite facing countless obstacles along the way. Bernard smiled, looking out at the sprawling city beyond his palace walls. "My dear friend," he said, "it is not about achieving greatness; it's about living with purpose and integrity. Remember that you are a temple of God, and every action you take should be an offering to the divine."
