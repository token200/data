Watanabe Muti was a master of disguise and deception. He lived in feudal Japan, where honor and loyalty were the most prized virtues. But for Muti, these values were nothing more than a game to be played with.

A skilled thief and con artist, Muti had made a name for himself in the underbelly of samurai society. With his quick wit and silver tongue, he could talk his way out of (or into) just about any situation.

One day, Muti received an offer from a wealthy merchant who wanted him to steal a valuable scroll from the temple of Kiyomizu-dera. The merchant was willing to pay a handsome sum for the scroll, which contained ancient secrets and prophecies. But the temple's high priest was notorious for his keen eye for thieves and his ability to spot a fake.

Muti accepted the job, knowing that it would be a challenge worthy of his skills. He spent weeks studying the temple's layout, learning its secrets and waiting for the perfect moment to strike. Finally, the night arrived when he slipped in through a hidden entrance, avoiding the guards and making his way deep into the temple.

With a flick of his wrist, Muti released the mechanism that unlocked the scroll's case. He reached inside, feeling the parchment's smooth surface, before carefully extracting it from its resting place.

But just as he was about to make his escape, Muti heard the high priest's voice echoing through the corridors. The old man had been tracking him all along! With lightning-fast reflexes, Muti transformed into a group of samurai guards, complete with fake mustache and ornate armor. He froze in place, his heart pounding, as the priest approached.

The old man eyed Muti warily, but he couldn't quite put his finger on what was different about him. The high priest ultimately dismissed him, unaware that the thief had just saved his own life by switching places with one of his own guards.
