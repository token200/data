Watanabe Masato trudged through the snow-covered streets of Kyoto, his eyes fixed on the small café in front of him. It was a place where he often escaped the chaos of his life as a freelance photographer.

"Masato-san! Welcome back," she said, handing him a steaming cup of coffee. "How's life been treating you?"

Watanabe Masato shrugged, taking a sip of the hot liquid. It was a topic he rarely discussed with anyone. His work as a photographer was his passion, but it also came with its share of struggles.
