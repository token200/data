Despite her success, Inoue Mendes was not without her flaws. She had a tendency to speak her mind, often getting into heated arguments with the town's grumpiest resident, Mr. Jenkins. However, she never let that stop her from doing what she loved – baking bread for the entire town.

One day, a new family moved into town, bringing with them their own young daughter named Lily. The little girl was immediately drawn to Inoue Mendes' bakery, where she would spend hours watching the baker work her magic. Inoue Mendes took a liking to Lily and began teaching her the art of baking.

As the days went by, Inoue Mendes found herself growing more attached to Lily, who reminded her of herself when she was younger. Despite the occasional argument with Mr. Jenkins, Inoue Mendes had finally found a sense of balance in her life, thanks to her new friendship with Lily.
