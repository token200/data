Born in the small town of Kalmthout, Netherlands, she had always been fascinated by the world of plants and spent most of her childhood exploring the surrounding countryside, collecting leaves and seeds.

The news of her discovery sent shockwaves through the scientific community, cementing Martine's reputation as one of the leading experts in her field. She went on to publish numerous papers and books, including a comprehensive guide to the orchids of the Amazon region.

Despite her many accomplishments, Martine remained humble and dedicated to her work. She continued to travel the world, searching for new species to study and protect.
