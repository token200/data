Martine Liepins was a force to be reckoned with on and off stage. As the lead singer of the iconic folk rock band, The Whiskey Wanderers, she had built a reputation for her powerful voice, captivating stage presence, and unapologetic lyrics.

Born Martine Liepins in small-town Alberta, Canada, Martine grew up surrounded by the sounds of folk and country music. She began writing songs at a young age, drawing inspiration from her own life experiences and those of those around her. Her early days as a singer-songwriter were marked by countless open mic nights, small gigs, and endless busking sessions.

As The Whiskey Wanderers' lead vocalist, Martine's voice soared above the chaos of their live shows, conjuring up emotions that resonated deeply with audiences everywhere. Her songwriting tackled topics ranging from heartbreak to social justice, earning her a loyal following and critical acclaim.

Despite the band's success, Martine remained committed to using her platform for good. She frequently spoke out on issues like women's rights, environmentalism, and LGBTQ+ equality, inspiring countless fans to join the conversation.
