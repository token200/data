Máté took a deep breath, feeling the weight of responsibility settle upon him. His stepfather had been a just and fair ruler, beloved by his people. Máté vowed to follow in his footsteps, using his own wisdom and compassion to guide Rome.

The sun began to set, casting a golden glow over the landscape. Máté felt a sense of determination rising within him. He knew that he had much to learn, but he was ready to take on the challenge. As he looked out at the world beyond Rome's borders, he whispered the words etched into the walls of his childhood home: "De vita beata".
