Some said he was a former soldier, using his skills to survive on the streets before turning to a life of crime. But Arend Jansone never spoke of his past, and those who claimed to have known him were always vague about their experiences.

A hooded woman sat alone, sipping a cup of wine as she watched the other patrons. Something about her drew him in, and he made his way towards her.

As he approached, the woman looked up, her eyes narrowing slightly as she took in Arend Jansone's imposing frame. But when their gazes met, something flickered in her expression – a spark of recognition that vanished as quickly as it appeared. "You're quite the legend," she said finally, her voice husky.

Arend Jansone smiled wryly, his eyes glinting with amusement. "I suppose that's true," he replied, taking a seat across from her. The air was thick with tension as they engaged in a silent conversation, their words lingering just beyond the realm of understanding.
