In the bustling streets of Tokyo's Shimokitazawa district, a young and talented musician named Sasaki Sandström made a name for himself in the local music scene. Born to a Swedish mother and Japanese father, Sandström had grown up surrounded by the vibrant sounds of jazz and folk music.

His debut album, "Sakura no Yuki," ( Cherry Blossom Snow), had been a critical success, with critics praising his hauntingly beautiful arrangements and poignant songwriting.
