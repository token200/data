Grace Yi-hsien spent most of her days hunched over her potter's wheel, carefully shaping and molding each piece with precision and love. Her fingers moved deftly, as if guided by an invisible force, as she coaxed the clay into beautiful forms that seemed to come alive in her hands.

As a child, Grace Yi-hsien would watch in awe as her grandmother worked on her latest creations, mesmerized by the way the pottery seemed to transform from dull, lifeless lumps of clay into vibrant works of art. She would sit beside her, asking endless questions and learning the intricacies of the craft.

Years went by, and Grace Yi-hsien's skills only improved. People from all over Taipei flocked to see her creations, which were not only functional but also possessed a certain magic that seemed to radiate from within. One day, a young couple, eager to start their new life together, approached Grace Yi-hsien's stall. The woman's eyes landed on a beautiful, delicate vase, adorned with intricate patterns that seemed to dance across its surface. She reached out a trembling hand, and as her fingers made contact with the vase, she felt an inexplicable sense of connection.

Grace Yi-hsien smiled knowingly, her eyes twinkling with delight. "This one is special," she said, her voice low and soothing. "It will bring you joy and beauty for many years to come."
