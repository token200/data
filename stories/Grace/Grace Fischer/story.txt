Grace Fischer stepped off the train and onto the worn platform, her eyes scanning the quaint station. The small town of Willow Creek was a far cry from the bustling city she had called home for so many years. As a former advertising executive, Grace had grown tired of the fast-paced lifestyle and longed for something more.

She had inherited her grandfather's old cottage on the outskirts of town from his passing, and with it came the promise of a simpler life. The realtor had warned her that the house needed work, but Grace was undeterred by the challenge. She had always been drawn to renovation projects, and this one felt like an opportunity to start anew.

As she walked through the overgrown garden, memories began to flood her mind. Her grandfather had spent countless hours tending to these very flowers, teaching her the value of patience and hard work. He had also instilled in her a love for baking, which was why the scent of fresh-baked cookies wafting from the kitchen drew her in like a magnet.

The cottage itself needed a lot of TLC, but Grace was thrilled to tackle each task with gusto. She spent hours sanding down the worn wood furniture, repainting the walls, and scrubbing away at the layers of grime that had accumulated on the windows.

As the sun began to set, casting a warm orange glow over the cottage, Grace stepped back to admire her handiwork. It was starting to feel like home. She lit a candle and poured herself a cup of tea, feeling a sense of peace wash over her.

Just then, there was a knock at the door. It was Emily, the owner of the local bakery next door. The two women had met briefly when Grace first arrived in town, but they hadn't had a chance to talk properly yet.

"I brought some fresh-baked scones," Emily said with a smile, her eyes twinkling with amusement. "I heard you were renovating my best friend's old cottage."
