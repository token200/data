Grace Wei (李伟) was a 25-year-old freelance writer who had always been fascinated by the world of ancient Chinese mythology. Growing up in a small town in rural China, she spent most of her childhood listening to her grandmother's stories about the gods and goddesses of old.

As she grew older, Grace's love for mythology only deepened, and she decided to pursue a career in writing. She moved to Beijing to study creative writing at a prestigious university, where she honed her skills and developed a unique voice that blended traditional Chinese culture with modern sensibilities.

After graduating, Grace began to make a name for herself as a writer of historical fiction. Her debut novel, "The Red Thread," was a critically acclaimed retelling of the classic Chinese legend of the Weaving Maiden and the Cowherd. The book's success allowed her to quit her day job and focus on writing full-time.
