José Pawlak was known for his eccentric behavior and unparalleled talent as a jazz pianist. As the leader of the band "Midnight Serenaders," he had gained a cult following in the city's underground music scene.

On this particular evening, José Pawlak took to the dimly lit stage, his fingers dancing across the keys of his worn Steinway piano. He remembered his father, a skilled violinist, taking him to listen to recordings by Frank Sinatra and Billie Holiday.
