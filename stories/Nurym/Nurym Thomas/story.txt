Nurym grinned back, feeling a sense of peace wash over her. This was where she belonged – surrounded by the people and places that made her feel like home. She had left Ravenswood after high school to chase her dreams in the city, but after two years of struggle and heartache, she'd realized that something was missing.

Rachel led Nurym out of the station and into the bright sunlight, where their parents were waiting with big smiles on their faces. "Hey, kiddo!" her mom called out, opening her arms for a hug. "We've missed you so much!"

Nurym hugged her tightly, feeling a sense of gratitude wash over her. This was what she needed – not the hustle and bustle of city life, but the love and support of her family and friends.

She spent hours chatting with the locals at the diner, reminiscing about old times and sharing stories about her adventures in the city. As she worked, Nurym felt a sense of purpose growing within her. This was where she belonged – surrounded by the people and places that made her feel alive. The memories of her past were still there, but they no longer dominated her life. She had found a new path, one that wound its way through the heart of Ravenswood, and Nurym Thomas was happy to be on it.

The days blended together in a warm haze of sunshine and laughter, as Nurym rediscovered the joys of small-town living. And when she finally looked up at the stars that night, she knew that this was where she belonged – home, surrounded by love and light, with no place to go but forward.
