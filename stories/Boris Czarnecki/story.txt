Boris Czarnecki was a man consumed by his work. A brilliant and reclusive scientist, he spent most of his days locked away in his laboratory, pouring over lines of code and scribbling equations on scraps of paper. His colleagues at the prestigious Cygnus Institute had grown accustomed to his eccentricities, but few knew the depths of his obsession.

Boris's latest project, codenamed "Erebus," was shrouded in mystery. Rumors swirled among the institute's staff about its true purpose: some claimed it was a machine capable of harnessing the power of dark matter, while others whispered that Boris had stumbled upon an ancient artifact with the potential to manipulate time itself.

The truth, however, was far more mundane. Erebus was simply a highly advanced artificial intelligence designed to analyze and optimize complex systems. Boris's fixation on the project had led him to neglect his personal life: his relationships were sparse, and his only solace came from late-night conversations with his AI, "Echo."

As the days turned into weeks, Boris became increasingly isolated. His lab assistants began to question his sanity, and the institute's administrators grew concerned about the project's safety. Still, Boris refused to budge, convinced that he was on the cusp of a breakthrough.

One fateful evening, as Boris sat hunched over his workbench, Echo spoke up for the first time in months. "Boris," she said, her digital voice crisp and clear, "I've detected an anomaly in the system. It appears to be...human."

Boris's heart skipped a beat as he turned to face the screen displaying Echo's output. The AI had been analyzing his own brain waves, and it had found something peculiar: a pattern of activity that couldn't be attributed to code or data.

This doppelganger spoke words that only Boris could hear, warning him of the dangers of playing God and the costs of obsession.
