A skilled potter, Nero spent most of his days hunched over his wheel, shaping clay into beautiful works of art that seemed to come alive in his hands.

As the sun beat down upon him, Nero's thoughts often wandered to his late father, who had taught him the ancient art of pottery when he was just a young boy. Jorge Claudius Nero was torn, unsure if he should accept the offer or continue working on his own terms.

As the years passed, Jorge Claudius Nero's reputation grew, and his work became sought after by collectors from all over the world. He never forgot his humble beginnings, however, always staying true to himself and his art.
