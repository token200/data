Mori Fliesbach stepped into the dimly lit tavern, his eyes scanning the room for any signs of trouble. As the proprietor's son and current enforcer, he had grown accustomed to the weight of responsibility that came with protecting the family business.

The sign above the door read "Fliesbach's" in bold letters, a testament to Mori's father's reputation as one of the most cunning bootleggers in the city. But those who knew him also understood that beneath his gruff exterior lay a heart of gold.

Mori made his way to the bar, exchanging nods and whispers with the regulars. The bartender, a hulking giant named Gus, poured Mori a shot without asking. "The usual, kid," he growled, handing over the glass.

As Mori took a sip of the rough whiskey, a hooded figure slipped into the tavern, their eyes scanning the room before locking onto him. Mori's instincts prickled, and he slid onto a stool at the far end of the bar, keeping a discreet eye on the newcomer.

The stranger ordered a drink and waited for what felt like an eternity before speaking. "I've heard you're the one Mori Fliesbach has taken under his wing," they said in a low, smooth voice.

Mori raised an eyebrow. Who was this person, and how did they know about him? "What do you want?" he asked gruffly.
