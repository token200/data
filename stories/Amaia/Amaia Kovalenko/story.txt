Amaia Kovalenko was a 25-year-old Russian-Georgian artist, known for her striking works that reflected the complexities of her cultural heritage. Born in Moscow to a Georgian mother and a Russian father, she had grown up surrounded by the vibrant colors and textures of both traditions.

As a child, Amaia would spend hours helping her mother in their family's small textile shop, weaving traditional Georgian patterns onto handmade cloth. She was fascinated by the way these intricate designs told stories of their ancestors' struggles and triumphs. One day, Amaia received an offer to participate in a group exhibition at a prominent Georgian gallery in Tbilisi. As she prepared for the show, Amaia began to create pieces that drew upon her Georgian roots, incorporating traditional motifs and patterns into her vibrant palette.
