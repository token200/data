As Amaia grew older, her fascination with the sea only deepened. She spent hours poring over books in the town library, devouring stories of mermaids and sea monsters, of pirates and lost treasure. Her parents, though skeptical at first, soon came to realize that their daughter's passion for the ocean was more than just a passing fancy.

Amaia's determination paid off when she won a scholarship to study marine biology at the University of Gran Canaria. She threw herself into her studies, determined to unlock the secrets of the sea. Her findings were published in prestigious journals, and she was hailed as a rising star in her field.

But for Amaia García Márquez, it wasn't just about the accolades or the recognition. It was about using her knowledge to protect the very thing that had captivated her imagination since childhood – the sea itself.
