Mykola Ruiz Garcia was a man with a passion for photography that rivaled his love for life itself. Born in the sun-kissed city of Barcelona, Spain, he spent most of his childhood capturing the vibrant colors and textures of the Mediterranean coastline. As a young boy, Mykola's parents encouraged his creativity, gifting him with an old film camera that he vowed to never let out of his sight.

As Mykola grew older, his fascination with photography only deepened. He spent countless hours studying the works of master photographers, experimenting with different techniques and styles until he developed a unique voice all his own. His photographs became a reflection of his insatiable curiosity about the world around him – a kaleidoscope of emotions, thoughts, and experiences that seemed to dance across the canvas.

Mykola's journey as a photographer took him to distant lands, from the misty mountains of Japan to the dusty deserts of Morocco. Wherever he went, his camera was always by his side, capturing the essence of each place with an artist's touch.
