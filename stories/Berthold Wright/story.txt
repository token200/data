As he made his way back to his office, Berthold's mind turned to the case that had been plaguing him for weeks. A series of high-society burglaries had left the wealthy elite of London shaken, and Berthold was determined to crack the code behind them.

He arrived at his office, a small room above the station, and settled into his chair. The files on the case lay scattered before him, each document a reminder of the countless hours he had spent poring over clues and interviewing witnesses. As he began to sift through the papers, a small piece of paper slipped from one of the files and fluttered to the floor.

Berthold picked it up, his eyes scanning the scribbled notes on the back. Berthold's heart skipped a beat as he recognized the words – they were from one of the missing persons' letters.

Suddenly, the pieces fell into place. He remembered the cryptic message that had been left at each crime scene: "Look to the wind". It was more than just a poetic flourish; it was a key to unlocking the entire case.

Berthold's eyes scanned the room once more, taking in the chaos of papers and files. But his mind was already racing ahead, piecing together the evidence he had collected over weeks of investigation. The game was afoot, and Berthold Wright was ready to take on whatever lay ahead.

As he sat back in his chair, a sense of satisfaction washed over him. This case would be his most challenging yet, but Berthold Wright was confident that he would solve it – for justice and for the people of London's high society who had been robbed of their precious possessions.

With a deep breath, Berthold Wright steeled himself for what lay ahead, knowing that the darkness that lurked beneath the surface of human nature was far from vanquished. But with his years of experience and unyielding determination, he stood ready to face whatever secrets the wind might hold.
