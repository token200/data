Veronica Michielin stepped out of her small apartment and onto the bustling streets of Tokyo. As she walked, the sounds of the city - chatter, horns honking, and the distant wail of sirens - blended together in a symphony that was both overwhelming and exhilarating.

A freelance journalist by trade, Veronica Michielin had made a name for herself in Tokyo's vibrant media scene. Her keen eye for detail and razor-sharp instincts had earned her a reputation as one of the city's top reporters. But despite her success, Veronica felt restless, like there was something just out of reach.

The café was dimly lit, with soft jazz playing in the background and the aroma of freshly brewed coffee wafting through the air. Veronica ordered a cup of coffee and sat down at a small table by the window, watching as people went about their day.

She spent hours poring over maps and reading books on history and culture. Her parents, both academics themselves, encouraged her curiosity, sending her off to study journalism at university.

Years later, as Veronica Michielin sat in that Tokyo café, she felt a sense of continuity with her past self.
