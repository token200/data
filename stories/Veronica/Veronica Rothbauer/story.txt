Veronica Rothbauer was a woman with an unquenchable thirst for adventure and a passion for photography. She had always been drawn to the unknown, whether it was exploring the depths of the ocean or capturing the beauty of the world's most breathtaking landscapes.

Years later, as a successful documentary photographer, Veronica Rothbauer had captured some of the most powerful images of our time. From the refugee crisis in Syria to the devastating effects of climate change, her lens had borne witness to some of humanity's darkest moments.

But despite all she had seen, Veronica remained steadfast in her commitment to telling stories that needed to be told. She knew that her photographs could inspire change, and was determined to use her platform to raise awareness about the issues that mattered most.

Despite having lost her family to conflict, she had grown up to become a skilled farmer, determined to feed her community and create a brighter future for herself.

Veronica Rothbauer was captivated by Aisha's spirit and determination. She knew that this story needed to be told, and so she spent weeks working with Aisha, capturing her voice and her vision.
