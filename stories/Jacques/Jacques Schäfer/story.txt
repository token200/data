As they chatted, Jacques learned that Madame Dupont was an avid photographer herself, and her collection of vintage cameras and lenses caught his eye.

Madame Dupont invited Jacques back to her shop the following day, and over the next few weeks, he spent hours learning about her equipment and techniques. Jacques was fascinated by the intricacies of old photography and saw parallels between Madame Dupont's style and his own work. As the days turned into weeks, Jacques' connection with Madame Dupont deepened. He found himself looking forward to their daily meetings, not just for the photography lessons but also for the chance to share stories and laughter. Through his work with Madame Dupont, Jacques Schäfer discovered a new sense of purpose – not just as a photographer but as a keeper of memories.
