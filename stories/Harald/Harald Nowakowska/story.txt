Harald Nowakowska was known for his exceptional skill as a jazz pianist in Warsaw's underground music scene. By day, he worked as an accountant, crunching numbers for a local firm, but by night, he transformed into a virtuoso on the keys.

Harald's love affair with jazz began when he was a teenager, sneaking into the city's hidden clubs to listen to saxophonist Jerzy Miller perform. As he grew older, Harald's own talent blossomed, and he began playing in local bands and competitions. One evening, Harald was approached by a prominent jazz critic, who asked to review his next performance at the city's iconic Jazz Club. After the show, the critic approached Harald with a proposal: he wanted to feature him on the cover of his magazine, alongside some of the world's top jazz musicians. Harald was both thrilled and intimidated by the offer, but he knew that this was an opportunity he couldn't afford to miss. He accepted, and soon found himself playing alongside some of the biggest names in jazz.

As his star continued to rise, Harald never forgot his humble beginnings as a teenager sneaking into clubs to listen to Jerzy Miller. He remained true to his art, always seeking to push the boundaries of what was possible on the piano.
