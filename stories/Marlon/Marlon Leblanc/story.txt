Marlon Leblanc was a man consumed by his passion for art. A struggling painter in his late twenties, he spent most nights locked away in his cramped studio apartment, lost in a world of colors and textures. His friends had given up on him, whispering among themselves that he'd never make a living off his craft.

But Marlon was undeterred. He poured every penny he saved into canvas, paints, and brushes, determined to create something truly special. And yet, despite his dedication, the studio remained a cold, unforgiving space. Shadows crept in like dark tentacles, threatening to snuff out the flame of inspiration that flickered within him.
