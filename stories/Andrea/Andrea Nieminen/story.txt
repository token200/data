After completing art school, Andrea worked as an assistant illustrator for several small publishers before taking a leap of faith to pursue her own projects full-time. Her inner critic often whispered cruel taunts, urging her to take greater risks and push the boundaries of what was acceptable.

One evening, as Andrea pondered a particularly tricky page layout, her phone rang. It was her best friend and confidant, Sofia, calling from across town. Over the next hour, they discussed everything from their shared love of Japanese horror movies to their plans for a weekend getaway to the coast.

For the first time in weeks, Andrea felt a weight lift from her shoulders. She realized that she didn't have to be defined by her fears; she could use them as fuel for her art.
