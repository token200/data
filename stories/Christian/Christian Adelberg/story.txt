Christian Adelberg stepped into the dimly lit tavern, the bell above the door jingling as he entered. A burly man with a thick beard and worn leather boots, Christian was known for his intimidating presence but gentle nature. As he made his way to the bar, the patrons parted ways, allowing him a clear path.

"What can I get you, friend?" the bartender asked, eyeing Christian's massive frame.

"A whiskey," Christian replied, taking a seat on one of the stools.

As the bartender poured his drink, Christian scanned the room. The fire crackled in the hearth, casting flickering shadows on the walls. A lone violinist sat by the window, her music weaving in and out of the silence like a melancholy waltz.

Christian's gaze lingered on the musician, and for a moment, time seemed to slow down. He remembered a similar tune his late mother used to play on her old piano. It was as if the notes had awakened something deep within him.

"Psst," the bartender called out, snapping Christian back to reality.

"Ah, yes?" he replied, taking a sip of his drink.

"Just wanted to warn you, stranger," the bartender said with a nod towards the violinist. "She's got a bit of a temper."

Christian raised an eyebrow but took another sip. He wasn't one for trouble, and besides, there was something about her music that drew him in.

As he sipped his drink, Christian's eyes wandered back to the musician. She caught his gaze, and their lock was brief but piercing. The musician's cheeks flushed, and she quickly looked away, returning to her instrument.

Christian finished his whiskey and stood up, tossing a coin on the bar. "Thanks for the advice," he said with a smile. He walked towards the window, where the violinist sat shrouded in shadows.

"Excuse me?" Christian asked softly, not wanting to startle her.

She looked up, her eyes welling up with tears. "Can't help it," she whispered, the notes of her music swelled as if in harmony with her emotions.

Without a word, Christian handed over his own violin from his pocket and began to play a gentle melody that complemented the mournful tune. The room fell silent once more, the only sound the soft strumming of strings and the crackling fire.
