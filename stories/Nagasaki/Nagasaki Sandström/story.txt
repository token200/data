Born to a family of skilled sailors and craftsmen, Nagasaki had always shown a natural aptitude for working with wood and sea. Her full name, Nagasaki Sandström, echoed through the bustling docks as she helped her master design and build vessels that would carry fishermen and traders across the treacherous waters.

The vessel was to be one of the largest and most luxurious ever constructed in Ravenshire, with intricate carvings and elaborate furnishings. Nagasaki Sandström was thrilled to take on the challenge, knowing it would be her chance to prove herself as a master shipwright.

She poured all her skill and passion into every detail, from the delicate carvings of sea serpents on the hull to the plush furnishings in the captain's quarters.

Finally, the grand ship was complete, its name "Wychwood's Pride" emblazoned across the prow in bold letters. Nagasaki Sandström beamed with pride as she watched the vessel set sail for the first time, its massive sails billowing in the wind as it vanished into the distance.

Thorne Blackwood, proud of his apprentice's accomplishments, presented her with a beautifully crafted wooden box adorned with intricate carvings of sea creatures. "For Nagasaki Sandström," he said, "the greatest shipwright Ravenshire has ever known."
