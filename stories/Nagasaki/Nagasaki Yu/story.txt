In the sweltering summer of 1960s Tokyo, Nagasaki Yu, a young and talented artist, struggled to find his place in the world. Born to a poor family in a small village on the outskirts of the city, Yu had always been drawn to the vibrant streets of Shinjuku, where he spent most of his days sketching the people and scenery that inspired him.

With a name that reflected his origins, Nagasaki Yu was often mistaken for someone from the western island of Nagasaki, known for its stunning beaches and rich history. But Yu's eyes sparkled with a fire that hinted at a creative soul, one that yearned to express itself through art.

As he honed his craft in secret studios and hidden alleys, Yu began to make a name for himself as a rising star in Tokyo's underground art scene. His bold brushstrokes and vivid colors captured the essence of city life, from the neon lights of Shibuya to the tranquil gardens of the Imperial Palace.
