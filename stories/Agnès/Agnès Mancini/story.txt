Growing up in a family of modest means, she had always been driven to succeed, pouring her heart and soul into every brushstroke. However, Agnès remained fiercely independent, refusing to compromise her artistic vision for the sake of fame or fortune. Despite her success, Agnès's personal life remained shrouded in mystery. Few knew about her troubled childhood, where she had struggled to find her place in the world. Or that she was secretly working on a new series of pieces inspired by the works of her idol, Frida Kahlo. Over coffee, she confided in her friend about her latest struggles with inspiration, feeling stifled by the weight of expectation that came with success.
