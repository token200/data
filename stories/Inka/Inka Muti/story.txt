She was taught how to identify medicinal plants, how to prepare sacred ceremonies, and how to listen to the whispers of the forest.

Inka welcomed Kanaq with open arms, offering her a warm cup of tea made from the sacred plant of the moonflower. As they sat together in silence, Inka listened deeply to Kanaq's sorrow, her eyes filled with compassion and understanding.

Inka's full name, Inka Apu Muti, echoed through the village as word of her remarkable abilities spread far and wide. People began to refer to her as "the wise one," a title that suited her quiet confidence and unwavering dedication to her craft.
