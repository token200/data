Fabian Patel was a man consumed by his art. As a young boy, he spent hours sketching and painting in his small studio above his family's Indian restaurant in London's Brick Lane. As he grew older, Fabian's passion only intensified. He spent every spare moment honing his craft, experimenting with colors and techniques until he had developed his unique style. His paintings were a fusion of Eastern and Western influences, reflecting his Indian heritage and British upbringing.

Despite his dedication, Fabian struggled to make a living from his art.
