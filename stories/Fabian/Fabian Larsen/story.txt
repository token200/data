Fabian Larsen stepped into the dimly lit tavern, the smell of stale beer and smoke wafting up to greet him. As a private investigator, he'd grown accustomed to the seedy underbelly of Copenhagen's nightlife. The patrons cast him suspicious glances, their faces a blur of features that seemed to shift and twist in the flickering candlelight.

Fabian scanned the room, his eyes settling on a figure huddled in the corner, nursing a mug of amber liquid. He nodded to himself, recognizing the telltale signs of desperation etched across the man's face. Larsen made his way over, weaving past clusters of rowdy patrons and sidestepping spilled drinks.

As he approached, the stranger looked up, their eyes locking in a flash of recognition. "Larsen," they spat, their voice low and venomous. "I should've known you'd be the one to show up."

Fabian flashed a wry smile, taking a seat beside them. "What do you mean? You look like someone with problems I can help solve."

The stranger snorted, draining the last of their drink in one gulp. "You think you're so clever, don't you?" They leaned in, their voice dropping to a whisper. "I used to be a journalist. I had it all: the job, the reputation... and then they came after me."

Fabian's interest was piqued. He pulled out his notebook and pen, scribbling down key phrases as the stranger spoke.

"They silenced my sources," the man continued, their eyes welling up with tears. "They took away my freedom, my family... everything I ever knew. And now, they're after me again."

Fabian's gut told him this was more than just a disgruntled ex-reporter with an axe to grind. He sensed a deeper conspiracy, one that went far beyond the petty corruption of local politics. His mind began racing with theories and leads, as he pulled out his phone to dial a number.

"Who are you going to call?" The stranger asked, their voice laced with desperation.

Fabian hesitated for a moment before responding, "Someone who owes me a favor."
