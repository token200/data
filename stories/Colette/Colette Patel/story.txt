Colette Patel stood at the edge of the bustling market, her eyes scanning the vibrant colors and lively chatter of the crowd. As an anthropologist, she had spent years studying the intricate customs and traditions of this small South Asian community, but nothing quite captured her imagination like the people themselves.

With a quick smile, Colette weaved through the stalls, greeting vendors and shoppers alike with a warm "Namaste." Her dark hair was tied back in a neat ponytail, revealing a smattering of freckles across her cheeks. She wore a crisp white shirt and worn jeans, a testament to her no-nonsense approach to fieldwork.

As she made her way through the market, Colette's mind turned to her latest research project: a comprehensive analysis of the local wedding customs. In particular, she was fascinated by the intricate rituals surrounding the bride's preparations, from the henna application to the final blessings from the priest.
