As an adult, Da Vinci traveled extensively throughout Europe, collecting knowledge and ideas like a gatherer collects seeds. He spent hours pouring over ancient texts in dusty libraries, seeking out hidden patterns and codes that others had overlooked.

Despite his fame as an artist, it was Da Vinci's work in the field of mathematics that truly set him apart. His treatises on geometry and perspective were considered revolutionary by his contemporaries, and scholars would later study them for centuries to come.
