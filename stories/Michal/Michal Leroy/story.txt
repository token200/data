Michal Leroy stepped into the dimly lit café, scanning the room for a familiar face. As a freelance journalist, she had spent countless hours in this very spot, sipping coffee and conducting interviews with locals. Tonight was no different.

Leroy's ears perked up at the mention of concrete evidence. She had been searching for this for months, but nothing seemed to pan out. This could be the break she needed.

As they talked, the hours slipped away, lost in a haze of caffeine-fueled conversation. Leroy pulled out her notebook and began scribbling down notes, her fingers flying across the page.

Marcus leaned back in his chair, a hint of a smile playing on his lips. "You're a formidable journalist, Michal Leroy," he said, his voice laced with admiration. "I have no doubt you'll uncover the truth."
