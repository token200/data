Levente Poirier spent his days conjuring up magical creations in his kitchen, filling the air with the sweet scent of sugar and flour. The townspeople adored him for it, and children would often gather outside his bakery, eagerly awaiting the daily treats that Levente Poirier would produce.

Despite his warm demeanor, there was something about Levente Poirier that made people feel like they were sharing a confidence when speaking to him. His listening skills were uncanny, and he possessed an unnerving ability to remember every single detail of every conversation.

Levente Poirier, however, listened attentively as Sophie recounted her tale. He nodded sympathetically and produced from his apron pocket a small notebook filled with cryptic notes and sketches. With each stroke of his pen, the darkness seemed to grow more tangible.

Levente Poirier closed his notebook with a firm thud, ushering Sophie back into the safety of the night. As she departed, he smiled once more – this time at her, rather than at anyone else. His secrets remained locked away within those ancient pages, guarded by Levente Poirier's unwavering resolve to protect Saint-Pierre from the shadows that lurked beyond its borders.
