In the scorching summer of 1922, Pablo D'Annunzio, the enigmatic and charismatic Italian fascist leader, stood atop a hill overlooking the Adriatic Sea. His piercing gaze seemed to bore into the souls of those who dared to oppose him, as if daring them to challenge his vision for a new Italy.

And now, with the support of Benito Mussolini, he was poised to take on the world.
