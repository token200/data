Eduard Wei, or Lee Wei as he was known to his friends, had always been fascinated by the ancient art of calligraphy. Growing up in a small village in rural China, he spent countless hours practicing the intricacies of Chinese characters on silk scrolls and paper. His love for calligraphy eventually led him to study at one of China's most prestigious art academies.

As Eduard Wei delved deeper into his craft, he began to notice a peculiar connection between the brushstrokes used in traditional Chinese calligraphy and the rhythms of Chinese poetry. He became obsessed with capturing the essence of these poems on paper, using bold and expressive strokes that seemed to pulse with life.
