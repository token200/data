Upon arrival at the museum, Fujita Kaczmarek began surveying the scene. He noticed a faint scent of lavender in the air, which piqued his interest. It was an unusual choice for a crime scene, as most thieves tended towards more traditional scents like sweat or gasoline.

Fujita Kaczmarek's eyes scanned the room, taking in every detail. That's when he spotted it – a small, almost imperceptible thread of fabric caught in the display case's locking mechanism.
