Line Meier trudged through the rain-soaked streets, her umbrella blown inside out by a gust of wind. She was running late for her shift at the local bookstore, and the last thing she needed was to get caught in a downpour.

As she splashed her way through puddles, Line's mind wandered back to the stack of books she had purchased on her lunch break yesterday. She had finally found a rare edition of Gabriel Garcia Marquez's "One Hundred Years of Solitude" that she had been searching for months. The thrill of the hunt still lingered in her veins, and she couldn't wait to dive into its yellowed pages.

"Ah, Line! You're just in time for the first book club meeting," Mrs. Patel said with a warm smile. "We've got a lively discussion planned on our latest selection."
