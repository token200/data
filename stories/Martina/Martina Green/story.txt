Despite her reserved nature, Martina's sharp wit and quick tongue made her a favorite among the townspeople. They would often gather at the store on Friday evenings for book clubs, where Martina would regale them with tales of the classics and share her own insights on the latest releases.

"Can I help you find anything?" she asked, her voice gentle but firm.

"What's your name?" Martina asked softly, as she pulled out a tissue from her apron pocket.

"My name is Sophia," the girl replied, her voice barely above a whisper.
