One day, while wandering through the streets of Tokyo, Martina Naoko stumbled upon a small, quirky art studio that caught her eye. The sign above the door read "Kokoro," which means "heart" in Japanese, and Martina Naoko felt an instant connection to the place.

As the days turned into weeks, Martina Naoko began to feel a sense of belonging in the studio. She had found a new family among Yumi and the other artists who shared her passion for creativity. And when she finally presented her first solo exhibition at Kokoro, it was clear that Martina Naoko had truly found her place in the world.
