His only appearance in public was at literary festivals and book signings, where he would sit stoically behind his table, his eyes fixed intently on some invisible point beyond the crowd.

But those who had met Martín swore that he possessed an otherworldly intensity. In the end, it was Martín Nagy's writing that spoke for him, revealing the depths of his own mysterious soul to an unsuspecting world.
