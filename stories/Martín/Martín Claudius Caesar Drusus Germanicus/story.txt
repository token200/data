Despite facing numerous challenges, Drusus proved himself to be a skilled military commander and strategist.

His most notable campaign was the conquest of Germania Superior, where he successfully subdued the region and expanded Rome's borders. His bravery and cunning earned him great respect among his comrades and superiors alike.

His wife, Aurelia Lepida, placed a gentle hand on his arm. "You did what was best for Rome, my love," she whispered. "Your legacy will live on through your sons."

Indeed, Drusus had a son named Tiberius, who would go on to become one of Rome's greatest emperors. And although he never lived to see it, Drusus took pride in knowing that his son would carry on his family's proud tradition.

The night wore on, and Martín's tales of conquests were told and retold, filling the room with laughter and camaraderie. As the fire crackled and spat, Drusus smiled, feeling a sense of satisfaction and contentment wash over him. He knew that he had left his mark on history, even if it was not in the classical sense of victory or glory.
