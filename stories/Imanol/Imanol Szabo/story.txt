He spent countless hours observing the works of Pollock and Kandinsky, mesmerized by their bold brushstrokes and vibrant colors. This exposure would shape his unique style, one that blended gestural marks with subtle nuances of color.

As he grew older, Imanol's fascination with art only deepened, leading him to study at the prestigious Academy of Fine Arts in Budapest. There, he honed his craft under the guidance of esteemed instructors, who encouraged his experimentation and innovation.

After completing his studies, Imanol embarked on a journey that would take him to some of Europe's most vibrant cities.
