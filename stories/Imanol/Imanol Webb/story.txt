Imanol Webb stepped off the train and onto the platform, her bright yellow sundress a stark contrast to the drizzly London morning. She had just arrived in town for a three-day writing retreat at a quaint country estate nestled in the rolling hills of the countryside.

As she made her way through the station, Imanol's mind began to wander back to the words that had been swirling inside her for months. She was a romance author, known for her swoon-worthy heroes and feisty heroines, but lately, she'd been struggling to come up with anything new.

The retreat promised solitude and creative freedom, just what Imanol needed to get her writing juices flowing again. She had big plans to tackle her latest manuscript and turn out a bestseller.

As she arrived at the estate, Imanol was greeted by the warm smile of the hostess, Mrs. Jenkins. "Welcome, dear! You must be exhausted from your journey." Imanol nodded gratefully as she was whisked away to her cozy room, where a steaming cup of tea and a plate of fresh-baked scones awaited her.

Mrs. Jenkins appeared at her side like a gentle guardian angel, offering words of encouragement and a fresh set of pages for Imanol to start anew. "You're a writer, dear," Mrs. Jenkins said with a smile. "Writers write even when the ink runs dry."
