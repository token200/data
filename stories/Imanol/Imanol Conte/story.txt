A move to Real Sociedad's first team would prove to be Imanol's baptism by fire. The pressure was intense, but he thrived under the spotlight. With each passing match, his reputation grew, and soon he found himself wearing the iconic red and white jersey of La Real.

Injuries took their toll on Imanol's career, but he refused to let them define him. He pushed through pain and fatigue, driven by a fierce determination to succeed. His teammates would often marvel at his resilience, as he would rise from the ashes like a phoenix, ready to take on the next challenge.

Imanol's crowning moment came in the 2015 Champions League semifinals against Barcelona. Trailing 1-0 at halftime, Imanol conjured up a masterclass performance, scoring two goals and creating several more as La Real stormed back to draw level. The comeback would be remembered for generations to come.

Though his playing days were eventually cut short by a lingering knee injury, Imanol Conte's legacy lives on in the hearts of Spanish football fans.
