Her colleagues back at the university had told her to keep an open mind, that the stories of the local tribe were shrouded in myth and legend.

Lio's thoughts turned to her research partner, Dr. Maria Rodriguez, who had left for a research trip in the Amazon just days before. Lio had stayed behind to explore the region herself, armed with nothing but a battered backpack and a determination to uncover the truth. She had always been drawn to the stories of the indigenous peoples, their struggles against colonization and assimilation.

As she made her way through the station, Lio noticed a group of locals eyeing her from across the room. They were dressed in worn jeans and flannel shirts, their faces lined with the weight of years spent working hard on the land. She approached them hesitantly, unsure if they would welcome her presence.

"Hi," she said finally, her voice barely above a whisper. "I'm Lio Taylor, an anthropologist from... outside."
