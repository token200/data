Despite his formidable reputation, Lio's closest allies feared him not because he was ruthless or cunning, but because they knew that beneath his charming facade lay a razor-sharp intellect and an unyielding determination. As the clock ticked down, Lio's nerves were stretched to breaking point. But he remained focused, pouring all his energy into the task at hand. And when the dust finally settled, Lio Valerius Aurelius Constantinus stood triumphant, the newly-minted Emperor of Rome perched upon his shoulders.
