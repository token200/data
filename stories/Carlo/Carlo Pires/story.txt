As a child, Carlo had dreamed of leaving Portugal behind, chasing the sun-kissed beaches and cosmopolitan thrills of the world beyond. But as the decades passed, he found himself disillusioned with the fast-paced life of his adopted cities, the endless noise and chaos that suffocated him.

Back in Lisbon, the air was fresh and clean, the streets were narrow and winding, and Carlo felt a sense of belonging he had long forgotten. He wandered through the alleys, passing by the old markets and cafes where he used to spend hours as a teenager, laughing with friends and listening to Fado music.

Carlo's thoughts drifted back to his late father, who had always told him that Lisbon was a city that could heal your soul. His mother, too, had said the same, speaking of the way the old stones seemed to hold secrets and stories from another time.
