As a child, Bekzat would spend hours watching traditional Uzbek artisans at work, mesmerized by their skill and craftsmanship. His parents, though not artists themselves, encouraged his creative pursuits, recognizing the joy it brought him. Bekzat's parents, Amira and Jamolodin Laurent, were proud of their son's determination and creativity.

When the mural was finished, the people of Tashkent gathered to celebrate its unveiling. Bekzat Laurent, beaming with pride, watched as the crowd erupted in applause, recognizing the beauty and emotion he had poured into his work.
