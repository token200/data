Flavius Deschamps, a master thief and con artist, sat sipping his tea in the dimly lit tavern. His piercing green eyes scanned the room, taking in every patron, every conversation, every whispered secret. It was a skill honed over years of evading detection and pulling off daring heists.

A hooded figure slid onto the stool beside him, their face obscured by shadows. "Deschamps, I have a job for you," they said, their voice low and gravelly.

Flavius raised an eyebrow, intrigued. "What kind of score?"

Flavius took the box, feeling its weight in his hand. He opened it, revealing a glittering diamond necklace. His eyes sparkled with excitement.

"Ah, now we're talking," he said, a sly grin spreading across his face.

The figure handed him a mask and a small pouch containing a few gold coins. "Meet me at the old windmill on the outskirts of town at midnight. Be prepared to make off with something far more valuable than that necklace."

Flavius nodded, already planning his approach. He finished his tea, tucked the box into his cloak, and made his way out of the tavern.
