Despite his introspective nature, Guillermo Liepiņš had an uncanny ability to connect with people from all walks of life. He'd strike up conversations with fellow street artists, beggars, or even the occasional lost tourist, effortlessly extracting their stories and weaving them into his photographs.

One rainy evening, as he was setting up his camera in front of a dilapidated church, Guillermo Liepiņš met an elderly woman named Anna. She introduced herself as a former resident of Riga during World War II and shared tales of the city's tumultuous past with him.

Enchanted by her words, Guillermo Liepiņš spent the next few hours capturing Anna's story through his lens. The resulting photographs told a poignant narrative of resilience and survival against all odds.

As the rain subsided and the sun dipped below the horizon, Guillermo Liepiņš packed up his gear and bid farewell to Anna. His mind was already racing with ideas for his next project – one that would continue to capture the essence of this captivating city and its people, just as he had promised to honor her story.

Guillermo Liepiņš disappeared into the night, camera in hand, leaving behind a trail of memories and images that would forever change the way Riga was seen by outsiders.
