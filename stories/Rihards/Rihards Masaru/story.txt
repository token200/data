His passion for art only grew stronger as he matured, leading him to pursue a degree in fine arts from Tokyo's prestigious Gakushuin University.

After graduating, Rihards devoted himself to honing his craft, working tirelessly in his small studio nestled between a ramen shop and a used bookstore. One day, Rihards received an offer from a prominent gallery in Kyoto to showcase his work. Eager for validation, he packed his bags and set off on the journey to the ancient city. Upon arrival, he was struck by the tranquil atmosphere that enveloped him – the soft rustle of koi fish in the canal, the sweet scent of incense wafting from a nearby shrine.

The exhibition was a resounding success, with art critics and collectors alike praising Rihards Masaru's unique vision. For the first time in his life, he felt like his work was being truly understood – not just as an artist, but as himself. And though the accolades brought a sense of validation, Rihards knew that true fulfillment lay not in external recognition, but in continuing to create from the heart.

As he walked through Kyoto's narrow streets, Rihards found solace in the timeless beauty of this ancient city.
