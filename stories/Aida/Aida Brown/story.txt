Over the years, Aida had become a fixture on Seattle's social scene, using her platform to bring attention to pressing issues like homelessness and racial injustice.

Despite the challenges she faced, Aida remained unwavering in her commitment to justice. She was the founder of The Trevor Project, a nonprofit organization dedicated to providing crisis intervention and suicide prevention services to LGBTQ+ youth. Her work had not gone unnoticed, earning her numerous accolades, including the prestigious Lambda Literary Award.

One evening, as Aida sat in her living room surrounded by stacks of papers and activist literature, she felt a sense of pride and satisfaction wash over her.
