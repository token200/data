His father, Marcus Aurelius, had been a just and fair ruler, beloved by his people and respected by his peers. But Aet Aurelius Antoninus Augustus knew that he could never quite live up to his legacy.

But Aet Aurelius Antoninus Augustus was resolute. He knew that his time was drawing to a close, and that it was imperative that he leave Rome in better condition than when he arrived. He spent hours reviewing the accounts of his reign, poring over documents and meeting with advisors, searching for any areas where he could improve.

Despite his efforts, however, Aet Aurelius Antoninus Augustus couldn't shake the feeling that his time was almost up. The stars seemed to be aligning in a most ominous way, and the winds were whispering secrets in his ear. He knew that soon, very soon, the gods would demand their due.
