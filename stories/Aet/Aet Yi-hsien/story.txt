News of Aet Yi-hsien's exceptional skills spread quickly, drawing attention from rival clans and powerful warlords seeking to exploit his talents. One such individual, the ruthless Lord Wu, set his sights on capturing the young monk and forcing him to fight in the arena.

Undaunted by the prospect of battle, Aet Yi-hsien accepted Lord Wu's challenge. As he entered the crowded stadium, a hush fell over the crowd, sensing the presence of something extraordinary. The clash of steel on steel echoed through the air as Lord Wu's soldiers charged forward. Aet Yi-hsien's fists moved in slow motion, weaving an intricate pattern of strikes and parries that left his opponents bewildered and defeated. The crowd gasped as one man was no longer enough to hold back the mighty warrior.

As he bowed to the audience, a smile spread across his face, illuminating the darkness of the arena. From that day forward, Aet Yi-hsien's legend grew, drawing in those seeking protection from the forces of darkness. Though his full name would forever be etched in history as "Gentle Moonlight," few knew that beneath the serene facade lay a heart forged in fire and tempered by steel.
