Úrsula García Fernández was a woman who defied convention in every way. Born into a small village in rural Spain, she was raised by her fiercely independent mother and grandmother, who taught her the value of hard work and determination. From a young age, Úrsula showed a keen interest in engineering and mechanics, often sneaking into her father's garage to tinker with his old cars.

Despite her family's wishes for her to pursue a traditional career as a teacher or nurse, Úrsula's passions lay elsewhere. She spent countless hours studying and experimenting, earning a degree in mechanical engineering and eventually landing a job at a prestigious automotive firm.

It wasn't long before Úrsula made a name for herself in the industry, developing innovative solutions for sustainable energy and transportation systems. Her work caught the attention of a prominent international organization, which offered her a coveted position as a lead engineer on a high-profile project.

As she traveled the world, working alongside brilliant minds from diverse backgrounds, Úrsula proved herself to be a trailblazer in more ways than one. She was a woman of color in a male-dominated field, and her success inspired countless young women to pursue careers in STEM.

But Úrsula's true passion lay elsewhere – in restoring old cars and sharing them with the world. In her spare time, she would spend hours scouring flea markets and antique shops for rare and vintage vehicles, meticulously restoring each one to its former glory. She poured all her energy into the project, researching, designing, and building every aspect of the car from scratch.

The collector was overjoyed, and word of Úrsula's incredible talent spread even further. As she looked out at the finished Mini Cooper, Úrsula smiled, knowing that she had truly found her calling – combining her passions for engineering, restoration, and making a difference in the world.
