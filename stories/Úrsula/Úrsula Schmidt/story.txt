Úrsula was both fascinated and skeptical, but she couldn't deny the allure of discovery that drove her forward.

Her parents had encouraged her curiosity, sending her on field trips to the local botanical gardens and encouraging her to explore the natural world.

Úrsula's academic prowess had earned her a place at the prestigious University of Berlin, where she had studied under some of the most esteemed botanists of her time. After completing her Ph.D., Úrsula had set out on her own, supported by grants and funding from various organizations. Her reputation as a leading expert in plant taxonomy had spread far and wide, earning her invitations to conferences and expeditions to remote corners of the globe.

The two women had met on one of Úrsula's early expeditions, bonding over their shared love of botany and adventure.
