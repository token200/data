Born and raised in Stockholm, Sweden, Ebba had always been fascinated by the stars and planets that twinkled above her city skyline as a child. As she grew older, her passion only intensified, leading her to pursue a career in astrophysics.

After completing her Ph.D., Ebba landed a prestigious research position at the Max Planck Institute in Berlin, Germany. There, she worked alongside some of the most talented minds in the field, delving into the intricacies of black holes and dark matter. Her groundbreaking work on the subject earned her international recognition, and soon she was invited to present her findings at conferences around the world.

Despite her success, Ebba remained humble and kind-hearted. Her warmth and intelligence made her a beloved figure in the academic community, and her peers looked up to her as a role model.

One evening, while working late in her laboratory, Ebba received an unexpected phone call from an 11-year-old boy who had written to her about his love for astronomy. The young boy, Max, was struggling to understand the concept of dark matter, and Ebba took it upon herself to explain it to him in a way that made sense to him. As they chatted over the phone, she realized that this chance encounter might just be the start of something special.

Months later, Ebba received an email from Max, who had been inspired by their conversation to pursue a career in science himself. Overjoyed and proud of her protégé, Ebba knew that her work was not only advancing human knowledge but also nurturing the next generation of scientists and thinkers. And as she looked up at the stars twinkling above her laboratory, she smiled, knowing that this chance encounter had been just the beginning of a new chapter in her own journey as Ebba Yang, astronomer extraordinaire.
