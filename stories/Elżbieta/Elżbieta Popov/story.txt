Elżbieta's eyes locked onto the photograph of Szymon, his smile plastered on his face like a mask. She knew that he was not to be trusted, and her mission was to eliminate him before he could do any more damage.

As she set out for the designated location, Elżbieta couldn't shake off the feeling that something was off. The Director had been vague about Szymon's true intentions, and Elżbieta knew that she needed to be on high alert at all times.

She arrived at the luxury hotel where Szymon was staying, her eyes scanning the surroundings for any sign of danger. She spotted a group of bodyguards lingering around the entrance, their faces expressionless as they eyed the crowd.

Without hesitation, Elżbieta sprang into action, weaving through the guests and making her way to Szymon's table. Her martini glass shattered on the floor as she drew her pistol, the sound echoing through the room like a death knell.

Elżbieta's bullet sailed towards Szymon's chest, but it seemed to slow in mid-air, like time itself had come to a standstill. For one moment, the two enemies locked eyes, their fates hanging precariously in the balance.

And then, everything went black.

The Director received the news that Elżbieta had been eliminated, and his face remained impassive as he watched her body being carried out of the hotel. But deep down, a hint of sadness flickered to life – for he knew that Nightshade was not just any operative; she was the best thing Poland had ever produced.

As the Director disappeared into the shadows, Elżbieta's legacy lived on, etched in the memories of those who had crossed her path. She may have been a ghost, but her presence would never be forgotten.
