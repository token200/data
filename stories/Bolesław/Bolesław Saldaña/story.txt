His parents, owners of the antique shop, encouraged his interest in history, often taking him on long walks through the narrow streets and alleys to show him the remnants of Krakow's rich past.

Years later, Bolesław Saldaña had honed his craft, producing breathtaking paintings that seemed to capture the very essence of the city. As she gazed deeper into one painting in particular, she felt as though she had entered the very heart of Krakow itself.

She began to visit his studio above the antique shop, hoping to catch a glimpse of Bolesław at work or simply strike up a conversation. Eventually, her persistence paid off, and she found herself sitting in the studio with Bolesław Saldaña himself.

Though introverted and taciturn, Bolesław revealed glimpses of his passion for history and art, sharing with Katarzyna the stories that inspired some of his most famous works.
