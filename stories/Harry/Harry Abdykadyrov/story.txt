Harry Abdykadyrov stepped out of the Moscow metro and onto the chilly winter air, his scarf wrapped tightly around his neck. As he emerged from the station, he was immediately struck by the stark beauty of Red Square. The ice rink glinted like a frozen lake, and the imposing walls of the Kremlin rose above the rooftops.

Harry was no stranger to Moscow's grandeur. Born into a family of diplomats, he had grown up moving between embassies and consulates around the world. But after completing his studies at Moscow State University, he had returned home to explore the city that had been his childhood playground.

As he made his way across the square, Harry noticed a small, unassuming shop tucked away between a souvenir stall and a street food vendor. The sign above the door read "Old Books and Rare Finds." Intrigued, Harry pushed open the door and stepped inside.

The shop was dimly lit, with shelves upon shelves of dusty tomes that seemed to stretch on forever. Harry wandered down the rows, running his fingers over the spines of the books, taking in the titles and authors. He felt a sense of nostalgia wash over him as he recognized some of the works of Pushkin and Dostoevsky.

The shopkeeper, an elderly woman with piercing green eyes, looked up from behind the counter as Harry approached. "Welcome to my humble abode," she said, her voice warm and raspy. "I see you have a taste for literature."

Harry smiled, feeling a sense of connection to this stranger. "Yes, I do," he replied, his English slightly accented. "I'm Harry Abdykadyrov."
