As he grew older, Harry's love for creativity only intensified, leading him to study fine arts at the prestigious Mohács University.

After graduating, Harry moved to Vienna, Austria, where he became fast friends with a fellow artist named Sándor Lendvai. Together, they formed a band that blended traditional Hungarian folk music with modern electronic elements, creating a unique sound that quickly gained popularity among the city's underground music scene.

As Harry Tóth's career began to take off, he found himself performing at some of Europe's most prestigious venues. One night, after a particularly grueling tour schedule, Harry returned to Budapest, where he was greeted with open arms by his friends and family. He spent several weeks exploring the city, reconnecting with his roots and finding new sources of inspiration. It was during this time that he began working on a new project – an album that blended traditional Hungarian folk music with electronic elements and avant-garde soundscapes.

As Harry Tóth delved deeper into the creative process, he found himself drawn to the works of his idol, György Ligeti, a renowned Hungarian composer known for his innovative and boundary-pushing compositions. The two shared a deep appreciation for the intersection of art and technology, and their influence can be heard throughout Harry's music.

The resulting album, titled "Ágak és Árkok" (Ropes and Walls), was met with critical acclaim upon its release.
