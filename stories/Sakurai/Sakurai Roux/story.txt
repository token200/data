Roux's eyes narrowed as she scrutinized the current page. She wanted to get it just right, but every change seemed to throw off the entire flow of the story. Her editor, Taro, would be breathing down her neck soon, expecting a finished manuscript in record time.

Just then, there was a knock at the door. Roux set aside her pencil and got up to answer it. It was her best friend and fellow artist, Shinji.

"Hey, Rix!" Shinji said, plopping down into the chair across from Roux's desk. "How's it going?"

Shinji chuckled and peered over at the manuscript. "Let me see." He flipped through the pages, nodding thoughtfully as he read.

"See any inspiration?" Roux asked, her eyes fixed on Shinji's face.

He nodded, a small smile playing on his lips. "I think I do."

Shinji got up and walked over to Roux's sketchbook, where she had left a few scraps of paper with her character designs. He flipped through them quickly, stopping at a particular illustration.

"This one," he said, holding out the page. "This is it. This is what you need."

Roux took the page from Shinji and studied it intently. As she looked at the character, an idea began to form in her mind.

"Okay," she said finally. "I think I know what to do now."

With renewed energy, Roux got back to work, the characters and story flowing from her pencil with a newfound ease. The hours flew by as she worked tirelessly, fueled by Shinji's encouragement and her own growing excitement for the project.
