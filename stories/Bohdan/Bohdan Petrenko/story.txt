However, after years of serving the KGB, he became disillusioned with their tactics and eventually defected to the West. Despite his tough exterior, Bohdan had a soft spot for those in need. One day, Bohdan received a call from an anonymous source claiming to have evidence of a major conspiracy involving high-ranking government officials. Intrigued, he agreed to meet with the informant, who revealed himself to be a former colleague from Bohdan's KGB days.

Bohdan listened intently, his eyes narrowing as he pieced together the evidence. He knew that taking on such a case would put him in grave danger, but he couldn't just walk away. As he left the café, he felt a sense of purpose wash over him – Bohdan Petrovich Petrenko was back on the job, and no one was going to stop him.
