His love for art and numbers was evident from an early age, and he spent most of his childhood experimenting with colors and shapes.

As Oliver Krūmiņš grew older, his passion for painting only intensified. He attended art school in Riga, where he honed his skills under the guidance of a renowned artist. One fateful day, Oliver Krūmiņš received an invitation to exhibit his paintings at a prominent gallery in Paris. Eager to showcase his work, he packed his bags and set off for the City of Light. Upon arriving at the gallery, Oliver Krūmiņš was struck by the sleek modernity of the space. His paintings, with their bold colors and abstract shapes, seemed out of place amidst the minimalist chic. However, as art lovers began to trickle in, they were won over by the beauty and complexity of his work.

As the night wore on, Oliver Krūmiņš found himself surrounded by admirers, all eager to discuss the intricate patterns and mathematical principles that lay beneath his art. He smiled humbly, knowing that he had stumbled upon a hidden language that spoke directly to the soul.
