Rumors swirled about Rein's past, with some claiming he was a former sailor who had lost everything at sea, while others whispered that he was the son of a wealthy industrialist. But one thing was certain: Rein was haunted by his demons, and his creations seemed to be the only solace he found in this quiet world.

One evening, as the sun dipped below the horizon, casting a warm orange glow over Ravenswood, Rein stepped into his workshop, his eyes fixed on the half-finished chair in front of him. He had been working on it for weeks, but tonight, something felt different. The wood seemed to be calling to him, urging him to finish the piece.

As he worked, the room grew darker, and the shadows cast by the flickering candles seemed to come alive. Rein's hands moved with a precision that bordered on reverence, as if he were channeling some ancient power into the wood. His mind wandered back to his childhood, spent playing among the trees of his family's forest, where the creatures whispered secrets in his ear.

Just then, the door creaked open, and a young woman stepped inside, her eyes scanning the room until they landed on Rein. For an instant, their gazes locked, and Rein felt a jolt of electricity run through him. He knew not what to make of this stranger, but something about her drew him in.

As she approached, Rein Henrik Sandström couldn't help but wonder if he had been alone for too long. Perhaps, in sharing his creations with others, he would find the connection he so desperately sought – a connection that would set him free from the shadows that haunted him.
