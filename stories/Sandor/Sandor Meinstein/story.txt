"Sandor Meinstein," they said, their voice still rough as the whiskey, "I've been looking for you."

Sandor raised an eyebrow, intrigued despite himself. Who was this person, and what did they want with him? He wiped his hands on a towel and walked over to the hooded figure, his movements deliberate.

"I'm afraid I'm not interested in anything," he said, trying to sound nonchalant.

The patron chuckled, a dry, mirthless sound. "I didn't think you'd be," they said. "You see, Sandor Meinstein, I have information about your past. Information that could change everything."
