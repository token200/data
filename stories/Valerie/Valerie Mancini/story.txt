The bartender raised an eyebrow, knowing Valerie's reputation for being fiercely independent and always getting to the bottom of things. As he poured her drink, Valerie's gaze drifted to the corner table, where a figure she recognized sat nursing a cup of coffee.

It was Marco, an old friend from her past who had vanished without a word five years ago. Valerie Mancini had assumed the worst - that Marco had been caught up in something shady and had skipped town to avoid trouble. But now, as she watched him fidget with his napkin, she wondered if there was more to it than that.

As she waited for Marco to notice her, Valerie Mancini sipped her drink and surveyed the room. The café was quiet, with only a handful of patrons scattered about. She spotted a few familiar faces - a couple on their first date, a group of rowdy college students - but none of them seemed out of place.

Finally, Marco caught her eye and nodded in her direction. Valerie Mancini smiled, making her way over to the table. "Marco, what are you doing here?" she asked, sliding into the chair across from him.

Marco leaned back in his seat, looking worn but not scared. "I'm trying to make amends," he said, his voice low and measured. "Valerie Mancini, I know I hurt you when we parted ways. But I've been working hard to turn my life around."
