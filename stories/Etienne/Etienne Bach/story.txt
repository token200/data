As Echoes in the Attic gained momentum, Etienne's compositions became increasingly experimental. He'd often incorporate found sounds – snippets of old film reels, creaking wooden floorboards, or whispers from forgotten records – into his music, recontextualizing them for a modern audience.

One fateful night, Etienne took to the stage at a cramped underground club, armed with nothing but his trusty guitar and a handful of loops pedals. In that moment, it was clear why Etienne Pierre Marcel Bach was one of the most exciting emerging talents in music. His innovative spirit and unwavering commitment to his art had earned him a devoted following, one that eagerly anticipated each new release from Echoes in the Attic.
