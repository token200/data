As he grew older, Mili became increasingly disenchanted with the corruption and inequality that plagued Rome's upper echelons. He knew that the only way to effect change was through hard work and dedication, and so he devoted himself to studying law, philosophy, and politics. His intellectual pursuits took him to the great libraries of Athens, where he pored over the works of Aristotle and Plato.

Upon his return to Rome, Mili threw himself into public service, advocating for reforms that would benefit the common people. Despite facing fierce opposition from powerful interests, Mili remained steadfast in his commitment to justice and equality.
