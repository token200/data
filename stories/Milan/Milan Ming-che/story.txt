One day, Milan Ming-che received a call from a local activist group who were fighting against a proposed development project that would displace hundreds of low-income families. Milan was intrigued by the case and agreed to take on the role of lead attorney for the activist group. She spent countless hours pouring over documents and conducting interviews with affected residents, as well as meeting with local officials to discuss the potential impacts of the project.

As Milan delved deeper into the case, she realized that the development company had a history of exploiting marginalized communities for their own gain. The project would not only displace families but also destroy community centers, schools, and other essential services that relied on those properties.

Armed with this new information, Milan Ming-che presented her findings to the court, arguing that the development project was an affront to public policy and an example of corporate greed. She rallied support from local residents, activists, and community organizations, who were equally outraged by the proposal.
