Bruno Smith was a man with a penchant for the unusual. He lived in a small, cluttered shop on the outskirts of town, filled to bursting with oddities and trinkets he'd collected over the years. There were vintage taxidermy animals perched on shelves, antique door knobs that opened onto nothing, and a VHS player that still worked despite being decades past its prime.

Bruno spent his days tinkering with his latest project: a wooden robot named Zeta. It was a mess of gears, wires, and mismatched limbs, but Bruno saw beauty in its chaos. He'd lost count of how many failed attempts he'd made to bring Zeta to life, but he refused to give up.

One day, as Bruno was hunched over his workbench, surrounded by scraps of metal and discarded dreams, a young girl wandered into the shop. She looked lost and scared, clutching a small backpack that seemed too big for her frame.

"Can I help you?" Bruno asked gruffly, not looking up from his work.
