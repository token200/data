Ines Põld was a young and talented Estonian singer-songwriter who had been making waves in the music scene with her unique blend of folk and electronic elements. With her striking features and captivating stage presence, she had quickly gained a loyal following among fans of indie music.

Born on a chilly winter morning in Tallinn, Estonia, Ines Põld grew up surrounded by the city's rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. Her love for music was inspired by her parents, who were both musicians themselves. As a child, she would spend hours listening to traditional Estonian folk songs and experimenting with her own melodies.

As she entered adulthood, Ines Põld began to develop her signature sound, which combined the introspective lyrics of indie folk with the soaring melodies of electronic music. Her debut album, "Kael" (which means "stone" in Estonian), was a critical and commercial success, earning her a spot on the Estonia's biggest music festival circuit.

One summer evening, Ines Põld took to the stage at the Tallinn Music Week, her voice soaring as she sang of love, loss, and resilience.
