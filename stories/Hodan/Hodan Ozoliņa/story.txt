Her best friend and colleague, Maria, had been killed in a tragic accident that she believed was orchestrated by the very same people she had once worked for. The loss had left Hodan feeling lost and alone, and she knew she couldn't stay hidden forever.

Hodan's heart skipped a beat as she pondered the risk and reward. She knew it was a long shot, but something about the message resonated with her. It was a call to action, a chance to take back control and fight for justice once more.

With a deep breath, Hodan made up her mind. At midnight, she would be at the old clock tower, ready to face whatever lay ahead. She turned to leave, her eyes never leaving the city below, as if willing herself to conquer the darkness that had consumed so much of her life.

For a moment, Hodan Ozoliņa felt a glimmer of hope, a sense that she was not alone in this fight. She knew that she would have to be brave, clever, and resourceful if she was to succeed. But as she walked away from the rooftop, she couldn't shake the feeling that she was being watched, that unblinking eyes were trained on her from the shadows.
