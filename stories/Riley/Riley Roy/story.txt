She spent most of her days lost in the vibrant streets of New Orleans, sketchbook in hand, searching for inspiration. With a wild mane of curly brown hair and a mischievous grin, Riley Anne Roy was always up for an adventure.

The sign above the door read "La Belle Époque," and the aroma of freshly brewed coffee wafted out into the street, enticing her to come inside. Riley Anne Roy pushed open the door and was immediately enveloped in the warm, cozy atmosphere.

As the afternoon wore on, Riley Anne Roy found herself lost in conversation with Madame LeRoux, discussing art, life, and the beauty of the world around them. As she left La Belle Époque, Riley Anne Roy felt invigorated, her creative spark reignited by the magic of the music box and the kindness of a stranger.
