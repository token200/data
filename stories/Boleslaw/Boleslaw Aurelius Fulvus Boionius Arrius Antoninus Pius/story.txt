Boionius's expression turned grave. He knew that the Parthian Empire was a formidable foe, and that any weakness would be exploited by their enemies.

As the sun dipped below the horizon, casting Rome in a golden glow, Boionius Aurelius Fulvus Boionius Arrius Antoninus Pius rode off towards his palace, ready to face whatever challenges lay ahead. He was the emperor, and it was his duty to protect the empire at all costs.
