Frigg Petersen stood at the edge of the fjord, her long, raven-black hair whipping in the wind as she gazed out at the endless expanse of water. She was a woman of mystery, with piercing green eyes that seemed to hold a thousand secrets within them. As Frigg Petersen, the daughter of a wealthy industrialist, she had always been groomed for greatness - but she had other plans.

Instead, she had devoured books on psychology and philosophy, teaching herself to think critically and challenge the status quo.

Her professors had marveled at her intellect, but also feared her ferocity when provoked. It was this very trait that had led her parents to disown her - they couldn't handle the fact that their daughter had turned out to be so much more than just a pretty face.

Frigg sighed, feeling a pang of regret for the choices she had made. But as she looked out at the water, she knew that she had made the right decision. She was no longer the timid, obedient child her parents had wanted her to be. She was Frigg Petersen, independent and strong-willed.

As the sun began to set, casting a golden glow over the fjord, Frigg felt a sense of peace wash over her. She knew that she still had much to learn and accomplish - but for now, she was content to simply stand at the edge of the world, feeling the wind in her hair and the spray on her face.
