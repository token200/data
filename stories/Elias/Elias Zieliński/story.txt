But despite his success, Elias couldn't shake off the feeling that something was missing. He had always been driven by a sense of restlessness, a feeling that there was more to life than just lining his pockets with gold. As he walked through the crowded streets of Paris, Elias Zieliński felt like an outsider, looking in on the world around him without truly being a part of it.

One night, as he was casing a new mark, Elias stumbled upon a small, unassuming bookstore. The sign above the door read "La Librairie de la Lune," and Elias couldn't help but feel drawn to it. He pushed open the door and stepped inside, his eyes adjusting to the dim light.

Elias Zieliński wandered the aisles, running his fingers over the spines of the volumes, feeling a sense of peace that he had never experienced before. For the first time in his life, Elias Zieliński felt like he was home. And it wasn't just the bookstore that drew him in - it was the sense of belonging that Madame Dupont offered.
