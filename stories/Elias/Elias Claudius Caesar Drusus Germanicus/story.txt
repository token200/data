Despite his many successes, Elias never forgot his humble beginnings or the values that had been instilled in him from a young age. He remained true to the ideals of Augustus, who had first adopted him as his own son, and he worked tirelessly to promote the welfare of Rome and its people.

As the years passed, Elias' reputation grew, and he became one of the most respected figures in Roman society. His full name - Elias Claudius Caesar Drusus Germanicus - would go down in history as a testament to his unwavering commitment to duty and his unrelenting pursuit of greatness.
