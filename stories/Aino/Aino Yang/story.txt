As a young chef, Aino had always been driven by a passion for preserving traditional family recipes. Her grandmother, who had immigrated to Japan from Korea, had passed down these recipes through generations, each one imbued with love and care. But after her grandmother's passing, Aino found herself struggling to keep the recipes alive.

She spent years working in various restaurants, trying to perfect each dish, but something was missing. The flavors seemed dull, the presentation uninspired. She longed to create something new, something that honored her heritage while also innovating for a modern audience.

One day, while exploring the winding streets of Tokyo's historic district, Aino stumbled upon an old, mysterious shop tucked away between a sake brewery and a Korean import store.
