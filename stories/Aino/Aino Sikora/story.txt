Aino Sikora stepped off the crowded train and onto the platform, her eyes scanning the bustling station for any sign of her contact. She had been waiting for what felt like hours, and she was starting to get anxious. Her name was Aino Sikora, and she was a skilled operative with a reputation for getting in and out of tight spots unnoticed.

The details were sketchy at best - rumors of a mole within the organization, someone feeding intel to their competitors. Aino Sikora's job was to find out who it was and put a stop to it before it was too late.

Just as she was starting to lose hope, a figure emerged from the crowd. It was a woman, tall and slender with piercing green eyes that locked onto Aino Sikora's immediately. The woman smiled, and Aino could sense a hint of nervousness behind it.

Aino nodded curtly, her mind racing with possibilities. Who was this woman, and what did she know? She asked softly, "What's your name?"

"I'm...Elara," the woman replied, glancing around nervously.

Aino raised an eyebrow. Elara wasn't exactly the most memorable name - but then again, that might be the point. The more people who knew her true identity, the more vulnerable she was to being compromised.

They stepped off the train and onto the platform, joining a throng of commuters rushing to their destinations. Aino Sikora kept pace with Elara, her eyes scanning the crowd for any signs of trouble. They moved swiftly through the station, making their way towards the exits.
