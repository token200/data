Born in Berlin, Germany, Aino grew up amidst the vibrant cultural scene of Weimar Republic-era Berlin. As a young woman, she was drawn to the works of Expressionist masters like Chagall and Klee, whose bold brushstrokes and vivid colors inspired her own unique style. After completing her studies at the prestigious Academy der Bildenden Künste in Vienna, Aino Carlburg set out to make a name for herself in the art world.

Her breakthrough came when she was commissioned to paint portraits of prominent intellectuals and artists who frequented Berlin's avant-garde salons. These sitters included notable figures like Bertolt Brecht and Erich Maria Remarque, whose strong personalities and convictions were reflected in Aino Carlburg's thoughtful and nuanced depictions.

As her reputation grew, so did the range of subjects she tackled. Aino Carlburg's paintings often explored themes of identity, morality, and social justice, reflecting her own concerns about the changing world around her. Despite her success, Aino Carlburg remained an enigmatic figure, rarely giving interviews or sharing personal anecdotes about her life. Years went by, and Aino Carlburg's art continued to captivate audiences worldwide. Her portraits remained timeless, imbued with a quiet intensity that seemed to draw the viewer in.
