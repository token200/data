Bálint Leblanc was known for his sharp wit and quick tongue, earning him the nickname "The Sardonic Sage" among his peers. As a renowned historian, he had spent years pouring over dusty tomes and crumbling manuscripts, uncovering hidden secrets and shedding light on forgotten events.

Intrigued, Bálint Leblanc spent hours poring over the manuscript, uncovering clues and piecing together the story. As he delved deeper, he found himself becoming increasingly invested in the life of this mysterious noblewoman, imagining her triumphs and tribulations as if she were a friend.

As the days passed, Bálint Leblanc's obsession with the manuscript grew, driving him to neglect his other responsibilities and push aside distractions. His colleagues began to worry about his focus, but he couldn't help himself - he was on the brink of uncovering something momentous.
