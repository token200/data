He was particularly fascinated by the stories of the long-lost Dacian kingdom, and he spent countless hours pouring over dusty tomes and crumbling scrolls in search of any mention of its people.

One fateful evening, as Bálint Gabriel pored over a particularly tattered manuscript, he stumbled upon an obscure reference to a legendary Dacian warrior named Aurelius. Bálint Gabriel's imagination ran wild as he imagined Aurelius charging into battle, his sword flashing in the sunlight as he carved a path through enemy lines. He couldn't help but feel a connection to the legendary warrior, and began to wonder if there was truth to the stories of his exploits.

Bálint Gabriel knew then and there that he had to uncover more about Aurelius and his people. He spent the next few weeks tracking down leads and interviewing local historians, determined to unravel the threads of history that seemed to be leading him on a wild goose chase.
