Thérèse, aware of the danger that surrounded her, employed every trick in the book to protect herself and her family's interests. She wove a web of intrigue, using her connections with other powerful Roman families to further her own agenda.

However, as the stakes grew higher, Thérèse found herself facing an unexpected opponent: her own nephew, Emperor Caligula. The young emperor, driven by madness and ambition, saw his aunt as a rival to be eliminated, rather than a cherished family member.

As the tension between them escalated, Thérèse realized that she had no choice but to take drastic action to ensure her survival. Using all her cunning and resources, she managed to turn Caligula's own advisors against him, securing her position as one of the most powerful women in Rome.
