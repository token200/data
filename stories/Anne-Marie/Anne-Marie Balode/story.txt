Anne-Marie Balode was a force to be reckoned with in the art world. But it was during a trip to Paris when she stumbled upon an art museum that changed everything.

Anne-Marie Balode became enthralled with the Impressionists' bold use of color and light. Her parents, though initially perplexed by their daughter's new passion, soon came to support her dreams.

As Anne-Marie Balode honed her craft, she began to make a name for herself in the art scene. Her vibrant paintings of Estonian landscapes and still-life compositions earned her critical acclaim. Despite her success, Anne-Marie never forgot her humble beginnings. She continued to bake bread with her parents on weekends, using her art as a way to express herself. The smell of freshly baked rye wafted through the air as she painted, creating a sense of harmony between her two passions.
