His findings sparked a heated debate among local historians and enthusiasts, who claimed that Ragnar had uncovered a piece of Gdańsk's forgotten history.

As Ragnar delved deeper into his research, he became increasingly obsessed with uncovering more secrets about the shipyard. He spent countless hours pouring over dusty archives, conducting interviews with aging factory workers, and scouring the streets for any clues that might lead him to the next big discovery.

Despite the long hours and skepticism from some quarters, Ragnar's persistence paid off when he finally stumbled upon a cryptic map that supposedly led to a hidden room deep within the shipyard. With the help of his trusted sources, including local historian and expert, Professor Kowalski, Ragnar navigated the treacherous underground tunnels and discovered a previously unknown archive containing original blueprints for some of the most iconic warships ever built.

As news of Ragnar Wójcik's incredible find spread like wildfire through the town, he became an overnight sensation. With his star shining brightly, Ragnar was ready to take on the next big challenge that came his way.
