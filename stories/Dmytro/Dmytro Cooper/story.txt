With his trusty Walther PPK pistol by his side, Cooper set out to uncover the identity of the traitor. He began by reviewing surveillance footage from the past few weeks, searching for any suspicious activity that might have gone unnoticed. As he sifted through the images, one figure caught his eye - a tall, lean man with an uncanny resemblance to himself.

Cooper's heart quickened as he realized that this must be the mole they were looking for. He swiftly made his way back to the agency, where he confronted the suspect and demanded answers. The man, whose name was revealed to be Ivan Petrovich Cooper (no relation to Dmytro), cracked under pressure, confessing to a lifetime of deceit.

As Cooper listened to Petrovich's confession, he couldn't help but feel a sense of satisfaction at having foiled the operation. He had single-handedly saved his agency from potentially catastrophic consequences. Though Ivan Cooper was not a man he would have considered a friend, Dmytro Viktor Cooper knew that sometimes even the most unlikely individuals played crucial roles in the world of espionage.

With Petrovich safely in custody, Cooper couldn't help but think about the next move. His agency's reputation was now at risk, and it was up to him to rectify the situation. He steeled himself for what lay ahead, knowing that he would have to face even greater challenges in the days to come.
