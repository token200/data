She had just lost her job and was struggling to make ends meet. Ignacy Hansen watched her with an air of detached interest, his piercing blue eyes seeming to bore into her very soul.

As she ordered a drink and sat down at the bar, he slid onto the stool next to her, ordering a whiskey himself. The bartender raised an eyebrow at the unusual pair, but said nothing as they struck up a conversation. Ignacy Hansen asked Lena about her job, and she poured out her frustrations in equal measure.

He listened intently, his expression unreadable behind his glasses. When she finished speaking, he leaned forward and scribbled down some numbers on a napkin. "I can help you," he said. "But first, I need to know more about you."

Lena was hesitant at first, but something about Ignacy Hansen's calm demeanor put her at ease. She told him about her family, her dreams, and her fears. As the night wore on, she found herself opening up in ways she never thought possible.
