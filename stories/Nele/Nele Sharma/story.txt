Nele Sharma's eyes sparkled with mischief as she locked up her small, cluttered bookstore. The scent of old books and fresh coffee wafted through the air, carrying the promise of adventure within its walls. Nele had always been a bookworm, but after years of working for others, she'd finally saved enough to open her own store.

The sign above the door read "Moonlit Pages" in elegant, cursive letters, which was fitting since Nele's love for stories knew no bounds. As she stepped back to admire her handiwork, a soft knock at the door interrupted her reverie. It was Ravi, her best friend and partner-in-crime.

"What are you doing here so late?" Nele asked, ushering him inside.

"I couldn't sleep," Ravi replied, his dark hair tousled from lack of rest. "I had a brilliant idea for our next book club pick."

Nele's eyes lit up. "Do tell!"

"Romantic novels set in India during the colonial era," he said, a grin spreading across his face. "Think 'The English Patient' meets Bollywood drama."

Nele's mind whirled with possibilities. She knew that her customers would love exploring lesser-known corners of Indian literature.

As they pored over book recommendations, their conversation drifted from novels to life-changing decisions. Nele shared her fears about running a business alone, and Ravi offered words of encouragement, reminding her of the countless women who'd successfully built their own enterprises before her.
