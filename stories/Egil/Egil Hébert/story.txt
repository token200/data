Egil Hébert listened intently as the chief recounted the events, his eyes narrowing slightly as he absorbed every detail. He asked pointed questions, probing for weaknesses in the evidence and exploiting any inconsistencies that might reveal themselves.

As the night wore on, Egil Hébert rose from his chair, paceing back and forth across the room with an air of contemplative intensity. The police chief watched him with a mixture of fascination and unease, sensing that this man was onto something but unsure what it was.

The next morning, Egil Hébert set out to investigate, beginning with the latest burglary site. He surveyed the scene meticulously, taking note of every detail – the shattered glass, the torn curtains, the faint scent of perfume on the air.

Hours turned into days as Egil Hébert worked tirelessly, following a trail of subtle clues that ultimately led him to an unexpected suspect: none other than the police chief's own sister. The motive was twofold – financial gain and a personal vendetta against her brother's department.
