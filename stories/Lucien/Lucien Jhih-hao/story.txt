One afternoon, as Lucien Jhih-hao was setting up his stall at the city's popular art market, he noticed a group of tourists gathered around his work. The tourists were impressed by the intricate details and vibrant colors, and soon, a line began to form.

As the day went on, Lucien Jhih-hao sold several of his pieces to enthusiastic buyers. He was thrilled to see his art finding its way into the hands of people who appreciated its beauty and significance. The sign above the door read "Moonlight Serenade," and the aroma wafting out of the windows was enticing. Lucien Jhih-hao felt an overwhelming urge to enter the café and explore its charm.

As he sipped his coffee, Lucien Jhih-hao felt at peace, surrounded by the café's tranquil atmosphere.

For a moment, Lucien Jhih-hao forgot about his art and his worries, and simply enjoyed the company of Mei and the soothing ambiance.
