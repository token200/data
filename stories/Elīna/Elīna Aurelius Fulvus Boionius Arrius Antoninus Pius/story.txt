Born into the illustrious Aurelia dynasty, Elīna had grown up surrounded by politics and power. Her name alone was a testament to her family's legacy: Elīna Aurelius Fulvus Boionius Arrius Antoninus Pius – a mouthful that struck fear into the hearts of those who dared oppose him.

As he rode through the desert, Elīna pondered his future as emperor. The weight of responsibility rested heavily on his shoulders, but he was determined to prove himself worthy of the title. His parents, both high-ranking officials in their own right, had instilled in him a strong sense of duty and loyalty.

Despite his impressive credentials, Elīna couldn't shake the feeling that there was more to life than politics and conquest. He longed for adventure, for excitement, and for love. But as a member of the imperial family, such desires were seen as weaknesses, not strengths.

For the first time in his life, he felt truly alive – Elīna Aurelius Fulvus Boionius Arrius Antoninus Pius, emperor of Rome, but also a man with a future, and a chance to forge his own destiny.
