As a young man, he drifted from one dead-end job to another, never staying in one place for too long. His temper was legendary among his coworkers, and more than once, he'd found himself at odds with his bosses.

But life took an unexpected turn when Mario's estranged father, Hans, passed away, leaving him a modest inheritance: a dilapidated flat in the same neighborhood where he grew up. The catch? A condition attached to the will: Mario had to fix up the place and live there for at least six months before he could even think about claiming his share of the estate.

Mario was skeptical, but something about the prospect of finally having a stake in the life he'd always lived on borrowed time resonated with him. He spent weeks scrubbing floors, patching walls, and scraping off decades of grime from every inch of the flat. It was backbreaking work, but for the first time in his life, Mario felt like he had something to call his own.

As he worked, Mario started noticing small things about himself that he'd never paid attention to before. The way the sunlight streamed through the grimy windows and highlighted the dust motes dancing in the air. The sound of birds singing outside his window on a sunny morning. He even caught glimpses of himself in the mirror, and for the first time, he saw someone who was capable of kindness, of compassion.

The kid needed someone to care for him, and Hans had left his old friend to watch over him until he was old enough to fend for himself.
