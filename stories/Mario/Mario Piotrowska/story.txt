Mario Piotrowska was a man with a passion for cooking that rivaled his love for adventure. As Mario Piotrowska grew older, his love for cooking only intensified. He went on to study hospitality in Warsaw and later honed his skills at a Michelin-starred restaurant in Paris. After years of working under some of the world's most renowned chefs, he decided it was time to strike out on his own.

Mario Piotrowska opened his own restaurant, "Kuchnia" (meaning "kitchen" in Polish), in a quaint alleyway in Krakow's historic district. The small eatery quickly gained a loyal following among locals and tourists alike, thanks to Mario Piotrowska's innovative takes on traditional Polish dishes.

One day, a prominent food critic from Warsaw stumbled upon Kuchnia while exploring the city.
