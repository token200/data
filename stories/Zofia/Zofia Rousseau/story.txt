Her great-grandfather, a French impressionist painter, had instilled in her a love for the classics, while her grandmother, a skilled seamstress, had taught her the intricacies of traditional embroidery.

She had always been fascinated by the connection between art and history, and it was this intersection that drove her to create some of her most breathtaking works.

Her latest project, a collection of paintings inspired by the city's lesser-known landmarks, was nearing completion. Zofia Rousseau spent hours in her small studio, carefully blending colors and textures to capture the essence of each site. The results were nothing short of stunning – vivid depictions of abandoned churches, crumbling bridges, and forgotten gardens.
