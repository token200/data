Irene Hassan was a woman with a passion for photography that rivaled her love for life itself. Born in Nairobi, Kenya, she had grown up surrounded by vibrant colors and bustling streets, which later influenced her striking black-and-white photographs.

As a young girl, Irene spent hours snapping pictures of the birds outside her window or the intricate patterns on the textiles her mother used to weave. Her parents encouraged her curiosity, gifting her with an old film camera that she treasured more than any possession.

Years went by, and Irene's passion for photography only intensified. She began to explore the world beyond Nairobi, capturing breathtaking landscapes in Ethiopia, the majestic architecture of Dubai, and even the vibrant colors of Indian street food stalls. Her photographs told stories of resilience, hope, and community, resonating with people from all walks of life.

Eager to showcase her work, she packed her bags and set off on an adventure that would take her to the city of love and light.
