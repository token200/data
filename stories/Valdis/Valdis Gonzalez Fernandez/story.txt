One day, while performing at a gig in a dingy warehouse on the outskirts of town, Valdis noticed a young girl sitting alone in the front row, nursing a torn poster that read "I want to be a rockstar too." Something about the girl's determination struck a chord with Valdis, and she decided to make her an offer.

After the show, Valdis introduced herself to the girl – who turned out to be 12-year-old Maria – and began to mentor her. Over the next few weeks, Valdis worked tirelessly with Maria, teaching her how to write songs, play guitar, and command a stage presence.

As Maria's skills improved, Valdis began to see her as a mini-me. She was young, spirited, and unafraid to speak her mind – qualities that reminded Valdis of her own early days on the punk rock scene. The two quickly formed a bond, with Valdis becoming like a surrogate big sister to Maria.

As Maria's confidence grew, she began to perform alongside Valdis at local shows, and eventually even landed her first gig with the Rebel's Ransom crew. It was a moment of triumph for Valdis Gonzalez Fernandez, who watched as one of her young charges blossomed into a talented performer in her own right.

When asked about her decision to take Maria under her wing, Valdis simply shrugged and said, "I know what it's like to feel like an outsider. I want to help others find their place."
