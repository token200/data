Ria Järv stepped off the train and onto the worn platform, her eyes scanning the familiar scenery of her hometown. The once-thriving industrial town was now a shadow of its former self, but to Ria, it still felt like home.

As she made her way through the deserted streets, the silence was broken by the sound of her own footsteps echoing off the abandoned buildings. She had returned to Tartu, Estonia, after years away, with a mix of emotions swirling inside her.

Ria's eyes wandered back to the old photograph in her pocket – one that showed her and her late mother, Maria, standing outside their family home on a warm summer day. The photo was creased and faded, but Ria knew every detail by heart.

She had grown up listening to her mother's stories of the Soviet era, when Tartu was a hub for cultural revolution and intellectual fervor. Her mother had been a key figure in the city's artistic scene, but she had vanished under mysterious circumstances when Ria was just a teenager.

Now, as Ria stood on the platform, she felt an overwhelming urge to uncover the truth about her mother's disappearance. She had spent years searching for answers, scouring archives and talking to old friends, but the truth remained elusive.

As she walked through the streets, Ria noticed something peculiar – a small, hand-painted mural on the side of a building, depicting a young woman standing in front of a red door with a subtle smile. The image sent shivers down her spine.

Ria knew that the artist was one of her mother's closest friends, a woman named Kati who had been a confidant to Maria before she vanished. Suddenly, the pieces began to fall into place – a series of cryptic messages, hidden journals, and forgotten conversations started to emerge from Ria's memory.

With each new discovery, Ria felt like she was unraveling a thread of her mother's life that had been carefully woven away. She knew she still had a long way to go, but for the first time in years, she felt a sense of hope – and maybe even closure – about what happened to Maria all those years ago.

As Ria walked into her old home, now empty and dark, she noticed a small door that she had never seen before. The same red door from the mural on the wall. She turned the handle, pushed it open, and stepped inside...
