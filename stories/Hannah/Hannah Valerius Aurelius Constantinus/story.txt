Hannah's thoughts were interrupted by the sound of her servant, Lucius, calling out to her. "Hannah Valerius Aurelius Constantinus, it's time for your lesson with Master Marcus," he said, his voice firm but respectful.

But lately, she had begun to question the morality of her family's actions. Was it right to rule over others simply for the sake of power?

Hannah felt a surge of frustration as she sat down to listen to Master Marcus' lectures. She knew that her family's empire was built on bloodshed and domination. But could she really condone such actions?

As the lesson drew to a close, Hannah stood up to leave. "Thank you, Master Marcus," she said, her voice firm. "But I have some questions for you." The old man raised an eyebrow as she began to ask him about the ethics of war and the treatment of conquered peoples.

For the first time in her life, Hannah felt like she was taking a stand. She was no longer content to simply follow the path laid out before her. She wanted to forge her own way, to make a difference in the world. And as she left Master Marcus' chambers, she knew that nothing would ever be the same again for Hannah Valerius Aurelius Constantinus.
