Beatriz Naoko was a free-spirited artist who had always followed her heart, no matter where it led. Born in Tokyo, Japan to Brazilian parents, she had grown up surrounded by vibrant colors and eclectic rhythms that would later influence her unique art style.

Her creativity was encouraged from a young age, and she devoured books on everything from anatomy to abstract art.

After completing her studies in Tokyo, Beatriz Naoko moved to New York City to pursue her dream of becoming a professional artist. She shared a small studio in Brooklyn with several fellow creatives, and spent long hours honing her craft, experimenting with different mediums and techniques.

Despite the challenges she faced as a solo female artist, Beatriz Naoko persevered, driven by her passion for self-expression. Her art was a reflection of her mixed heritage, blending traditional Japanese motifs with vibrant Brazilian colors and bold geometric shapes.

As her reputation grew, Beatriz Naoko began to receive commissions from galleries and collectors around the world. Her work was exhibited in museums and art fairs, and she became known for her innovative use of materials and her ability to tell complex stories through simple images.

One day, Beatriz Naoko received an invitation to showcase her work at a prestigious gallery in Rio de Janeiro, a city that held a special place in her heart. She accepted the opportunity without hesitation, knowing it would be a chance to share her art with her community and connect with her Brazilian roots.
