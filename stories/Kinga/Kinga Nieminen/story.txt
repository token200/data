Kinga Nieminen stood atop the windswept hill, her long blonde hair whipping about her face as she gazed out at the vast expanse of the Finnish wilderness. The snow-capped peaks of the Lakeland Mountains stretched out before her like a canvas of white silk, untouched and pristine.

Kinga Nieminen watched them, mesmerized, as they darted and played in the snow. It was moments like these that she felt most alive, most connected to the natural world. But Kinga Nieminen had other plans. She dreamed of exploring the world beyond Finland, of making a name for herself as an artist or a writer. Her parents might not understand, but she knew that this was who she truly was meant to be.
