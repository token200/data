Charlotte Pold had always been fascinated by the art of taxidermy. As a young girl growing up in a small town in the Pacific Northwest, she spent hours watching the local naturalist preserve and mount specimens for display in the town's museum. It was only a matter of time before Charlotte decided to follow in the footsteps of her idol.

After completing her degree in biology and fine arts, Charlotte moved to Portland to hone her skills under the guidance of renowned taxidermist, Mr. Jenkins. She spent countless hours practicing the delicate art of preserving and mounting specimens, determined to create pieces that would rival those of her mentor.

As she worked tirelessly in his studio, Charlotte's passion for taxidermy only grew stronger. Her clients soon took notice, seeking her out for bespoke commissions that showcased her unique style and attention to detail. Word spread quickly about the talented young taxidermist with a knack for capturing the essence of even the most elusive creatures.

One fateful evening, as the sun dipped into the Willamette River, Charlotte received an unexpected visit from none other than Mr. Jenkins himself. He had been impressed by her work and wanted to offer her an opportunity to showcase her talents at his esteemed museum's annual gala. The event was being held in just a few short weeks' time.

Overjoyed by the prospect of displaying her art alongside some of the world's most renowned taxidermists, Charlotte threw herself into the task with renewed vigor. Weeks flew by as she worked tirelessly to create an exquisite piece that would leave all who attended speechless. When the night of the gala finally arrived, Charlotte Pold's masterpiece took center stage, earning rave reviews from attendees and solidifying her reputation as a rising star in the world of taxidermy.

As the evening drew to a close, Mr. Jenkins approached Charlotte with a warm smile. "You've surpassed even my expectations, my dear," he said, shaking her hand warmly. "I have no doubt that your talents will take you far."
