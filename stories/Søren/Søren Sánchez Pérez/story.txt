The owner, an elderly man named Carlos, greeted him warmly. "Welcome, young artist!" he exclaimed. "I see you have a good ear for music." Søren nodded, his eyes scanning the shelves stacked with guitars, violins, and flutes. He had always been fascinated by the way music could evoke emotions, just like painting.

Carlos noticed Søren's fascination and offered him a guitar to try out. As soon as he plucked the strings, the sounds that filled the shop were hauntingly beautiful. Søren was captivated, and for the first time in his life, he felt a sense of connection between music and art.

Over the next few weeks, Søren visited La Casa del Sonido regularly, learning to play the guitar and composing songs inspired by his paintings. His passion for music had awakened a new dimension to his art, and people began to take notice. Søren's murals, once confined to the walls of Barcelona's streets, were now accompanied by melodies that seemed to come alive.

As word of Søren Sánchez Pérez's unique talent spread, he became a celebrated figure in the artistic community.
