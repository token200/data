He spotted a familiar face - a young CIA agent named Rachel, who had worked alongside him on several missions. They exchanged brief nods as they passed each other by, the only acknowledgement of their years-long partnership.

Abdiweli's task was to uncover the identity of this traitor and put an end to it.

The Somali government's struggles to rebuild after decades of civil war were evident everywhere - in the crumbling buildings, the makeshift market stalls, and the people themselves.

He knew that building trust would be essential to getting close to the mole, so he took a deep breath and focused on learning more about Somali culture and customs.

Over the next few days, Abdiweli gathered pieces of intelligence, following a trail of clues that led him deeper into the heart of Somalia's government. It was clear that someone with high-level clearance had access to classified information - but who?

As he pondered his next move, Abdiweli couldn't shake the feeling that he was being watched. He glanced around the crowded market, scanning for any sign of surveillance. The city seemed to be holding its breath, waiting for him to make a move.
