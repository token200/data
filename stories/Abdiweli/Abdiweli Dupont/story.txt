As a child, Dubi would spend hours listening to reggae records with his grandfather, who had immigrated from Jamaica.

As he grew older, Dubi's passion for music only deepened. He began playing the guitar and singing in local bands, drawing on influences from Bob Marley to Amy Winehouse. His soulful voice and genre-bending style quickly earned him a loyal following among London's hipster crowd.

But despite his success, Dubi struggled to find purpose beyond his music. He drifted from one job to another, never quite feeling fulfilled. That was until he met Luna, a free-spirited artist who introduced him to the world of street art.

Luna's vibrant murals and stencil work sparked something in Dubi.
