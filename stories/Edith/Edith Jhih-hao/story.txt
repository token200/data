Edith Jhih-hao was a master weaver, renowned for her intricate and exquisite tapestries that told stories of ancient myths and legends. Her small shop in the heart of the city was a treasure trove of colors and textures, where people from all walks of life would come to marvel at her handiwork.

Edith Jhih-hao's fingers moved with precision and speed as she worked on her latest masterpiece, a vibrant depiction of the Chinese dragon. Her eyes sparkled with creativity as she wove threads of silk and wool into a tapestry that seemed to pulse with life.

One day, a young girl named Lily wandered into Edith Jhih-hao's shop, searching for a special gift for her mother's birthday. As she browsed the shelves, her eyes landed on a stunning tapestry that seemed to shimmer in the light. It was a depiction of the moon, surrounded by delicate clouds and stars.

"Ah, you've found the Moonlit Serenade," Edith Jhih-hao said with a smile, noticing Lily's fascination. "It's one of my most beloved creations. I spent months weaving the threads to capture the soft glow of the moon."

Lily's eyes widened as she reached out to touch the tapestry. Edith Jhih-hao chuckled, knowing that her work was not just beautiful but also magical.

"How much?" Lily asked, her voice barely above a whisper.

Edith Jhih-hao named a price that was reasonable, considering the time and skill that had gone into creating the tapestry. Lily nodded, and Edith Jhih-hao carefully wrapped the Moonlit Serenade in tissue paper, ready to be taken home.

As the day drew to a close, Edith Jhih-hao stepped back to admire her work. Her fingers moved over the threads, feeling the familiar satisfaction of a job well done. She knew that she had created something special, something that would bring joy and beauty into Lily's mother's life.

And so, the Moonlit Serenade found its way into a home, where it would be cherished for years to come, a testament to Edith Jhih-hao's skill as a weaver and her love of storytelling through thread.
