As he made his way through customs, Giuseppe Hussein flashed his ID and smiled blandly at the officials. They barely gave him a second glance, used to seeing his name pop up in local news outlets whenever he unearthed some long-lost relic.

After collecting his luggage, Giuseppe Hussein hailed a taxi and headed towards the outskirts of the airport. His contact, a local guide named Alessandro, was supposed to meet him at a crumbling olive grove on the outskirts of town.

As the taxi wound its way through the dusty streets, Giuseppe Hussein couldn't help but feel a thrill of excitement. He had been searching for this temple for years – pouring over dusty texts, scouring the countryside for any sign of the ancient structure.

Alessandro nodded, a slow smile spreading across his face. "That's the temple of Zeus," he said, his voice barely above a whisper.
