As he grew older, Joe began to write his own stories, weaving together threads from his childhood observations and his own dreams of exploration. His characters were complex and multifaceted, with Giuseppe "Joe" Girace himself often taking center stage in stories both real and imagined.

Despite his success, Joe remained humble and grounded, crediting his roots for his unique perspective. When asked about his writing process, he would smile wistfully and say, "I write from the heart, just like my nonno used to make pasta."

The book, titled "The Family Table," was a love letter to the power of food and community, written in Joe's characteristic lyrical prose. It was a huge success, cementing his reputation as one of the most gifted writers of his generation.
