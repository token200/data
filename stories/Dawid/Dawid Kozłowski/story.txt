Years later, Dawid found himself in Berlin, where he had reinvented himself as a successful businessman. His polished suits and charming demeanor fooled even the most discerning eye, but those who looked closer could see the faint scars beneath his skin, reminders of a life lived hard and fast.

"Dawid Kozłowski is not someone to be trifled with," he would tell people who asked, his voice low and serious.

Piotr arrived to find Dawid pacing back and forth, his eyes wild and unhinged. "I've been watching them," Dawid said, his voice barely above a whisper. "They're getting too close.
