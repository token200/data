Orsolya Moreno Martinez was no stranger to the streets of Los Angeles. Growing up in the city's vibrant arts district, she had spent countless hours sketching the eclectic mix of murals and graffiti that adorned the walls of buildings everywhere.

She was struck by the sheer talent on display and knew she had found her community. From that moment on, Orsolya became an integral part of the city's thriving street art scene.

As Orsolya Moreno Martinez continued to hone her craft, she began to incorporate more personal narratives into her work, reflecting on themes such as identity, community, and the struggles faced by immigrant families like her own.
