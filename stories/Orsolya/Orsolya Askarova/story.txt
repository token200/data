Orsolya Askarova stepped off the plane and onto the sun-kissed soil of Tbilisi, Georgia. The warm breeze carried the sweet scent of blooming flowers as she made her way through the airport, her eyes fixed on the bright blue skies above.

A renowned expert in ancient Mesopotamian languages, Orsolya Askarova had spent years searching for the elusive fragments of the long-lost Script of Erishkigal. Legend had it that this script held the secrets of the underworld, and Orsolya was determined to unlock its mysteries.

As she made her way through the bustling streets of Tbilisi, Orsolya's mind wandered back to her years of research. She had spent countless hours poring over dusty texts and crumbling tablets, searching for any hint of the Script's existence. And finally, after months of searching, she had received a cryptic message from a mysterious collector claiming to possess one of the few remaining fragments.

Orsolya's eyes scanned the crowded market stalls, her heart racing with excitement. She knew that time was running out – the collector's message had been vague, and Orsolya suspected that she might be taking a gamble by trusting this stranger.

Just as she was about to lose hope, a figure emerged from the crowd, dressed in a traditional Georgian embroidered dress. "Orsolya Askarova?" he asked, his eyes sparkling with recognition.

Orsolya nodded, her heart racing with excitement. The man handed her a small, intricately carved box. Inside, she found a single fragment of clay – the first tangible evidence of the Script's existence in centuries.

Tears of joy pricked at Orsolya's eyes as she carefully placed the fragment in a special case. This was it – the discovery of a lifetime. She knew that her work would change the course of history, and for that, she was eternally grateful to the enigmatic collector who had led her to this moment.

With the fragment safely in hand, Orsolya Askarova set off on the journey of a lifetime, ready to unravel the secrets of the Script of Erishkigal and unlock the mysteries of the ancient Mesopotamian underworld.
