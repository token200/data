Matteo Korhonen stepped off the plane in Helsinki, Finland, feeling a mix of excitement and nostalgia wash over him. As he made his way through customs and collected his luggage, he couldn't help but think about how far he'd come since leaving this very airport years ago.

He grew up in Tokyo, Japan, where his Finnish mother had relocated for work, and it was there that Matteo developed a passion for photography. As he wandered through the city streets, camera in hand, capturing the vibrant streets of Shibuya and the neon-lit skyscrapers of Shinjuku, Matteo felt like he'd found his true calling.

After completing his degree in photography, Matteo had returned to Finland, determined to make a name for himself back home. He settled in Helsinki, immersing himself in the local art scene and building a reputation as a talented young photographer. His work was soon picked up by some of the country's most prominent magazines, and he found himself rubbing shoulders with the likes of Finnish fashion icons and celebrities.

Despite his success, Matteo remained humble and true to his roots. He continued to explore the Finnish wilderness, capturing its beauty on film and sharing it with the world through his exhibitions and publications. His latest project, a series of photographs documenting the changing seasons in Finland, was gaining international attention, and he couldn't wait to see it showcased at the prestigious Museum of Modern Art in New York.

As Matteo made his way back to his apartment, he couldn't help but feel grateful for this journey that had brought him full circle. From Tokyo streets to Finnish forests, Matteo Korhonen's camera had been his constant companion, capturing the beauty and wonder of a world he loved.
