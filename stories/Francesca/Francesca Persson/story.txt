It was a strategy that had worked for her so far, but one that left her feeling isolated and alone.

Despite her tough exterior, Francesca Persson was vulnerable in ways she rarely allowed anyone to see. Her parents had divorced when she was young, leaving her with a deep-seated sense of loss and abandonment. As they talked, Francesca began to open up about her past, sharing stories she had long kept hidden. For the first time in years, she felt seen and heard.

As the seasons passed, Francesca found herself drawn into the garden's rhythms and routines. She began to see the world through Emma's eyes, finding beauty in the everyday moments that had previously gone unnoticed.
