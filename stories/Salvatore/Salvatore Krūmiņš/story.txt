The patrons were huddled together, sharing stories and sipping their drinks, trying to escape the torrential rain. That's when he spotted her - a striking woman with piercing green eyes and raven-black hair, sitting alone by the window.

Salvatore Krūmiņš couldn't help but be drawn to her presence. He mustered up the courage to approach her, introducing himself with a charming smile. The woman, whose name was Sofia, was equally intrigued by Sal's enigmatic nature. As they conversed, their conversation flowed effortlessly, like the coffee beans roasting in the café's oven.

Sofia, it turned out, was a writer, working on her debut novel. Sal, being an avid reader himself, found common ground with Sofia's passion for storytelling. The hours slipped away as they delved into discussions about literature, philosophy, and life itself. For the first time in months, Salvatore Krūmiņš felt a sense of belonging.

As the storm subsided, and the café began to empty, Sal knew he had to leave. He walked Sofia home under the star-studded night sky, his heart pounding with excitement. As they stood outside her door, Sal hesitated for a moment before turning to her and asking if she'd like to join him on a midnight stroll along the Daugava River.
