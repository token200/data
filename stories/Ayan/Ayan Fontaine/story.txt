As a young girl, Ayan had grown up on these roads, exploring the winding highways with her brother by her side. They were inseparable, sharing secrets and dreams beneath the starry sky. But fate had other plans.

Now, at 25, Ayan felt like she was still searching for herself. She worked as a mechanic in this small town, fixing cars and trucks for the locals. It wasn't glamorous, but it paid the bills and allowed her to be close to the sea.

"Can I help you?" Ayan asked gruffly, wiping her hands on a rag.

The stranger smiled, his eyes crinkling at the corners. "Just looking for a mechanic. Heard this was the place to come."

Ayan's instincts told her to be wary, but there was something about the stranger that put her at ease. Maybe it was the way he smiled or the way his eyes sparkled in the sunlight.

As they talked, Ayan found herself opening up to him in ways she never thought possible. She shared stories of her brother and their adventures on these roads. The stranger listened intently, asking questions and making her laugh.

For the first time in years, Ayan felt like she was connecting with someone. Maybe it was just a fleeting moment, but she couldn't shake the feeling that this stranger had the power to change her life.
