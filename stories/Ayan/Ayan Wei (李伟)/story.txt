Ayan Wei's eyes, an piercing shade of indigo that seemed to see right through you, missed nothing. Born into a humble family in a small village on the outskirts of Beijing, Ayan's life was forever changed when his parents fell ill. With no choice but to leave them behind, he set out to forge his own path.

A talented artist, Ayan's brushstrokes danced across canvases with an uncanny grace. His paintings were vibrant, full of a passion that belied the hardships he had faced. Yet, despite his success in the city's art scene, he remained haunted by the ghosts of his past.

One fateful night, while lost in thought, Ayan stumbled upon an old antique shop tucked away in a forgotten alley. The proprietor, an elderly man named Lü, sensed something peculiar about the young artist and revealed to him that Ayan was, in fact, the descendant of a legendary calligrapher who had once lived during the Ming dynasty.
