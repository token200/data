After completing his studies, Edward moved to New York City to pursue a career in fine arts. He worked multiple part-time jobs while honing his skills as a painter, eventually landing a spot at a prestigious gallery. His paintings quickly gained attention from collectors and art critics alike, praising the unique blend of Indian and Western styles.

One evening, while sipping wine with friends at a quaint little café, Edward found himself lost in conversation about life, music, and art. A young woman named Sophia listened intently, her eyes sparkling with curiosity as Edward spoke about his inspirations and creative process. The two quickly discovered they shared a love for Indian classical music and spent the rest of the evening exploring the city's hidden gems.

As their friendship blossomed into something more, Edward found himself drawn to Sophia's infectious laughter and passion for life. He began to take her on artistic tours around the city, visiting museums and galleries late at night when they were closed to the public. Together, they'd sit in silence, absorbing the vibrant colors and textures of his paintings.
