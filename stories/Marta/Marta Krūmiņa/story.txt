Marta Krūmiņa was a master weaver from the small town of Riga, Latvia. She had spent her entire life learning the ancient art of tapestry-making from her grandmother, who had learned it from her own mother. Marta's fingers moved deftly as she worked the loom, the threads glinting in the dim light of her workshop.

As a child, Marta would sit for hours watching her grandmother weave intricate patterns into fabric. She was fascinated by the way the colors blended and contrasted, creating something entirely new from individual threads. As she grew older, Marta began to help her grandmother with the weaving, learning the techniques and secrets that had been passed down through generations of women in their family.
