Réka María stepped off the train and onto the worn platform, her eyes scanning the small station for any sign of life. She had been traveling for days, ever since she received that mysterious letter in the mail - an invitation to her grandmother's old cottage on the outskirts of town.

But something about that letter had stirred something deep within her, and she found herself packing her bags and boarding the train to return to the place where it all began.

Réka María felt a shiver run down her spine as she took the key, feeling an inexplicable sense of belonging for the first time in years. She had always known that she was adopted, and that her biological parents were long gone, but something about this place seemed to be calling to her.

Réka María wandered through the rooms, taking in every detail, every scent, every creak of the old wooden floorboards. She sat down on the porch, watching as the stars began to twinkle in the night sky, and knew that she was finally home. This place had been waiting for her all along - it had been waiting for her to rediscover who she truly was.
