As she grew older, Réka's passion for music only intensified. She spent countless hours practicing, perfecting her craft, and exploring the vast repertoire of classical music. Her dedication paid off when she was accepted into the prestigious Franz Liszt Academy of Music in Budapest, where she honed her skills under the guidance of renowned teachers.

One evening, as she was preparing for a performance at Carnegie Hall, Réka received an unexpected visit from her grandmother, who had flown in from Budapest just to attend the show. Her grandmother, a retired pianist herself, beamed with pride as she watched Réka rehearse her violin. She couldn't help but feel a sense of nostalgia wash over her, remembering the countless hours she spent teaching Réka and her siblings music.

After the show, Réka took a moment to speak with her grandmother, who was beaming with pride. "Anna, you've made me so proud," she said, using Réka's given name as a term of endearment.
