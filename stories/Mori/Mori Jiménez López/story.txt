Mori Jiménez López was a man consumed by his craft. As a master silversmith, he spent most of his days hunched over his workbench, his hands moving with precision and purpose as he coaxed intricate designs from the metal.

Born in a small village nestled in the Andes mountains, Mori had grown up surrounded by the sounds and smells of his family's silverwork. His abuela would tell him stories of the ancient gods who had once commanded the flow of molten metal, and how their craftsmen had shaped it into vessels worthy of the divine.

As a young man, Mori had learned the trade from his father, who had learned it from his father before him. He had honed his skills in the village's small workshop, producing beautiful but simple pieces that were sought after by locals and travelers alike.
