Alfie Liebad was a man consumed by his craft. A master clockmaker, he spent most of his days hunched over his workbench, surrounded by ticking timepieces and whirring gears. His workshop, tucked away in a small alleyway near the city square, was a testament to his dedication. Shelves groaned under the weight of clocks in various states of repair, each one a tiny universe unto itself.

Alfie's eyes were a deep, piercing blue that seemed to hold a world of knowledge within them. They sparkled with intensity as he worked, his brow furrowed in concentration. His fingers moved with precision, the tools in his hands an extension of himself. Every movement was deliberate, every gesture calculated to coax the most beauty from his creations.

Despite his focus on timekeeping, Alfie had a secret: he loved the art of nostalgia. He believed that clocks held not just mathematical secrets, but whispers of the past. In the intricate mechanisms and delicate balance of each timepiece, he saw echoes of love, loss, and memory.
