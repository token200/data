Brage Rasmussen was known for his unorthodox approach to life. As a free-spirited artist and musician, he lived by his own rules and never seemed to conform to societal norms. His bright pink hair and eclectic style often drew attention, but it was his infectious energy that truly won people over.

But this sense of not belonging only fueled his creativity. As a teenager, he spent hours sketching and playing music, pouring all his emotions onto the page.

As an adult, Brage's artistic talents took him to some unexpected places. He became a sought-after painter, commissioned by galleries and collectors around the world. But despite all his success, Brage remained humble. He believed that true art came from a place of vulnerability, not bravado. And so, he continued to push boundaries, experimenting with new mediums and techniques. His music, too, was a reflection of this approach – soulful and raw, it spoke directly to the hearts of those who listened.

One evening, Brage Rasmussen found himself at a small jazz club in New Orleans. He had been invited by a fellow musician, and as he took the stage, something shifted inside him.
