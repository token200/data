He spent countless hours sketching the intricate patterns on traditional folk clothes and admiring the vibrant colors of the local markets. This exposure would later influence his unique style, which seamlessly blended realism with abstract expressionism.

His art often reflected this obsession, featuring characters with multiple identities or none at all. Critics praised his work for its depth and complexity, but some saw it as a form of escapism from the mundane world.
