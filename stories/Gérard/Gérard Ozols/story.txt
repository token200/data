Gérard Pierre Marie Ozols was a man with a passion for art and music. After completing his training, Gérard set out on his own, traveling to Italy to study sculpture and later to Spain to learn flamenco music. He became known for his stunning pottery and his mesmerizing guitar playing, which seemed to evoke the very essence of the Spanish passion.

Years passed, and Gérard's reputation as an artist spread far and wide. People came from all over to see his work and experience the energy he brought to every performance. One day, a wealthy collector offered Gérard a lucrative deal: create a series of paintings inspired by his homeland, the French Riviera, for a grand exhibition in Monte Carlo. The catch was that he had to complete the pieces within just six weeks. Undaunted, Gérard Ozols accepted the challenge.

For days on end, Gérard worked tirelessly, fueled by coffee and inspiration. As the deadline loomed closer, his studio became a whirlwind of color and movement.
