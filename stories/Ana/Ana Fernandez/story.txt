Ana Fernandez had always been fascinated by the art of photography. As a child, she would spend hours poring over her parents' old cameras, learning the intricacies of composition and lighting. As she grew older, her passion only intensified, and she began to pursue it as a career.

She started by taking on freelance work, shooting weddings, portraits, and landscapes for local clients. Her talent quickly became apparent, and soon she was flooded with requests from people wanting her to capture their special moments.

Ana Fernandez was both thrilled and intimidated by the opportunity. She spent weeks researching and brainstorming ideas, pouring over books and documentaries on social justice and inequality.
