With his camera in hand, Pekka captured the struggles and triumphs of these unsung heroes, giving voice to those who had been silenced.

As he walked through the abandoned coal mines, Pekka couldn't help but think of his own father, who had taught him the art of photography all those years ago. Now, as a father himself, Pekka wanted to carry on that legacy and give his own son the same love for photography that he had.
