Vanessa Liepiņa stepped off the bus and onto the winding streets of Riga, Latvia's charming capital city. As she walked through the historic center, she couldn't help but feel a sense of nostalgia wash over her. It was here that her family had settled after fleeing Latvia during World War II.

Her parents, refugees themselves, had instilled in her a deep sense of pride in their heritage. Vanessa Liepiņa's eyes lit up. "Da, I love Latvian music," she replied, using the correct phraseology in both English and her native language.

The musician nodded, pleased that someone had taken an interest. As he began to play, Vanessa Liepiņa closed her eyes and let the melodies transport her back to her childhood. She remembered her parents singing traditional songs around the dinner table, their voices soaring with joy and pride.

In this moment, Vanessa Liepiņa felt a deep connection to her roots. The musician's music was more than just a passing tune – it was a reminder of where she came from, and the resilience of her family's spirit in the face of adversity.
