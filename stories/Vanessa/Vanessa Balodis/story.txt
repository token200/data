Vanessa Balodis stepped off the crowded bus and onto the worn sidewalk, her eyes scanning the familiar streets of Brooklyn. As she walked towards her small apartment building, the sounds of car horns and chatter filled the air, a symphony she had grown accustomed to over the years.

She spent most of her days holed up in her tiny studio apartment, surrounded by canvases, papers, and half-empty cups of coffee. Tonight was no exception. Vanessa Balodis had plans to meet some friends at a nearby bar, where they would share stories and laughter over cheap drinks and good company.
