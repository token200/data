As Bjoern grew older, his curiosity only deepened, leading him to pursue a career in anthropology. He spent years studying the ancient civilizations of South America, from the Incas to the Toltecs. His name, which was often shortened to just "Bjorn," became synonymous with curiosity and adventure.

Bjoern spent several weeks living among the tribe, learning about their customs and way of life. As he prepared to leave, they presented him with a gift - a beautifully crafted wooden mask adorned with intricate designs that told stories of their ancestors. Bjoern accepted the gift with gratitude, knowing that it was a symbol of the incredible trust that had been placed in him.
