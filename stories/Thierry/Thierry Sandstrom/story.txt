Thierry's fingers danced across his sketchbook as he copied the works of Monet and Renoir. His mind was elsewhere, though - on the note he had received from an old acquaintance, inviting him to a secret meeting in Berlin.

He made his way to the appointed hour, slipping into a cramped alleyway near Checkpoint Charlie. A figure emerged from the shadows, dressed in a long coat and fedora. Thierry recognized the man - it was Jacques, his former partner in crime.

"You're back," Jacques said, his voice low and gravelly. "I see you're still good at your job."

Thierry shrugged. "Just passing through, my friend. I'm not in the game anymore."

Thierry's eyes met Jacques', and he knew his cover was blown. But it was too late now - the damage was done.

With a swift smile, Jacques handed Thierry a small package. "This is your ticket out," he said, before turning and disappearing into the night.

Thierry took the package, feeling a thrill of excitement mixed with fear. What lay within? Only time would tell.
