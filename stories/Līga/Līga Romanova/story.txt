But it was her mother, a dancer herself, who nurtured Līga's love for movement and music. Under her guidance, Līga honed her craft, training tirelessly in the prestigious Vaganova Academy.

Līga's breakthrough came when she joined the Bolshoi Ballet as a corps de ballet member. Her hard work and dedication did not go unnoticed, and soon she was cast in leading roles in productions of Swan Lake and Giselle. The critics raved about her technical prowess and emotional depth, but Līga remained humble and focused.

One fateful evening, Līga's life took an unexpected turn. On stage at the Mariinsky Theatre, just moments before a performance of The Firebird, she received news that would shake her world: her mother had passed away after years of battling illness. Līga was devastated, but instead of letting grief consume her, she channelled it into her art.

Her subsequent performances were marked by an intensity and vulnerability that moved audiences to tears. As she danced, Līga poured her heart onto the stage, honoring the sacrifices of her mother and celebrating their shared passion for movement and music.
