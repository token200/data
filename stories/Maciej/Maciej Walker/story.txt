The police had given up, but Maciej Walker knew that sometimes these cases took time to crack.

Just as he was starting to lose hope, his phone rang, shrill and insistent. He picked it up, listening intently as Sarah's voice came on the line.

"Maciej, I have something," she said, her tone urgent. "I just got a call from an old friend of my sister's. She claims to have seen her at a nightclub in Warsaw last week."

Maciej Walker's ears perked up. This was the lead he had been waiting for. He quickly scribbled down some notes and made plans to head to the club.

He slipped inside, scanning the room for any sign of his quarry. It didn't take long - he spotted her, sitting at a table in the corner, nursing a drink. Maciej Walker made his way over, introducing himself with a smile.

To his surprise, she didn't run. Instead, she looked up at him and nodded, as if to say "I knew you'd come."
