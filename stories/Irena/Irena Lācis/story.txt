Her dissertation on modernist architecture in Latvia earned her widespread recognition and set her on a path towards establishing herself as a leading figure in the field.

Years later, Irena Lācis was approached by the city of Riga to design a new cultural center that would serve as a hub for artistic expression and community engagement. Irena threw herself into the project, pouring over historical records and conducting extensive research on Latvia's architectural heritage.
