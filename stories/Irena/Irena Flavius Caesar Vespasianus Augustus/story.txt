Her contributions earned her the title of Augusta – a testament to her exceptional service to the empire.

Irena Flavius Caesar Vespasianus Augustus became an unlikely figure in Roman society, respected for her intellect and dedication to her craft. Despite facing skepticism from some who doubted a woman's place in a male-dominated field, Irena persevered, driven by her passion for understanding the workings of the universe.
