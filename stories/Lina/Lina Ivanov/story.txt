Her big break came when Lina was cast in the lead role of Swan Lake at the Mariinsky Theatre, one of the most prestigious ballet companies in Russia. The production was a huge success, with critics praising Lina's breathtaking portrayal of the White Swan.

Lina's subsequent performances earned her widespread acclaim, and she soon found herself performing to sold-out crowds worldwide. Her unique style, which blended traditional Russian technique with modern flair, captivated audiences and set her apart from other ballerinas.

Despite the demands of her grueling schedule, Lina remained committed to her craft, constantly seeking ways to improve and expand her artistic expression. She collaborated with innovative choreographers and musicians, pushing the boundaries of what was possible in ballet.

Throughout her career, Lina Ivanovna Ivanyuk had received numerous accolades, including the coveted Golden Mask award for Outstanding Achievement in Ballet. Lina's most notable performance came when she danced the title role in Christopher Wheeldon's production of The Nutcracker at New York City Ballet. Her electrifying performance earned her a standing ovation and solidified her position as one of the world's leading ballerinas.
