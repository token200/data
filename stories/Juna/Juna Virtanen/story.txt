As Juna grew older, her interest in botany only deepened. She pursued degrees in both biology and anthropology, allowing her to explore the intersections between human culture and the natural world. Her research took her to the Amazon rainforest, where she spent years studying the medicinal properties of the region's indigenous plants.

Despite her many accomplishments, Juna remained humble and grounded. She continued to travel the world, exploring new landscapes and discovering new species.
