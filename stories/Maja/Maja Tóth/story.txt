Her breakthrough piece, a exposé on corruption within the city's government, earned her widespread recognition and praise from colleagues and readers alike.

As Maja's reputation grew, so did her ambition. She began to work on larger-scale projects, investigating high-profile cases of crime and injustice. Her tenacity and dedication earned her a spot as a staff writer at the prestigious Budapest Times, where she was tasked with uncovering the stories that others deemed too sensitive or too difficult.
