Bakhyt Eglīte was known for his exceptional skill in archery among the people of Kyzylorda Province, Kazakhstan. The young hunter had learned to shoot with precision from a tender age, under the guidance of his wise and experienced mentor, Ulanbek. As Bakhyt grew older, he became increasingly renowned for his incredible marksmanship, earning him a reputation that preceded him wherever he went.

One day, while out hunting in the vast steppes, Bakhyt Eglīte came across a group of wild horses, their coats glistening in the sunlight. The crowd that had gathered to watch him was awestruck by his skill.

As news of Bakhyt Eglīte's prowess spread, he began to receive invitations from nobles and merchants who sought his services as a hunting companion. His talent for archery soon made him an indispensable asset in the hunt, earning him a substantial sum of money and respect.

His dedication to his craft was unwavering, and his passion for hunting was contagious, inspiring others to take up the sport.

Bakhyt Eglīte's exceptional abilities had made him a celebrated figure in Kazakhstan, but he knew that there was always room for improvement. He continued to hone his skills, practicing tirelessly under the watchful eyes of Ulanbek and other experienced hunters. As the years went by, Bakhyt's legend grew, cementing his place as one of the greatest archers in Kazakh history.
