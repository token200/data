It was said that Zoltán Aelius Hadrianus possessed a mind sharper than any Roman senator, capable of unraveling the most complex of problems.

He moved with an air of purpose, his dark hair slicked back to reveal a forehead etched with the lines of countless late nights spent pouring over scrolls. Marcus watched in awe as the master approached him, a faint smile playing on his lips.

Yet, there was something about the master's words that resonated deep within Marcus' soul – a sense of belonging to something greater than himself.

As the stars began to twinkle in the night sky, Zoltán Aelius Hadrianus vanished into the shadows, leaving Marcus to ponder the enormity of his decision.
