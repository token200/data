Emperor Augustus took notice of this rising star and appointed him as one of his most trusted generals. The partnership between Augustus and Drusus Nero would prove instrumental in expanding Rome's dominion.

The name Eric Claudius Drusus Nero Germanicus became synonymous with bravery, intelligence, and unyielding resolve.

Tragedy struck when Augustus passed away, and his adopted son Tiberius succeeded him as emperor. He chose to remain in Germany, overseeing the construction of a mighty wall to keep at bay the invasions of the Barbarian tribes.

Despite the demands of his military duties, Drusus Nero remained devoted to his family. He ensured that his adopted son, Caesar Augustus Germanicus - also known as Caligula and later Tiberius - received the best education available. His legacy lived on through the next generation, inspiring a new wave of Roman leaders who would shape the course of history.
