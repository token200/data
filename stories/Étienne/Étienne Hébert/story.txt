Étienne Hébert was a man of few words, but his piercing green eyes spoke volumes about the depth of his emotions. He was a quiet soul, often lost in thought as he wandered through the streets of Montreal, taking in the sights and sounds of the vibrant city.

As he left the bakery, Étienne couldn't help but think about how he could incorporate more of Madame Dupont's warmth and hospitality into his photographs. He wanted to capture the essence of Montreal, with all its quirks and charm. Étienne Hébert was a man on a mission, determined to tell the stories that needed to be told.
