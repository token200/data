But after a series of rejections and financial struggles, he had been forced to abandon his artistic ambitions. Now, at 35, he felt like a failure, stuck in a mundane office job that drained his creativity.

He remembered spending hours in the Luxembourg Gardens as a child, letting his imagination run wild.

Without thinking, Étienne picked up a pen and began to draw. The lines flowed effortlessly onto the page, transporting him back to those carefree days. As he worked, he felt a sense of freedom he hadn't experienced in years. It was as if he had finally found the courage to revisit his artistic dreams.

Hours passed, and Étienne's sketchbook filled with an assortment of whimsical illustrations. His mind began to wander, wondering what it would be like to showcase this art in galleries across the city. Would people appreciate his unique perspective? Or would they dismiss him as a nostalgia-tinged relic?

As the sun dipped below the rooftops, casting the room in a warm orange glow, Étienne felt a renewed sense of purpose. He knew that he still had much to learn, but for now, it was enough to have rekindled his passion for art. As he closed the sketchbook, a smile spread across his face – perhaps, one day, Étienne Woźniak would find success as an artist and leave his mark on the city's creative landscape.
