Lee Chiatti was known for her fearless approach to photography. As a freelance photographer, she had traveled the world, capturing breathtaking images that told stories of people and places.

Born in Rome, Italy, to an American father and Italian mother, Lee grew up surrounded by the vibrant colors and rich history of the Eternal City. Her parents encouraged her curiosity, taking her on long walks through the Colosseum and the Pantheon, teaching her about the importance of preserving cultural heritage.

After studying photography at the University of Rome, Lee began to build a name for herself in the industry. She worked with local non-profits, documenting the struggles of marginalized communities and shedding light on important social issues. Her work was met with critical acclaim, and soon she was in demand as a photographer for top publications.

Lee's latest project, "The Forgotten Coast," had taken her to the rugged shoreline of Costa Rica. She spent weeks capturing the majestic beauty of the Pacific Ocean, from the towering sea cliffs to the tiny beachside towns that dotted the coastline. Her images told a story of resilience and adaptation, as the locals struggled to preserve their way of life in the face of climate change.
