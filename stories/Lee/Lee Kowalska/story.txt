Her performance earned her critical acclaim and set the stage for a successful acting career.

Lee's subsequent roles on stage and screen solidified her reputation as a talented and versatile performer. However, it was her relationship with poet and playwright Jack Kerouac that would prove to be one of the most significant events of her life. The two had met in 1951, and their romance had been marked by a shared passion for art, literature, and music. Together, they formed a creative partnership that produced some of the most innovative and enduring works of the Beat Generation.

Tragically, their relationship was cut short when Jack died in 1969, leaving Lee heartbroken and lost. Yet, even in the midst of grief, Lee continued to create, pouring her emotions into works like "The Women Who Loved Only Me," a semi-autobiographical novel that chronicled her own experiences as a woman in show business.
