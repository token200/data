As she grew older, Lucyna's passion for music only deepened, and she began to study at the local conservatory. She proved to be a prodigy, quickly mastering the intricacies of traditional Kazakh instruments such as the dombra and the tanpura. Her instructors were impressed by her talent and dedication, and soon Lucyna was performing in front of packed audiences across the region.

Despite her success, Lucyna never forgot her humble beginnings. She continued to live in Kyzyl, where she was beloved by the community for her warmth and generosity. But no matter how far she traveled or how many accolades she received, Lucyna Imankulov never forgot where she came from.
