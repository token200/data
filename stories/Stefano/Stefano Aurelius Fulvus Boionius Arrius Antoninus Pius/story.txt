One day, as Stefano was strolling along the Tiber River, he chanced upon a wise old sage who sensed the young man's innate wisdom. The sage, recognizing the fire in Stefano's eyes, took him under his wing and began to teach him the ways of Stoicism.

Stefano proved a quick learner, devouring the teachings of Zeno and Epictetus with ravenous intensity. As he delved deeper into the mysteries of the universe, he found solace in the timeless wisdom of the ancient philosophers.

Years passed, and Stefano's reputation as a philosopher grew, earning him a place among Rome's most respected thinkers. His full name, Stefano Aurelius Fulvus Boionius Arrius Antoninus Pius, became synonymous with wisdom and intellectual rigor.
