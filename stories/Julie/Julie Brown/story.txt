Julie Brown, the charismatic and quick-witted host of the iconic sketch comedy show "The State," was known for her sharp tongue and infectious energy. Born on November 17, 1969, in Oakland, California, Julie had always been a force to be reckoned with, whether she was taking on the world of politics or skewering the absurdities of everyday life.

Despite the success of "The State," Julie continued to work in comedy, appearing in various TV shows and films throughout her career. She also lent her voice to several animated projects, including the hit show "Futurama."
