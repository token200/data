Mathieu María sat alone at the small café, sipping his coffee and watching the rain drizzle outside. As a freelance writer, he spent most of his days holed up in this very spot, trying to meet deadlines and make ends meet.

Born Mathieu María, he had always been drawn to words, spending hours as a child scribbling stories in the margins of his schoolbooks. His parents, both artists themselves, encouraged his creative pursuits, and he went on to study journalism in college.

But it was writing that truly set him free. He found solace in the written word, losing himself in the worlds he created. Mathieu María's imagination ran wild, from dystopian futures to whimsical fantasy realms.

His latest piece was due soon, and he was struggling to come up with something new. The words stuck in his head refused to budge, no matter how hard he tried.

As the rain intensified outside, Mathieu María felt himself getting lost in the narrative, the characters springing to life on the page. Leila sat across from him, quietly observing the process, a knowing glint in her eye.
