Mathieu Esposito stepped into the bustling streets of Little Italy, his eyes scanning the crowded alleys and quaint shops. As a photographer, he was always on the lookout for unique perspectives and stories to capture. His latest project, documenting the lives of Italian-American families in New York City, had been taking shape over the past few months.

As he turned onto Mott Street, Mathieu spotted an elderly man sitting on a bench, watching the world go by with a mixture of sadness and nostalgia. The man's eyes seemed to hold a thousand stories, and Mathieu felt an inexplicable connection to him.

"Mind if I join you?" Mathieu asked, his camera slung over his shoulder.

The old man looked up, startled, but then smiled weakly. "Not at all, young man. I've got time to spare."

Mathieu sat down beside the man and introduced himself. His name was Sal, a retired butcher who had spent decades working in the family business on Mulberry Street. As they talked, Mathieu learned about Sal's life, from immigrating to America as a child to raising a large family of his own.

Sal spoke with a deep reverence for his heritage, sharing stories of traditional Italian cuisine and old-world customs that were slowly fading away. Mathieu listened intently, taking mental notes and snapping photos of the intricate details on Sal's hands, worn from years of hard work.

As the sun began to set, casting a warm glow over the streets, Sal stood up, his eyes misty with emotion. "You know, Mathieu, I never thought anyone would care about my story. But you...you're like a breath of fresh air."

Mathieu smiled, feeling a sense of gratitude toward this stranger who had shared so much of himself with him. As he packed up his camera, Sal pressed a small package into his hand.

"What's that?" Mathieu asked, curious.

"A little something for your project," Sal replied, his voice low and serious. "Try it when you need to remember where you come from."
