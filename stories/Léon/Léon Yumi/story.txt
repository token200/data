Léon Yumi was known for his striking features and enigmatic smile. Born to a Japanese mother and French father in the city of Paris, Léon's mixed heritage had always made him feel like an outsider. As he grew older, Léon became increasingly drawn to the world of art, where he could express himself freely without being tied down by societal expectations.

Léon Yumi spent most of his days painting and studying the works of famous artists, from Monet to Picasso. His own style was a unique blend of traditional Japanese techniques with modern French flair. Léon's passion for art had taken him on a journey around the world, from the streets of Tokyo to the galleries of New York.
