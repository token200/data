Therese Roche had always been passionate about storytelling, spending hours scribbling in her notebook and dreaming up characters like herself – strong-willed and independent women who refused to be defined by society's expectations. Despite the challenges, Therese persevered, pouring her heart and soul into writing as a way to cope with the stress of everyday life. She began writing short stories for Sophie's bedtime, using the stories to teach her about empathy, kindness, and resilience. As Sophie grew older, she began to appreciate her mother's creative endeavors, even encouraging Therese to submit her work to local literary magazines.

Overjoyed, Therese spent the next few weeks revising and editing, pouring all of her emotions into the pages. When the book was finally published, Therese couldn't help but feel a sense of pride and accomplishment.

The success of her novel not only validated her own writing abilities but also provided a sense of financial security for herself and Sophie. As they sat down to read together, Therese watched as her daughter's eyes widened with excitement, her face lighting up with the same sense of wonder that had once driven Therese Roche's own creative endeavors.
