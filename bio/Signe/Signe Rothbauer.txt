Here's a summary of the fictional character Signe Rothbauer:

Signe is a slender woman with an unassuming presence. She stands at around 5'6" (168 cm) tall, which allows her to easily blend into the crowd while still being noticeable enough to make an impression. Her hair is a rich, dark brown that falls in loose waves down her back, often hidden under a scarf or hat when she's sketching. Her eyes are a deep brown that seem to hold warmth and kindness, with a subtle sparkle of creativity whenever she's focused on art.

Signe is an introspective and creative person who has found solace in the world of art. She's observant and empathetic, always seeking to capture the essence of those around her through her sketches. Despite her reserved nature, Signe has a quiet confidence that draws people to her, making her a beloved fixture at the café where she spends most of her days. Her passion for drawing people is genuine, and she has a natural ability to put others at ease, which makes her an excellent sketcher.

**Interests:**
Signe's interests are deeply rooted in art and observation. She loves nothing more than capturing the intricate details of human expression on paper, often spending hours observing people at cafes like hers. Her sketchbook is always by her side, filled with sketches of friends, strangers, and everything in between. Signe also appreciates literature, music, and poetry, often finding inspiration for her art in these creative pursuits. She's fascinated by the stories behind every face she sees, and her sketchbook serves as a way to record those stories.

**Other Details:**
It's worth noting that Signe has a quiet sense of determination about her. Despite being drawn to more expressive pursuits, she seems to have found a unique balance between creativity and practicality, having established herself in Berlin after moving from rural France. Her calm demeanor and soothing presence at the café make her a favorite among patrons, who seek out her company as much for her art as for her warm listening ear.
