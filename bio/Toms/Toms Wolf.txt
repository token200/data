Wolfe was known for his sharp wit, quick intellect, and biting sarcasm. Wolfe's interests were diverse and far-reaching. He was fascinated by technology, politics, and popular culture, which he often incorporated into his writing. In addition to his work on Ralph Kramden, he wrote extensively on topics such as the counterculture movement of the 1960s, American exceptionalism, and the evolution of language.
