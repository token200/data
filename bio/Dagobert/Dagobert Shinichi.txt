Despite his erratic behavior, Dagobert appears self-assured, as if he's masterfully navigating the fine line between truth and madness.

However, he rebelled against this expectation, opting for the freedom to explore the world on his own terms. His apartment is a reflection of his eclectic tastes, with dusty books and papers scattered about, alongside an assortment of peculiar artifacts.

As Dagobert delves deeper into the mysteries of the ancient tome, it's clear that his fascination with knowledge has become a double-edged sword – driving him to unravel secrets, but also risking his grip on reality.
