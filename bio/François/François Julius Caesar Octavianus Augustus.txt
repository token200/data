While he can be ruthless in pursuit of power, Augustus also has a dry sense of humor and a willingness to listen that serves him well in times of crisis.

He is particularly fond of classical Greek and Roman literature, and often spends hours pouring over the works of Homer, Virgil, and Ovid. In his free time, he also enjoys hunting, horseback riding, and engaging in spirited debates with his advisors. Above all, however, Augustus's greatest passion is his empire - its people, its laws, and its endless possibilities for growth and exploration.
