Here is a summary of the fictional character Titus Briedīte:

He is intelligent, cunning, and charismatic, able to think several steps ahead of those around him. Despite his polished exterior, however, he is haunted by the demons of his past and struggles with feelings of isolation and loneliness.

**Interests:** Titus has a deep fascination with the streets of Riga, Latvia, where he grew up on the margins of society. He loves to explore abandoned buildings and hidden alleys, searching for clues about his past and the secrets that lie beneath the city's surface. As a skilled con artist and master of disguise, Titus is always on the lookout for new challenges and opportunities to test his skills.

**Hobbies:** In his rare moments of downtime, Titus enjoys collecting rare books and documents related to history, psychology, and philosophy. He is also an avid listener of classical music and often finds solace in the works of Chopin and Debussy.

**Philosophy:** Despite his many successes, Titus is haunted by the feeling that he has lost touch with his own soul. He struggles with the idea that his life's work – creating illusions and deceptions for others – may have come at the cost of his own humanity. As a result, he is constantly grappling with the nature of identity and reality, seeking to understand what it truly means to be alive.
