Here is a summary of the fictional character William Aurelius Valerius Diocletianus:

**Physical Attributes:**
- Height: 6 feet 2 inches (188 cm) - towering figure that commands attention
- Eye Color: Piercing blue, like the Mediterranean Sea on a clear day - intense and unnerving
- Hair Color: Dark brown, often styled in a strict, militaristic manner - reflecting his disciplined personality
- Face Shape: Sharp jawline, prominent nose, and angular features - chiseled from the same stone as the Pantheon's facade

**Personality:**
- Diocletianus is a force to be reckoned with, exuding confidence and authority that borders on arrogance.
- He is calculating, weighing every move carefully before making a decision, often leaving those around him uncertain about his intentions.
- Beneath his stern exterior lies a complex personality - hints of introspection and self-doubt peek through the cracks in his armor.

**Interests:**
- Politics and Empire-Building: Diocletianus's greatest passion is forging and maintaining power, using every tool at his disposal to keep Rome on top.
- Military Strategy: As a seasoned commander, he has an intimate understanding of war tactics and is constantly seeking ways to improve the empire's defenses.
- Philosophy and Spirituality: The Emperor grapples with fundamental questions about existence, the nature of the gods, and the balance between power and humanity - leaving room for both awe and trepidation.

**Additional Traits:**
- Diocletianus is an accomplished orator, able to inspire loyalty in his followers while intimidating those who dare oppose him.
- He has a reputation for ruthlessness when dealing with perceived threats to the empire, earning him both admiration and fear from his contemporaries.
- Despite his ironclad exterior, Diocletianus possesses a deep respect for tradition, history, and the accumulated knowledge of Rome - traits he strives to incorporate into his rule.

As the story highlights, beneath Diocletianus's formidable persona lies a multifaceted individual grappling with the weight of responsibility, driven by an unyielding desire to protect and preserve the Roman Empire at all costs.
