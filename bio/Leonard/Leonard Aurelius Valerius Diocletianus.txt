Here's a summary of the fictional character Leonard Aurelius Valerius Diocletianus:

**Physical Attributes:**
- Height: 6'2" (188 cm), indicating his tall stature that commanded attention.
- Eye Color: Piercing blue eyes, symbolizing his intensity and calculating gaze as described in the story.

**Personality:**
Diocletianus is a complex character with a multifaceted personality. Initially, he is depicted as a charismatic leader, sharp-minded, and dedicated to Rome's prosperity. However, as his power grows, so does his ego, leading him down a path of paranoia, ruthlessness, and ambition. His obsession with the will of the gods drives his actions, which eventually consume him, revealing the cracks in his armor.

**Interests:**
- Politics: Diocletianus's primary interest is advancing Rome's power and influence, often at any cost.
- The Will of the Gods: He believes that his actions are guided by a higher power, driving him to launch brutal campaigns against perceived threats.
- Power and Prestige: Diocletianus craves recognition and admiration from both the Roman people and the gods themselves.

Despite his grand ambitions, his fixation on external validation and his obsessive pursuit of divine favor lead him down a path that ultimately proves self-destructive. His downfall serves as a cautionary tale about the dangers of unchecked ambition and the importance of humility in leadership.
