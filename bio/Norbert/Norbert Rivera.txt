Norberto holds a law degree from the University of Puerto Rico and has completed various certifications in public health, business management, and international relations. He is an accomplished writer and has published several essays on social justice, education, and economic development.

Career:
As mentioned earlier, Norberto began his career as a student leader at the University of Puerto Rico, where he organized protests against the island's economic crisis. He went on to become a successful lawyer before entering politics, eventually becoming Mayor of San Juan from 1990 to 2009 and earning multiple accolades for his work in education reform, healthcare, and economic development.

Legacy:
Norberto Rivera continues to be remembered as an influential figure in Puerto Rican politics and social justice. His tireless efforts to improve the lives of Puerto Ricans have left a lasting impact on the island's history and future. As he always said, "Mi vida es servir" ("My life is service"), guiding his actions as a leader and advocating for public health, education, and economic development.
